Confidentiality Policy

All orders are handled under strict confidentiality. Your name, university, DNP programme, specialisation track, clinical site, PICOT topic, and project details are never shared with any third party under any circumstances. Work produced for your order is never resold, reused in another student's order, submitted to academic databases, or published in any form without your explicit written consent.

Access to your order is limited to the assigned writer and the quality reviewer responsible for checking the deliverable before it is sent to you. No other staff member, contractor, or party has access to your order details or the work produced for your order.

We do not request your student ID number, university login credentials, or access to your university portals. We do not submit work on your behalf to any learning management system, committee portal, or institutional repository.

Writer Standards and Vetting Policy

Every writer on our team is required to meet three criteria before being assigned to any order:

Doctoral nursing credential: An earned DNP or equivalent doctoral-level nursing qualification. Writers without a doctoral nursing credential are not assigned to DNP capstone or coursework orders regardless of other qualifications.

AACN 2021 Essentials knowledge: Demonstrated understanding of the ten competency domains of the AACN 2021 Essentials and how they map to DNP programme assessment — including Domain 4 (Scholarship for Nursing Practice), Domain 5 (Quality and Safety), and Domain 7 (Systems-Based Practice), which are most commonly referenced in capstone rubrics and committee review.

Track-specific clinical background: Active or recent clinical practice experience within the specialisation track they are assigned to support. A CRNA-track order is assigned to a writer with anaesthesia practice background. A PMHNP-track order goes to a psychiatric-mental health specialist. Clinical currency matters because DNP capstone topics, outcome instruments, and data sources differ substantially by track.

Writers are not assigned to orders outside their verified track expertise. If your order spans multiple specialisations or requires a clinical context our current team cannot support, we will inform you before accepting the order.

Revision Policy

Every deliverable includes unlimited revisions at no additional charge until the work meets your committee's documented requirements. A revision request must reference specific feedback — either the committee's revision letter, your faculty advisor's written comments, or your programme rubric — so the writer can address each point precisely.

Revision turnaround times are typically 2 to 5 business days for minor revisions (section additions, clarifications, reference updates) and 5 to 10 business days for major revisions (chapter restructuring, new literature synthesis, substantive methodology changes). Rush revision turnaround is available on request for orders with imminent deadlines.

Revision requests submitted within 30 days of the original delivery date are covered under the included revision policy at no charge. Revision requests submitted after 30 days may be subject to a revision fee depending on the scope of changes requested and the time elapsed since delivery. Contact us to discuss any revision request and we will advise before any charges are applied.

Delivery Policy

Agreed delivery deadlines are met. If a delivery will be late due to circumstances within our control (writer unavailability, quality review delay), we notify you at least 48 hours before the agreed deadline and offer either a revised delivery date or, if the delay materially affects your submission timeline, a partial or full refund.

Delivery dates are based on the order details provided at the time of inquiry. If the scope of the order expands after work begins — additional sections requested, a change in the chapter format required by the faculty, or source materials provided late — the delivery date may be adjusted accordingly. Any adjustment is confirmed with you in writing before work on the expanded scope begins.

Deliverables are sent by email or the agreed file-sharing channel as a Word document (.docx) unless you specify a different format requirement from your programme.

Academic Integrity Policy

Our service produces model documents, expert-written reference drafts, and editorial support that students use to understand doctoral-level standards, revise their own work, or submit as a reference document alongside their own writing. This is the same form of expert support provided by dissertation coaches, academic writing centres, subject matter expert consultants, and professional editors.

We do not take online exams, access student portals, submit work directly to learning management systems, or impersonate students in any academic process. Placing an order with our service means you accept responsibility for how the work we produce is used within your academic programme. We do not facilitate or encourage violations of your institution's academic integrity policy.

Data Security Policy

All files shared with us — your partially completed drafts, committee feedback letters, faculty rubrics, data files for analysis, IRB approval letters, and any other documents — are stored securely with access limited to your assigned writer and quality reviewer. Files are not retained beyond the period needed to complete the order and any associated revisions, unless you request that we retain materials to support future orders.

We do not store payment details. Payment is processed through secure third-party payment processors. We do not have access to your full card number, bank account details, or payment credentials at any point.

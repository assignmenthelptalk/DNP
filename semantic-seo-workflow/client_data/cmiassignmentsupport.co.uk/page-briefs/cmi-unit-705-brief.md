## Critical distinction from Level 5 change content
CMI 512 (Level 5) covers Principles of Change Management — Kotter, Lewin, ADKAR applied to a departmental or organisational change at Evaluate depth. CMI 705 Critically Analyses strategic change leadership at organisational scale — full transformations, board accountability, organisational resilience, the leader's role across multi-year change programmes. The scope, command verb, source standard, and analytical depth are all fundamentally different. This distinction must be made explicit in the page content.

**Key frameworks:**
- Kotter's 8-Step Change Model (1996) — at L7: not applied but Critically Analysed — which steps are empirically supported? where does it fail in complex organisations?
- Lewin's Force Field Analysis (1951) — driving vs restraining forces; the unfreezing-changing-refreezing model; L7 critique: oversimplified, assumes equilibrium
- ADKAR model (Prosci) — individual change: Awareness, Desire, Knowledge, Ability, Reinforcement; most practitioner-oriented model; L7 limitation: individual focus inadequate for system-wide change
- McKinsey 7-S Framework — seven interdependent elements (Strategy, Structure, Systems, Shared Values, Style, Staff, Skills); used for diagnosing change readiness and interdependencies
- Complexity theory and change — Stacey (1996, 2001); change in complex adaptive systems is emergent, not planned; challenges all linear change models
- Organisational resilience — Weick and Sutcliffe (2007) high reliability organisations; Hollnagel's Safety-II; adaptive capacity at organisational scale
- Kanter's change leadership roles (1985) — change strategists, change implementers, change recipients; the strategic leader's specific responsibilities at each level
- Psychological safety in change — Edmondson (2018); why change fails when the cultural conditions for learning are absent

**Common failure points:**
1. Applying Kotter as a framework to follow rather than Critically Analysing it as a change model with significant empirical limitations
2. Conflating L5 change management (departmental, operational change) with L7 strategic change (organisational transformation at board level)
3. AC3 (sustaining change, resilience) underdeveloped — often receives one paragraph
4. No complexity theory engagement — the most significant theoretical challenge to all planned change models at Level 7
5. Resistance management described as a people problem rather than examined as a systemic and political phenomenon

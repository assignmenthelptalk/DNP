**Key distinction from Level 5 people management content:** Unit 502/503 cover team-level people development — Tuckman, Belbin, Maslow, Herzberg applied to managing individuals and teams. Unit 702 operates at enterprise level — talent pipelines, workforce capability strategy, organisational performance architecture, and the cultural conditions created by the strategic leader across the whole organisation.

**Key theories:**
- Strategic talent management frameworks — Collings and Mellahi (2009) pivotal positions model; Boudreau and Ramstad (2007) talentship; McKinsey War for Talent research
- Succession planning models — replacement planning vs succession management distinction; 9-box grid (performance vs potential); pipeline development
- Human Capital Theory — Becker (1964); the strategic case for investment in people as organisational capability
- Learning organisation — Senge (1990) five disciplines; Argyris and Schön (1978) single-loop vs double-loop learning
- High-performance work systems (HPWS) — Combs et al. (2006) meta-analysis; the HR practice bundles that produce high performance at organisational level
- Organisational capability — Ulrich and Smallwood (2004); capability as the identity of the organisation in the marketplace
- Psychological safety at scale — Edmondson (2019) The Fearless Organization; conditions for learning and performance across large organisations

**Common failure points:**
1. Applying Level 5 people management frameworks (Tuckman, Maslow) at Level 7 — these are team-level models, not enterprise talent strategy
2. Succession planning described as a process rather than Critically Analysed as a strategic capability
3. AC3 (high-performance culture) treated as a brief conclusion rather than a discrete criterion requiring Evaluate depth
4. No strategic scope — analysis addresses the manager's team rather than the organisation's talent and capability architecture
5. Peer-reviewed sources missing — talent management research requires engagement with SHRM journals, Academy of Management Review, Human Resource Management Journal

**Derived Attributes**:
- The "critical" prefix (Critically Analyse / Critically Evaluate) always requires the student to challenge the framework being used, not just use it
- Evaluate ≠ Analyse: Evaluate requires criteria and a concluding judgement; Analyse requires decomposition and relationship-mapping
- Justify ≠ Explain: Justify requires evidence-backed argument for a chosen position; Explain provides cause/reason for a fact
- Identify/Describe/Explain = Bloom's lower order → pass grade territory at Levels 3–4; insufficient for merit/distinction
- Discuss requires genuine balance — presenting only one side of an argument, even with evidence, fails the verb requirement

**Values per Attribute**:
- "Evaluate": requires naming the criteria used to evaluate → applying the entity to those criteria → reaching a conclusion that names strengths and weaknesses with evidence
- "Critically Evaluate": additionally requires acknowledging what those criteria cannot measure and why alternative frameworks exist — most commonly seen in Level 6 and 7 management reports
- "Discuss": minimum two distinct perspectives with supporting evidence for each; conclusion that synthesises, does not simply repeat a point
- "Justify": evidence must be external (theory, data, research, legislation) — personal opinion without evidence is always insufficient

##### H3: Identify — The Foundation Verb
> **Attribute + Value**:
> - Meaning in CMI context: Name, list, or point out specific items, factors, or examples — no explanation required
> - Common misinterpretation: Students pad Identify answers with explanations, diluting the focus; or they list without being specific enough
> - Compliant response includes: A clear list or statement naming the required items — specificity matters (name the theory, name the legislation, name the management model)
> - Response starter: "Three key factors that affect team performance are: (1) communication clarity, (2) role definition, and (3) performance feedback frequency."
> - Levels most common: Level 3; appears in Level 4 pass criteria
> **Writer note**: At Level 3 pass grade, Identify answers can be list-format. At merit, even Identify answers benefit from specific naming (a specific theory or legislation rather than a generic reference).

##### H3: Describe — Characteristics, Features, and Sequences
> **Attribute + Value**:
> - Meaning in CMI context: Provide the characteristics, features, or sequential steps of something — what it looks like or how it works
> - Common misinterpretation: Students confuse Describe with Explain — Describe does not require reason or cause
> - Compliant response includes: Named features or stages with sufficient detail to demonstrate familiarity — not just a definition
> - Response starter: "Transformational leadership is characterised by: a clear and compelling vision, personalised motivation of individual team members, intellectual stimulation that encourages creative problem-solving, and idealised influence through the leader's own exemplary behaviour."
> - Levels most common: Level 3; occasionally Level 4 pass criteria
> **Writer note**: Describe requires more than a one-sentence dictionary definition. Name the components or stages. A Describe answer about a leadership style should name the specific behaviours, not just say "focuses on motivation."

##### H3: Explain — Cause, Reason, and Process
> **Attribute + Value**:
> - Meaning in CMI context: Give the reason WHY something is the case, or HOW a process works — must go beyond description to cause and effect
> - Common misinterpretation: Students Describe when asked to Explain — they tell you WHAT but not WHY
> - Compliant response includes: A cause-effect relationship, a process with reasons at each step, or a reasoned account of why a factor operates as it does
> - Response starter: "Tuckman's model progresses from Forming to Storming because the initial absence of established roles and norms creates interpersonal conflict as team members test boundaries and negotiate their positions within the group."
> - Levels most common: Level 3 (merit), Level 4 (pass)
> **Writer note**: The test for Explain: can the reader answer "why?" after reading the sentence? If yes, the verb is met. If the sentence only answers "what?", it Describes, not Explains.

##### H3: Discuss — Balanced Argument from Multiple Perspectives
> **Attribute + Value**:
> - Meaning in CMI context: Present a balanced argument — consider multiple perspectives, viewpoints, or positions with supporting evidence for each; reach a reasoned conclusion
> - Common misinterpretation: Students present one perspective thoroughly and the opposing view superficially — this fails the balance requirement; or they use "discuss" as a prompt to simply write everything they know about a topic
> - Compliant response includes: Minimum two distinct perspectives or viewpoints, evidence supporting each, a synthesis or conclusion that takes a reasoned position without dismissing the alternative
> - Response starter: "There are competing perspectives on the effectiveness of performance-related pay. Proponents argue that... [evidence]. Critics contend, however, that... [evidence]. Considering the organisational context of..."
> - Levels most common: Level 3–4
> **Writer note**: A Discuss answer that only presents one side — even with strong evidence — does not meet the verb. Balance is non-negotiable. The student's view must be argued, not simply stated.

##### H3: Analyse — Component Decomposition and Relationship Mapping
> **Attribute + Value**:
> - Meaning in CMI context: Break an entity, concept, or situation into its component parts and examine how those parts relate to each other and to the whole — the verb demands relationships, not just a list of parts
> - Common misinterpretation: Students list components (which is Describe) without explaining the relationships between them; or they use "analysis" to mean "lots of information about X"
> - Compliant response includes: Named components, explanation of each component's function, explanation of how components interact or affect each other, and a statement about what this analysis reveals
> - Response starter: "An analysis of the organisation's communication barriers reveals three interacting factors: (1) hierarchical structure, which limits upward information flow because... (2) geographic distribution, which reduces informal communication and therefore... (3) meeting culture, which compounds barrier (1) by..."
> - Levels most common: Level 4–5; also appears in Level 3 merit criteria
> **Writer note**: Analyse requires the word "because" to appear frequently — if the writer is not explaining WHY a component does what it does, they are Describing, not Analysing.

##### H3: Evaluate — Criteria-Based Judgement
> **Attribute + Value**:
> - Meaning in CMI context: Apply named criteria to assess strengths and weaknesses of something and reach a supported conclusion — requires explicit criteria, not just a list of pros and cons
> - Common misinterpretation: Students list advantages and disadvantages without criteria (this is a comparison, not an evaluation); or they reach a conclusion without naming what they evaluated against
> - Compliant response includes: Named evaluation criteria (e.g., effectiveness, feasibility, sustainability, cost, ethical alignment), application of those criteria to the subject, identification of strengths and limitations against each criterion, a conclusive judgement
> - Response starter: "Evaluating transformational leadership against criteria of team motivation, sustainability of change, and applicability in crisis situations reveals: [criterion 1 — strength with evidence; criterion 2 — limitation with evidence; criterion 3 — contextual strength...]. On balance, transformational leadership is most effective when..."
> - Levels most common: Level 5 (core verb), Level 6; appears in Level 4 distinction criteria
> **Writer note**: The most common error at Level 5. "Evaluate" does not mean "talk thoroughly about." The criteria must be NAMED and the entity must be MEASURED against them. Without named criteria, the response analyses at best.

##### H3: Justify — Evidence-Backed Argument for a Decision or Position
> **Attribute + Value**:
> - Meaning in CMI context: Provide convincing evidence-based reasoning to support a chosen decision, recommendation, or position — opinion alone is insufficient; the argument must be supported by theory, data, legislation, or research
> - Common misinterpretation: Students state WHY they chose something (explaining their preference) without grounding the argument in external evidence — this is personal justification, not academic justification
> - Compliant response includes: A clear position or decision, external evidence that supports it (theory, data, legislation, research findings), acknowledgement and rebuttal of the strongest counter-argument, a reinforced conclusion
> - Response starter: "The recommendation to implement a flexible working policy is justified by three converging evidence sources: (1) Gallup's 2023 State of the Global Workplace report, which found that... (2) the Equality Act 2010, which requires... (3) the organisation's own engagement survey results, which demonstrate..."
> - Levels most common: Level 5–6
> **Writer note**: Justify and Evaluate are paired at Level 5–6 because they often appear together in a unit brief (e.g., "Evaluate and Justify a management approach"). They are not the same: Evaluate assesses multiple options against criteria; Justify defends the chosen option with evidence.

##### H3: Assess — Framework-Based Judgement of Current State
> **Attribute + Value**:
> - Meaning in CMI context: Use a framework or set of criteria to judge the current state, quality, or performance of something — similar to Evaluate but often applied to an existing situation rather than a concept or model
> - Common misinterpretation: Students Describe the current state rather than measuring it against a standard — Assess requires a benchmark or framework
> - Compliant response includes: Named assessment framework (e.g., SWOT, balanced scorecard, competency framework, legislative standard), application of the framework to the specific situation, a judgement about what the assessment reveals
> - Response starter: "Assessing the team's current performance against the CMI Management Standards reveals that while task completion rates meet expectations (KPI data, Q3 2024), the team's collaborative decision-making (Standard 4.2) is inconsistent because..."
> - Levels most common: Level 5–6
> **Writer note**: Assess appears most frequently in units about performance management, operational planning, and risk — where a specific standard or benchmark is available to measure against. If the student cannot name the framework they are assessing against, they have not met the verb.

##### H3: Review — Systematic Examination Against a Standard or Goal
> **Attribute + Value**:
> - Meaning in CMI context: Systematically examine something against a stated purpose, goal, or standard to determine what is working, what is not, and what needs to change — Review implies currency (what is the state NOW) and action-orientation (what should change)
> - Common misinterpretation: Students write a historical narrative ("here is what happened") rather than a current evaluation against a goal
> - Compliant response includes: Statement of what is being reviewed and against what standard/goal; structured assessment of current state; identification of what meets or fails the standard; recommendations for change
> - Response starter: "A review of the organisation's talent development programme against the strategic objective of building internal succession pipelines reveals three areas of misalignment: (1) the programme prioritises..."
> - Levels most common: Level 5–6
> **Writer note**: Review and Evaluate are similar — the key difference is that Review is often applied to an existing practice or programme (something real and current), while Evaluate is applied to a theory, model, or approach (which can be abstract). Review always implies: here is what we have, here is what we need, here is the gap.

##### H3: Develop a Plan — Specific, Actionable, Time-Bound Output
> **Attribute + Value**:
> - Meaning in CMI context: Produce a specific, actionable plan with named objectives, responsible parties, resources required, timelines, and success measures — not a conceptual description of what a plan would include
> - Common misinterpretation: Students write a narrative about how they would approach planning rather than producing a plan — the output must be a plan, not a description of planning
> - Compliant response includes: SMART objectives (Specific, Measurable, Achievable, Relevant, Time-bound), named responsible parties, resource requirements, milestone timeline, success criteria, risk considerations
> - Response starter: Use a structured table format — Objective | Action | Responsible | Timeline | Resource | Success Measure — each row is a SMART objective
> - Levels most common: Level 5–6 (operational planning units); Level 7 (strategic planning units)
> **Writer note**: This is the one verb that explicitly calls for a formatted output. A narrative paragraph attempting to describe a plan does not meet "Develop a Plan." The assessor expects to see a structured deliverable — a plan they could hand to a manager to action.

##### H3: Propose — Reasoned Recommendation with Supporting Rationale
> **Attribute + Value**:
> - Meaning in CMI context: Put forward a specific recommendation with a supported rationale — the proposal must be concrete (specific action, not vague direction) and supported with evidence
> - Common misinterpretation: Students propose at a conceptual level ("the organisation should improve communication") rather than at a specific, actionable level ("the organisation should implement a weekly team briefing structure with a defined agenda template, trialled in Q2")
> - Compliant response includes: A specific, named proposal; the evidence or reasoning that supports it; anticipated benefits; any risks or qualifications
> - Response starter: "This report proposes the implementation of a structured onboarding programme for all new managers, specifically: a 90-day cohort programme comprising four modules aligned to CMI Level 5 unit outcomes, delivered via blended learning..."
> - Levels most common: Level 5–7 (often appears in recommendations sections of management reports)
> **Writer note**: Propose is often encountered in the recommendations section of a management report, even when the unit LO uses a different primary verb. Every recommendation IS a proposal. It must be specific enough to action.

##### H3: Critically Analyse — Analysis Plus Explicit Challenge of Assumptions and Limitations
> **Attribute + Value**:
> - Meaning in CMI context: Perform a full Analysis (decompose components, map relationships) AND explicitly identify the limitations, assumptions, biases, or gaps in that analysis — challenge the frameworks, theories, and sources being used
> - Common misinterpretation: Students Analyse thoroughly and believe the depth of their analysis constitutes critical thinking — it does not; critical thinking requires explicitly naming what the analysis cannot tell you and why
> - Compliant response includes: Full component analysis with relationship mapping; explicit identification of limitations of the framework or evidence used; alternative theories or perspectives that challenge the primary analysis; a conclusion that acknowledges the partial nature of any analytical frame
> - Response starter: "An analysis of the PESTLE factors affecting the organisation's expansion strategy reveals [components, relationships]. However, this analysis carries three key limitations: (1) PESTLE treats political and economic factors as discrete when [relationship limitation]... (2) the model does not account for [gap]... Alternative frameworks, such as Porter's Five Forces, would reveal..."
> - Levels most common: Level 6–7
> **Writer note**: The word "however" and phrases like "a limitation of this framework is" and "an alternative perspective would argue" are structural signals that Critical Analysis is occurring. Without these challenge statements, the response is Analysis, not Critical Analysis.

##### H3: Critically Evaluate — Evaluation Plus Challenge of the Evaluation Criteria Themselves
> **Attribute + Value**:
> - Meaning in CMI context: Perform a full Evaluation (apply criteria, reach a judgement) AND challenge the appropriateness of the criteria themselves — question whether the evaluation framework captures what actually matters
> - Common misinterpretation: Students mistake depth of Evaluation for Critical Evaluation — writing a very thorough evaluation with lots of evidence is not the same as challenging whether the criteria being used are the right criteria
> - Compliant response includes: Named evaluation criteria applied fully; a judgement reached; explicit challenge of whether those criteria are the most appropriate measure; alternative criteria that would produce a different judgement and why; a final position that acknowledges the evaluative limitations
> - Response starter: "Evaluating the leadership programme against criteria of participant satisfaction, behaviour change, and productivity metrics reveals... [judgement]. However, critically, these criteria measure observable outcomes rather than leadership capability development over time. Alternative criteria — such as 360-degree leadership assessment scores at 6-month intervals — would reveal..."
> - Levels most common: Level 6 (required for distinction); Level 7 (standard expectation across most units)
> **Writer note**: The highest cognitive demand in CMI assessment. Very few students reach this level naturally. It requires the student to step outside the analysis and question its own foundations. Assessors at Level 6–7 mark for this explicitly — it is not optional for distinction.

#### H2: Command Verbs and CMI Grades — Pass, Merit, Distinction — Major Query Need: Grade connection
> **Context vector**: How command verb compliance determines grade outcome
> **Main entity + attributes to cover**:
> - Pass: demonstrates the minimum required cognitive depth for the primary verb; may meet Identify/Describe/Explain within a higher-verb unit
> - Merit: applies the primary verb with evidence and theory — meets the verb at the stated cognitive level
> - Distinction: applies the verb at full depth AND demonstrates critical thinking (limitations, alternatives, meta-cognition) — at Level 6–7, this means Critically Evaluate even when the LO says Evaluate
> - The verb provides the floor: a student cannot achieve distinction by meeting Evaluate brilliantly if the LO required Critically Evaluate
> - Conversely, meeting Critically Evaluate when only Evaluate was required earns distinction marks
> **Component**: Reference table — Verb | Pass Requirement | Merit Requirement | Distinction Requirement
> **Predicate to use**: "demonstrates" / "requires" / "achieves" / "earns"
> **First word of first paragraph**: "CMI grade outcomes at all levels are determined by..."
> **Word count target**: 220–280 words

##### H3: What Assessors Look for in Each Grade Band
> **Attribute + Value**:
> - Pass: "The learner CAN [verb] [topic]" — evidence that the verb has been met at a basic level
> - Merit: "The learner demonstrates [verb] at a consistent level with appropriate evidence" — verb met with supporting theory and examples throughout
> - Distinction: "The learner demonstrates [verb] with critical awareness, acknowledging limitations and alternative perspectives" — the "critical" layer applied regardless of whether the LO uses the "Critically" prefix
> **Writer note**: Many students believe distinction requires more content. It does not — it requires higher-order thinking. A 3,000-word Level 5 assignment that critically evaluates the criteria used in its own analysis is more likely to achieve distinction than a 5,000-word assignment that evaluates thoroughly but uncritically.

#### H2: FAQ — CMI Command Verbs
> **Critical rule**: Must not repeat Main Content — these must add information or clarification not given above.

**Questions and Answer Directions**:
1. What is the difference between Evaluate and Critically Evaluate in CMI? → Evaluate requires applying named criteria to assess strengths and weaknesses and reaching a conclusion. Critically Evaluate requires all of that AND questioning whether the criteria themselves are the right measure — acknowledging what they cannot capture, and presenting alternative evaluation frameworks. Critically Evaluate is the standard expectation at Level 7 and required for distinction at Level 6.
2. What does Justify mean in a CMI assignment? → Justify means providing evidence-based reasoning for a decision, recommendation, or position. The evidence must be external — theory, data, legislation, or research findings. Stating your personal reasoning for a choice is explanation, not justification. Your argument must be supported by sources that an assessor can verify.
3. How is Analyse different from Describe? → Describe gives features or characteristics — what something looks like or how it works. Analyse breaks something into components AND explains how those components relate to each other and to the whole. The key signal: Analyse requires the word "because" and relationship language ("which therefore," "this leads to," "as a result"). Describe does not.
4. Do all CMI assignments have a command verb? → Yes — every CMI Learning Outcome contains at least one command verb. In some LOs there are two (e.g., "Evaluate and Justify"). Distinction responses at Level 5 often require Critically Evaluate even when the LO states Evaluate — assessors look for the critical layer in merit-to-distinction transitions.

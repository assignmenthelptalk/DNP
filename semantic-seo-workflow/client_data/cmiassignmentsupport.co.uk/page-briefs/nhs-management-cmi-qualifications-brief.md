**Key distinctions this page must establish:**
1. The NHS Leadership Academy programmes (Mary Seacole, Elizabeth Garrett Anderson) are not CMI qualifications — they are leadership development programmes that CMI accredits in some versions. A student completing Mary Seacole does not have a CMI Level 5 Diploma unless their programme was specifically the CMI-accredited version.
2. Band 7 does not automatically mean Level 5 — some Band 7 NHS roles are entering Level 7 programmes, particularly clinical leaders moving into hybrid clinical/managerial roles.
3. CMI Level 5 focuses on management of a department or team — directly applicable to Band 7 ward managers, department heads, clinical team leaders.
4. CMI Level 7 focuses on organisational strategy and board-level leadership — applicable to Band 8c+, executive directors, Heads of Service.

## FAQ Items (for FAQPage schema)

1. Which CMI level is right for Band 7 NHS managers? → CMI Level 5 Diploma is the standard alignment for Band 7 — covers management and leadership at team/department level, matching the scope of a ward manager or clinical department head. Some Band 7 roles in senior clinical leadership may be directed toward Level 7.
2. Is the Mary Seacole Programme the same as CMI Level 5? → Not automatically. The Mary Seacole Programme is an NHS Leadership Academy programme. Some versions are CMI-accredited, meaning participants can receive CMI Level 5 credit. Confirm with your Trust whether your programme is the CMI-accredited version.
3. Can the NHS fund my CMI qualification? → Yes — NHS Trusts allocate CPD funding for management qualifications, and some ICBs have workforce development grants. Funding is not automatic. Apply early through your line manager and workforce development team. NHS England and the Leadership Academy have funded cohort programmes at both L5 and L7.
4. What CMI units are most relevant for NHS managers? → At Level 5: Unit 501 (management and leadership principles), Unit 502/503 (developing and managing teams), Unit 504 (performance management), Unit 509 (stakeholder relationships). At Level 7: Unit 701 (strategic leadership), Unit 702 (people strategy), Unit 704 (organisational strategy), Unit 705 (leading change).
5. Why do NHS managers struggle with CMI assignments? → Shift patterns and clinical urgency interrupt study time. The transition from clinical to analytical academic writing is challenging. NHS examples are highly relevant but require care around patient confidentiality and data governance. Expert assignment support helps structure NHS-specific content to CMI assessment criteria.
6. Can you help an NHS manager with a CMI assignment? → Yes — our writers have supported NHS managers at Band 6 through 9+ across Level 5 and Level 7 units. We understand the NHS context — ICS structures, NHS workforce challenges, NHS leadership frameworks — and apply CMI assessment criteria at the correct command verb depth.

**Key frameworks — all requiring Critically Analytical engagement:**
- ISO 31000 (2018) — international risk management standard; principles, framework, and process; L7 critique: principles-based rather than prescriptive — implementation varies significantly; does not provide quantitative methods
- COSO ERM Framework (2017) — Committee of Sponsoring Organizations Enterprise Risk Management; integrates risk with strategy and performance; five components (governance and culture, strategy and objective-setting, performance, review and revision, information, communication and reporting)
- Risk appetite and risk tolerance — COSO and ISO distinguish appetite (broad direction) from tolerance (acceptable deviation); strategic leaders set appetite at board level; L7 requires Critically Analysing how risk appetite is operationalised, not merely defined
- Quantitative vs qualitative risk assessment — Monte Carlo simulation, probability-impact matrices; L7 critique: qualitative approaches dominant in practice because of data limitations and cognitive biases in probability estimation
- Black Swan theory — Taleb (2007, 2012) — high-impact, hard-to-predict events outside normal expectation; the limits of probabilistic risk models; strategic implications for resilience over prediction
- Scenario planning — Schoemaker (1995), Shell scenario planning methodology — not prediction but preparation; relevant when probability estimation is impossible or unreliable
- Organisational resilience — Hollnagel's Safety-II (2014); resilience engineering vs traditional risk management; the distinction between predicting failure (risk management) and building adaptive capacity (resilience)
- Risk governance and board accountability — FRC UK Corporate Governance Code (2024); principal risk disclosure requirements; the role of Risk Committee and Audit Committee; three lines of defence model

**Common failure points:**
1. Describing ISO 31000 as a checklist to follow rather than Critically Analysing its limitations (principles-based, no quantitative guidance, implementation-dependent)
2. Risk appetite discussed as a concept but not Critically Analysed — how is it set? who sets it? how does it constrain strategic decision-making?
3. Black Swan theory referenced superficially without engaging with Taleb's critique of probabilistic risk models
4. AC3 (risk culture) reduced to "embed risk awareness in training" — does not engage with the strategic leader's role in shaping culture through behaviour, governance design, and incentive structures
5. No engagement with the tension between risk management (predict and prevent) and resilience (adapt and absorb)

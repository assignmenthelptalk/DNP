##### H3: Management Reports, Essays, and Reflective Accounts — All CMI Formats
> **Attribute + Value**:
> - Management report → structured with executive summary, methodology, analysis section, recommendations — most common format for Level 5 and 6
> - Essay → argumentative or discursive, used in Level 3 and 4 units with command verb "Discuss" or "Analyse"
> - Reflective account → first-person, uses Gibbs' or Kolb's reflective model, most common in Level 5 and 7 CPD units
> - Case study analysis → applied to a real or hypothetical organisation context, required in Level 6–7 strategic units
> **Sequence modeling note**: "Management report" should appear in subject position first, as it's the highest-frequency format. Reflective account second.
> **Writer note**: Describe each format in 2–3 sentences explaining what it is AND when it's required by CMI. This helps students self-identify their format need.

##### H3: Harvard Referencing and Command Verb Alignment Included
> **Attribute + Value**:
> - Harvard referencing → in-text citations + full bibliography, formatted to CMI/academic standard
> - Sources used → ManagementDirect, academic journals, CMI publications, government/industry reports
> - Command verb depth → Analyse (not just describe), Evaluate (pros/cons/evidence), Assess (measure against criteria), Justify (give evidence-backed reasoning)
> **Writer note**: Students frequently fail CMI assignments by describing rather than analysing. Explicitly state that the writing service applies the command verb at the correct cognitive depth — this is a strong differentiator.

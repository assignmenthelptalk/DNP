**Key theories — all requiring Critically Analyse depth (not just evaluation):**
- Transformational leadership (Bass and Avolio, 1990, 1994) — Full Range Leadership Model; but at L7 must examine empirical limitations, cultural dependency, heroic leader critique
- Distributed leadership (Spillane, 2006; Gronn, 2002) — leadership as collective practice distributed across the organisation; counterpoint to heroic/individual leadership models
- Authentic leadership (Avolio and Gardner, 2005; George, 2003) — self-awareness, relational transparency, balanced processing, internalised moral perspective; critique: authenticity is socially constructed, not fixed
- Servant leadership (Greenleaf, 1977; Liden et al., 2008) — leader as steward of followers' development; applied increasingly in NHS and public sector leadership contexts
- Adaptive leadership (Heifetz and Laurie, 1997) — distinguishing technical problems (existing solutions) from adaptive challenges (require new learning and behaviour); critical for strategic leaders navigating complexity
- Strategic leadership vs operational management (Hambrick and Mason, 1984; Boal and Hooijberg, 2001) — upper echelons theory; strategic leaders shape organisational outcomes through their values, experience, and cognitive base
- Leadership and organisational culture — Schein's culture model (assumptions, espoused values, artefacts); how strategic leaders shape and are shaped by culture

**Critical distinction from Level 5:** At L5, these theories are Evaluated. At L7, they are Critically Analysed — meaning: empirical evidence base examined, theoretical limitations engaged, competing models placed in direct dialogue, original strategic position synthesised. A L7 response that Evaluates transformational leadership (as a L5 student would) will not pass.

**Common failure points:**
1. Evaluating rather than Critically Analysing — the most significant and most common referral cause at Level 7
2. Using management textbooks rather than peer-reviewed journals — assessors at L7 expect empirical research, not secondary summaries
3. Addressing leadership style theories without engaging the strategic scope — L7 is about leadership at organisational and sector level, not team management
4. No original synthesis — the response summarises what theorists say rather than producing a defended strategic position
5. Ignoring culture dimension — AC3 requires the relationship between strategic leadership and organisational culture/values/ethics to be addressed as a distinct criterion

## FAQ Items (for FAQPage schema)

1. Does CMI use Harvard referencing? → Yes — Harvard referencing is required on all CMI assignments at Levels 3–7. In-text citations use the author-date format (Author, Year), and a full reference list is included at the end of the document in alphabetical order.
2. How many references does a CMI assignment need? → Level 3: 6–8 sources; Level 4: 8–10; Level 5: 10–12; Level 6: 12–15; Level 7: 15–20. Quality increases with level — Level 7 sources should be primarily peer-reviewed academic journals.
3. How do I cite a government report in CMI Harvard referencing? → Use the issuing body as the author: (NHS England, 2023). In the reference list: NHS England (2023) *NHS Long Term Workforce Plan*. London: NHS England. Available at: [URL] (Accessed: [date]).
4. What is a secondary citation in Harvard referencing? → A secondary citation references a source you found cited in another author's work, rather than reading the original. Format: (Maslow, 1943, cited in Robbins, 2017). Secondary citations should be avoided where possible — locate and read the original source.
5. Can I use websites as sources in CMI assignments? → Reputable websites — government departments, NHS England, ACAS, CMI itself, major professional bodies — are acceptable. Wikipedia, LinkedIn articles, and unsigned commercial websites are not acceptable CMI sources. At Level 7, websites should be supplementary to peer-reviewed journal articles.
6. Are CMI publications acceptable sources in CMI assignments? → Yes — CMI's own research reports and management publications are acceptable, particularly for practical guidance. They are not peer-reviewed academic sources. At Level 7, CMI publications should supplement peer-reviewed journals, not replace them.

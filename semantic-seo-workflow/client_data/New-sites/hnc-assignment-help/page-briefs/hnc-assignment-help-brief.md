#### H2: HNC Pass, Merit, and Distinction Criteria: What Each Grade Requires
> **EAV to cover**: Pass criteria (Pn) — knowledge demonstration and application; Merit criteria (Mn) — analysis, justification, comparison; Distinction criteria (Dn) — synthesis, critical evaluation, independent judgement, professional recommendations
> **Word count**: 320–400 words
> **Specific content**:
> - Pass criteria: student demonstrates knowledge of the subject area and applies it to the given scenario or brief — descriptive and applied
> - Merit criteria: student analyses the topic, justifies choices, compares options, and provides reasoned argument — moves beyond description
> - Distinction criteria: student synthesises knowledge from multiple sources and frameworks, critically evaluates competing perspectives, exercises independent academic judgement, and makes well-reasoned professional recommendations
> - Distinction cannot be achieved by writing more — it requires a qualitatively different kind of academic thinking
> - Many students fail to achieve Distinction because they write at Merit level — they describe and apply theory without evaluating its limitations or comparing competing frameworks
> - Distinction language markers: "critically evaluate," "synthesise," "recommend with justification," "analyse the implications of"
> - Pearson uses specific criterion codes: P1, P2, P3 / M1, M2 / D1, D2 — each must be explicitly addressed in the assignment response

### FAQ SECTION

**Q: What is HNC assignment help and what does it include?**
HNC assignment help provides expert guidance on completing Level 4 Higher National Certificate assignments issued by Pearson BTEC or SQA. The service covers understanding assessment criteria (Pass, Merit, Distinction), structuring written responses to address each criterion, Harvard referencing, and producing work that meets the analytical standard required at Level 4 — not just descriptive summaries of theory.

**Q: Does HNC assignment help cover both Pearson BTEC and SQA qualifications?**
Both Pearson BTEC HNC (England and Wales) and SQA HNC (Scotland) are covered. Pearson BTEC HNC is the most common qualification and uses criterion-referenced P/M/D grading with specific Pn, Mn, and Dn codes per unit. SQA HNC uses a similar structure with different unit credit values. Guidance is tailored to the awarding body relevant to the student's programme.

**Q: How does Level 4 HNC assessment differ from BTEC Level 3?**
Level 4 HNC assessment requires analytical and critical writing that goes beyond the descriptive approach accepted at BTEC Level 3. Distinction criteria at Level 4 demand synthesis across multiple frameworks, critical evaluation of competing perspectives, and evidence-based recommendations — cognitive tasks that were not explicitly required at Level 3. Harvard referencing is introduced at Level 4 and is mandatory, whereas it was not required at Level 3.

**Q: Can I get help with a specific HNC unit, not just general assignment writing?**
Unit-specific guidance is available across all major HNC subjects including Business, Computing, Engineering, and Health and Social Care. Each subject page provides detailed guidance on the most commonly assessed units, including the specific P/M/D criteria, assessment format (report, essay, case study, reflective account), and the academic writing standards required for each unit type.

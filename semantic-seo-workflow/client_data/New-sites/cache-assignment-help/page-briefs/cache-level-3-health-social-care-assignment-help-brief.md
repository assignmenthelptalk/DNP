#### H2: How to Write CACHE Level 3 HSC Portfolio Evidence
> **EAV to cover**: Portfolio evidence entity — attributes: evidence types (reflective account, personal statement, professional discussion record, witness testimony, product evidence), assessment criteria mapping, Harvard referencing (Level 3 requirement), word count guidance, marking criteria
> **Word count**: 340–400 words
> **Specific content**:
> - Evidence types and their function: reflective account (practitioner reflects on own practice using a model — Gibbs 1988 or Kolb 1984); personal statement (factual description of knowledge, understanding, or skills — less analytical than reflection); professional discussion record (assessor questions practitioner verbally — written record submitted); witness testimony (third-party confirmation of competency); product evidence (care plans, risk assessments, communication records — anonymised)
> - Harvard referencing: required at Level 3 — in-text citation format: (Author, Year: page number if direct quote); reference list format: Author Surname, Initial. (Year) *Title of Book*. Place of Publication: Publisher.
> - Assessment criteria mapping: every piece of evidence must be mapped to the specific unit learning outcome and assessment criteria code (e.g., Unit 2, LO3, AC3.1)
> - Common failure points: submitting a description as a reflective account; referencing legislation without showing application; using first person without critical evaluation; failing to map evidence to criteria codes
> - Word count guidance by evidence type: reflective accounts typically 600–1,000 words per unit; personal statements 300–500 words; professional discussion records vary by assessor requirement

#### H2: CACHE HSC Unit Referral and Resubmission: What to Expect and How to Fix It
> **EAV to cover**: Resubmission entity — attributes: referral reasons, feedback interpretation, correction process, resubmission window, IQA role
> **Word count**: 280–340 words
> **Specific content**:
> - Referral (not a fail): NCFE CACHE uses "referral" rather than "fail" — the unit is returned for further work
> - Common referral reasons: assessment criteria not met (evidence too thin); theory not linked to practice; legislation mentioned but not applied; reflection described as narrative without evaluation; Harvard referencing errors at Level 3
> - Resubmission process: candidate receives assessor feedback specifying which AC (Assessment Criteria) is unmet → candidate revises only the referred sections → resubmitted to same assessor → IQA (Internal Quality Assurer) may review
> - Number of resubmission attempts: varies by centre policy — NCFE does not set a universal cap, but most centres allow one or two attempts before requiring unit retake
> - How to read assessor feedback: feedback maps to specific AC codes — if AC3.2 is referred, locate that criteria in the unit specification and rewrite the evidence to meet it directly
> - IQA role: internal quality assurer checks that assessor decisions are consistent and fair — not directly accessible to candidates but can be raised through centre appeals process

### FAQ SECTION

**Q: What units are in the NCFE CACHE Level 3 Diploma in Health and Social Care?**
Core units common to both routes include Communication in Health and Social Care, Equality Diversity and Inclusion, Duty of Care, Person-Centred Approaches, Safeguarding and Protection, and Health and Safety. The Adults route adds Dementia Awareness and Mental Health and Wellbeing units. The Children and Young People route adds Child and Young Person Development and Supporting Children and Young People's Mental Health units.

**Q: Which laws do I need to reference in a CACHE Level 3 HSC assignment?**
Legislation selection depends on the unit — the Care Act 2014 covers adult safeguarding and wellbeing; the Mental Capacity Act 2005 applies to any unit on decision-making and autonomy; the Equality Act 2010 covers equality, diversity, and inclusion units; the Human Rights Act 1998 applies across person-centred care and rights-based units. Each Act must be applied to practice, not merely named.

**Q: What is the difference between a reflective account and a personal statement in CACHE HSC?**
A reflective account requires the candidate to describe a specific professional situation and then evaluate it using a reflective model — typically Gibbs 1988 — linking to theory and legislation and identifying future actions. A personal statement describes the candidate's knowledge or skills without requiring the evaluative structure of a reflective model. Both must be mapped to specific assessment criteria.

**Q: Can I resubmit a CACHE Level 3 HSC unit if it is referred?**
Referral means the unit has been returned for further work on specific assessment criteria that were not sufficiently met — it is not a final failure. Most centres allow at least one resubmission attempt. The assessor's feedback identifies the exact AC codes that require additional evidence or revision. The resubmitted sections are assessed against the same criteria.

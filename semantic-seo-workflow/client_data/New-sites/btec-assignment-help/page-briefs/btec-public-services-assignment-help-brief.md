## New H2 Section Specification

### H2: Leadership, Teamwork, and Self-Management Evidence in BTEC Public Services
> **EAV to cover**: Leadership theory application in public services context, teamwork models with role definitions, self-management evidence formats (reflective logs, PDP, fitness logs), portfolio evidence for service entry
> **Word count**: 300–360 words
> **Specific content**:
> - **Leadership theory in public services context**: transformational leadership (Burns 1978, Bass 1985) focuses on inspiring followers through vision, motivation, and intellectual stimulation — evaluated in public service context by assessing whether a named leader or leadership approach achieves organisational change and motivates staff during periods of reform or resource constraint; situational leadership (Hersey and Blanchard) argues that effective leaders adapt their style to follower readiness — Directing (high task, low relationship) for new recruits; Coaching (high task, high relationship) for developing officers; Supporting (low task, high relationship) for experienced staff; Delegating for high-competence, high-commitment personnel; command leadership (rank structure, chain of command, clear authority) predominates in uniformed services and is evaluated in assignments by assessing its advantages (speed of decision, clarity of role) and limitations (inhibits initiative, inappropriate for complex community policing)
> - **Teamwork models**: Tuckman's five-stage model (Forming, Storming, Norming, Performing, Adjourning) describes how groups develop over time; Merit application involves analysing which stage a described team has reached and why; Distinction requires evaluating the model's limitations (not all teams progress linearly, adjourning stage added retrospectively) and comparing it against Belbin's role-based model (which describes functional complementarity rather than temporal stages); Belbin's nine team roles (Plant: creative problem-solving; Resource Investigator: external networking; Co-ordinator: goal-focused facilitation; Shaper: drive and challenge; Monitor Evaluator: critical analysis; Teamworker: collaborative support; Implementer: practical organisation; Completer Finisher: quality and deadline focus; Specialist: expert knowledge) should be applied to analyse a described team's strengths and vulnerabilities
> - **Self-management evidence formats**: reflective logs from physical training, drill, patrol, or fieldwork activities provide the basis for professional development evidence; personal development plans (PDPs) should be SMART, linked to service entry requirements (fitness standards, competency frameworks), with review dates and progress evidence; physical fitness testing logs should record test dates, results against service-specific standards, and identify training priorities with a progressive plan meeting FITT principles

#### H2: What Is a BTEC Referral and What Causes It
> **EAV to cover**: Referral definition (not partial pass, not conditional), the binary rule, most common causes (by frequency), difference from incomplete submission
> **Word count**: 300–350 words
> **Specific content**:
> - Referral = BTEC term for not meeting all Pass criteria — not partial pass, not conditional pass, not low pass — minimum threshold not reached
> - Binary outcome: one unmet Pass criterion = Referral regardless of how many other criteria the submission satisfies
> - Most common causes in frequency order: 1) failing to address one or more Pass criteria (absent from submission); 2) addressing criterion at wrong cognitive level (less common at Pass level, more common at Merit); 3) submitting incomplete evidence (lab report without results, portfolio without observation records)
> - Incomplete submission vs Referral: some centres have interim submission policies; where they operate, missing evidence can be added before deadline; where they do not, first submission is the submission

---

#### H2: The BTEC Resubmission Process for Internally Assessed Units
> **EAV to cover**: One opportunity guarantee (Pearson regulations), tutor obligations, deadline rules, same academic year requirement
> **Word count**: 300–360 words
> **Specific content**:
> - One resubmission opportunity guaranteed by Pearson's assessment regulations for internally assessed units
> - Centre cannot deny resubmission to a student who submitted original work by the centre's deadline
> - Late submissions may have different entitlements — check centre's late submission policy
> - After Referral confirmed: tutor must issue new or amended Assignment Brief targeting only unmet criteria (regulatory requirement — full redo brief does not comply)
> - Student submits only new evidence addressing unmet criteria; previously awarded criteria remain in portfolio
> - Deadline set by centre; must be within same academic year; missing deadline = resubmission opportunity forfeited (no further attempt without exceptional circumstances)

---

#### H2: Targeted Resubmission Brief: How to Use It Effectively
> **EAV to cover**: Targeted brief mechanics, feedback analysis, criterion-by-criterion planning, annotation technique, word count principle for resubmissions
> **Word count**: 300–360 words
> **Specific content**:
> - Targeted brief identifies specific criteria not met — all preparation and writing effort must focus exclusively on those criteria
> - Do NOT revisit already-awarded criteria — risks inadvertently changing quality of already-awarded evidence
> - Before writing: obtain detailed feedback from first submission (not just which criteria were not awarded, but why each was not awarded)
> - Map feedback to criteria: criterion not addressed at all (write missing content); at wrong cognitive level (rewrite at correct level); partially addressed but incomplete (complete evidence); addressed in wrong task section (move to correct location)
> - Write directly to each criterion using the criterion verb as guide — do not write generally about the topic hoping new content satisfies criteria
> - Annotate draft against criterion codes before submitting — if you cannot annotate, the evidence is not clearly present
> - Resubmission may be considerably shorter than original — this is appropriate; focus on quality and direct relevance

#### H2: Grade Ceiling After Resubmission: What Can and Cannot Be Achieved
> **EAV to cover**: Pearson allows but does not require Merit/Distinction on resubmissions; centre policy variation; typical Pass ceiling; HNC/HND exceptions; strategic implication for progression; how to clarify policy
> **Word count**: 270–320 words
> **Specific content**:
> - Pearson regulations allow, but do not require, centres to award Merit and Distinction on resubmissions where evidence meets those criteria
> - Most centres apply Pass ceiling: Referral + successful resubmission = Pass; Merit and Distinction not available through resubmission at these centres
> - Some centres, particularly at HNC/HND level, allow full grade range on resubmission — confirm in writing before submitting
> - How to clarify: ask tutor or programme coordinator in writing before submitting the resubmission (not after): "Is the maximum grade available on this resubmission a Pass, or can Merit and Distinction criteria also be awarded?"
> - Strategic implication: if unit is capped at Pass after resubmission and a Distinction profile is required for a specific university entry offer, identify what overall qualification grade that unit-level Pass produces and whether it still meets the progression requirement

#### H2: External Assessment Failures: The Resit Route and How to Prepare
> **EAV to cover**: No resubmission for external units, resit in next Pearson window, registration process, exam technique as primary preparation priority
> **Word count**: 300–350 words
> **Specific content**:
> - No resubmission for external units — regulatory and non-negotiable; must resit in next available Pearson assessment window
> - Pearson runs two windows per year for most subjects: January/February and May/June; exact dates managed by centre; registration through examinations officer; registration deadline typically several weeks before exam date
> - Preparing for external assessment resit: majority of external assessment failures result from insufficient preparation for the examination format, not insufficient subject knowledge
> - Priority preparation activities: read command words in past papers and practise matching response type to command word; practise timed responses under examination conditions (without notes); analyse stimulus material before answering questions; review Pearson mark scheme for available past papers
> - Students who treat resit as subject content revision without addressing examination technique typically produce the same quality response as the first attempt

---

#### H2: Authentication and Academic Integrity on BTEC Resubmissions
> **EAV to cover**: Authentication declaration requirement, what counts as legitimate help vs malpractice, Pearson's IV process on resubmissions, consequences of malpractice
> **Word count**: 260–310 words
> **Specific content**:
> - All resubmitted work requires authentication declaration: the work is the student's own, complies with centre's academic integrity policy, was not submitted for another assignment
> - Legitimate help: understanding the criteria, understanding what evidence each criterion requires, understanding what cognitive level (describe/analyse/evaluate) each verb demands, reviewing model answers for what Distinction evidence looks like — all legitimate because they develop the student's ability to produce their own work
> - Malpractice: having work written externally and submitted as the student's own — constitutes academic malpractice with the same consequences as plagiarism; potentially more serious on resubmission because the student has already received feedback indicating what needed to improve
> - Pearson's internal verification samples resubmissions — IV checks that the resubmission brief is targeted (not a full redo brief), that the evidence addresses only the unmet criteria, and that authentication is in order
> - Consequences of malpractice: potential removal of grade for entire unit; in serious cases, removal from qualification programme; the resubmission opportunity is forfeited without benefit of the doubt

### FAQ SECTION

**Q: How many times can I resubmit a BTEC assignment?**
One resubmission on internally assessed units. If the resubmission also receives a Referral, no further attempt is available on that assignment.

**Q: Can I improve from Pass to Merit or Distinction through resubmission?**
No. Resubmission is only available following a Referral. A student who achieved Pass cannot resubmit to upgrade.

**Q: What happens if I miss the resubmission deadline?**
The resubmission opportunity is typically forfeited. Contact your programme coordinator immediately if you are at risk of missing a resubmission deadline — do not wait until after the deadline.

**Q: Do I have to resubmit the entire assignment or just the parts that were referred?**
Only the criteria that were not met. The targeted brief from your tutor specifies exactly which criteria the resubmission must address. Evidence that already met its criteria stays in the portfolio.

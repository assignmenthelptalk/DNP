#### H2: How BTEC Grading Works: Pass, Merit, and Distinction Criteria
> **EAV to cover**: Grading system mechanics — criterion-referenced, cumulative, per-unit; what P/M/D require in practice
> **Word count**: 300–360 words
> **Specific content**:
> - Explain criterion-referenced grading: grade is determined by which criteria a student meets, not by a numerical mark
> - Pass criteria: foundational requirements — task completed, information presented, basic understanding demonstrated
> - Merit criteria: require analysis, explanation of reasons, comparison, structured argument — ALL Pass criteria must also be met
> - Distinction criteria: require evaluation, synthesis, justified recommendations, independent judgement — ALL Pass and Merit criteria must also be met
> - Clarify that missing one Pass criterion while meeting all Distinction criteria still results in a Refer (not even a Pass)
> - Use one concrete example (e.g., a Business unit): P1 = describe; M1 = analyse; D1 = evaluate and justify

#### H2: Internal Assessment vs External Assessment in BTEC
> **EAV to cover**: Internal assessment process (tutor-marked, internally verified, externally moderated by Pearson); external assessment process (Pearson-set tasks or written exams, centre-administered, no resubmission)
> **Word count**: 280–330 words
> **Specific content**:
> - Internal assessment: tutor sets and marks the brief, internal verifier checks marking consistency, Pearson external moderator samples the portfolio
> - External assessment: Pearson writes and marks the task; sat at the centre on set dates; examples — Business Environment written exam at National, some IT and Engineering units
> - Key difference: internal assessment has a resubmission route; external assessment does not
> - Students often do not know which of their units is externally assessed — address this directly with the instruction to check their Assignment Brief header or centre timetable

#### H2: BTEC Referral and Resubmission Rules
> **EAV to cover**: Referral definition, one resubmission opportunity, conditions, tutor responsibility, what happens after a second failure
> **Word count**: 250–300 words
> **Specific content**:
> - Referral = the BTEC term for failing to meet Pass criteria on a unit — equivalent to an ungraded or fail result
> - Students have ONE resubmission opportunity on internally assessed units
> - For the resubmission, tutors must issue a new or amended Assignment Brief addressing only the criteria not yet met
> - If the resubmission is also referred, no further attempt is permitted on that assignment (the student may need to repeat the unit or negotiate with centre)
> - External assessment units: no resubmission; the student must resit in a future assessment window
> - State explicitly: our service helps students understand exactly which criteria they failed and what evidence is needed for a successful resubmission

#### H2: Why Students Struggle with BTEC Assignment Criteria
> **EAV to cover**: Common failure modes — misreading criteria verbs, insufficient evidence depth, missing connections between criteria, poor Harvard referencing (HNC/HND), misunderstanding external assessment format
> **Word count**: 270–320 words
> **Specific content**:
> - Criteria verb confusion: students confuse "describe" (P), "analyse" (M), and "evaluate" (D) — they produce description when evaluation is required
> - Insufficient evidence: completing the task but not demonstrating the specific skill the criterion targets
> - Disconnected criteria: treating each criterion as an isolated task instead of building a coherent argument that satisfies P, M, and D together
> - At HNC/HND: failing to reference academic sources correctly using Harvard referencing — tutors penalise or refer work for referencing errors
> - External assessment preparation: students underestimate the exam-style format of Pearson-set tasks and approach them as coursework
> - Each of these failure modes corresponds to a specific type of support we provide — connect to the service section below

### FAQ SECTION

**Q: What levels does your BTEC assignment help cover?**
BTEC assignment help covers Level 3 National (Extended Certificate, Diploma, Extended Diploma), Level 4 HNC (Higher National Certificate), and Level 5 HND (Higher National Diploma) — across all Pearson-accredited subjects including Business, Health and Social Care, Engineering, IT, Sport, and Construction.

**Q: Can you help with both Pass and Distinction criteria on the same unit?**
Distinction-level guidance includes coverage of all Pass and Merit criteria, because BTEC grading is cumulative — a student cannot achieve Distinction without satisfying every Pass and Merit criterion first. Every model answer we produce maps evidence to all active criteria simultaneously.

**Q: Is BTEC assignment help available for externally assessed units?**
External assessment units — such as the Business Environment written exam at BTEC National — use a different format from internally assessed coursework. We provide structured preparation guidance for Pearson-set tasks, including question deconstruction, command word analysis, and timed response planning.

**Q: What do I need to send to get BTEC assignment help?**
Send your Assignment Brief (the document your tutor issued, showing the unit title, learning outcomes, and P/M/D criteria), your current level and subject, and your submission deadline. No prior work is required — we build the response framework from the criteria directly.

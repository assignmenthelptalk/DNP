## New H2 Section Specification

### H2: Distinction in BTEC H&SC: Evaluation Standards at National, HNC, and HND
> **EAV to cover**: Cross-level comparison of what constitutes sufficient Distinction evidence — cognitive standard is the same (evaluation, synthesis, justified judgement) but what counts as sufficient evidence escalates by level; specific evidence sources by level
> **Word count**: 300–360 words
> **Specific content**:
> - At **BTEC National Level 3**: Distinction requires professional judgement on care approach — evaluating the tensions between care values (autonomy vs protection, duty of care vs individual rights), reaching a justified position using the Mental Capacity Act 2005 principles (presumption of capacity, best interests decision-making), and critically evaluating safeguarding outcomes using serious case review findings (Victoria Climbié 2003, Baby P 2009) to identify systemic failures, not just describe events
> - At **HNC Level 4**: Distinction requires evidence-based evaluation of care models and legislative frameworks using Harvard-referenced academic and professional sources — NICE clinical guidelines, CQC inspection reports, Department of Health policy documents, academic journals (Journal of Advanced Nursing, Health and Social Care in the Community). Evaluation must demonstrate Level 4 academic engagement: not just applying a care model but critically assessing its evidence base and limitations
> - At **HND Level 5**: Distinction requires critical evaluation of health and social care policy against practice outcomes — using strategic management frameworks, identifying the gap between legislative intent and practice implementation, evaluating competing policy priorities (integration vs austerity, person-centred care vs resource constraints), and reaching a justified management recommendation. Peer-reviewed policy research (Health Policy journal, BMJ, The Lancet) provides the Level 5 academic sourcing standard
> - The practical test for each level: "Does the response make a justified evaluative judgement with the evidence base appropriate to this qualification level?"

**Derived Attributes**:
- **Core Legislation**: Children Act 1989 (paramountcy principle, parental responsibility, care and supervision orders); Children Act 2004 (s.47 significant harm duty, Safeguarding Children Partnerships); Working Together to Safeguard Children 2018 (statutory guidance for inter-agency working); Childcare Act 2006 (duty to provide early years provision, EYFS statutory basis); EYFS statutory framework 2021 (seven areas of learning, Characteristics of Effective Learning, welfare requirements including safeguarding obligations, staff-to-child ratios); SEND Code of Practice 2015 (graduated approach: Assess-Plan-Do-Review, Education, Health and Care Plans, Local Offer); Children and Social Work Act 2017 (Child Safeguarding Practice Reviews replacing serious case reviews)
- **Child Development Theories**: Piaget (sensorimotor/preoperational/concrete operational/formal operational), Vygotsky (ZPD and scaffolding), Bowlby (maternal deprivation hypothesis, four attachment types via Ainsworth's Strange Situation: secure/insecure-avoidant/insecure-ambivalent/disorganised), Bronfenbrenner (five systems: microsystem/mesosystem/exosystem/macrosystem/chronosystem), Bandura (social learning), Chomsky (Language Acquisition Device, nativist), Skinner (operant conditioning), Kohlberg (three levels of moral development), Parten (six stages of play)
- **EYFS Seven Areas**: Prime areas — Communication and Language; Physical Development; Personal, Social and Emotional Development (PSED). Specific areas — Literacy; Mathematics; Understanding the World; Expressive Arts and Design. Prime areas given priority 0–2 years
- **EYFS Characteristics of Effective Learning (COEL)**: Playing and Exploring; Active Learning; Creating and Thinking Critically — describe HOW children learn, not what
- **Serious Case Reviews / Child Safeguarding Practice Reviews**: Victoria Climbié (Laming Report 2003 — failures of multi-agency communication, cultural assumptions, inadequate supervision); Baby P/Peter Connelly (Haringey LSCB 2009 — professional deference to authority, multiple missed opportunities); Daniel Pelka (Coventry LSCB 2013 — disguised compliance, professional optimism bias). - **Observation Methods**: narrative (running record), time sampling, event sampling (ABC format), sociogram, checklist; Pass = non-interpretive record; Merit = developmental theory link; Distinction = methodology evaluation, alternative methods comparison, combined evidence synthesis

## New H2 Section Specification

### H2: Legislation, Policy, and Safeguarding in BTEC CPLD: Distinction Evidence Standards
> **EAV to cover**: How the legislative framework connects to CPLD assignment evidence at each grade level; SEND Code of Practice 2015 as Distinction evidence framework; serious case review evaluation as Distinction evidence; what Ofsted report references signal at Distinction level
> **Word count**: 300–360 words
> **Specific content**:
> - **How legislation connects to CPLD evidence**: BTEC CPLD assignments do not assess legislation in isolation — they assess how legislation shapes practice. Pass = name the legislation and describe its requirements (Children Act 2004 places a duty on local authorities to investigate where a child may be at risk of significant harm under s.47). Merit = apply the legislation to the specific scenario, explaining the mechanism (how the s.47 duty in the scenario would trigger a strategy discussion, the multi-agency response, and the CPLD practitioner's role). Distinction = evaluate the legislation's effectiveness in achieving its protective intent, identifying where practice gaps exist between the legislative requirement and actual implementation outcomes
> - **SEND Code of Practice 2015 as Distinction framework**: the Graduated Approach (Assess-Plan-Do-Review cycle) is the statutory framework for supporting children with SEND in early years settings; Distinction evidence applies the Assess-Plan-Do-Review cycle to the identified needs of a specific child, evaluates whether the current provision constitutes the least restrictive appropriate support, and critically assesses the transition from SEND Support to EHCP threshold using the criteria in Part 3 of the Code; referencing Ofsted/CQC SEND inspection framework reports in the evaluation demonstrates the professional evidence engagement Distinction requires
> - **Serious case review evaluation at Distinction level**: a Distinction-level safeguarding response does not merely describe what went wrong in a named case (Pass) or analyse the multi-agency failures that allowed harm to continue (Merit) — it evaluates what systemic changes resulted from the inquiry, whether those changes have been effective, and what residual vulnerabilities remain in the safeguarding system; the sequence Victoria Climbié (Laming 2003) → Children Act 2004 → Baby P (Haringey LSCB 2009) → Munro Review 2011 → Working Together 2013/2015/2018 revisions → Children and Social Work Act 2017 (Safeguarding Children Partnerships) is the legislative reform chain that a Distinction response can use to evaluate whether policy learning has translated into practice improvement
> - **Ofsted reference at Distinction**: citing Ofsted's Annual Report, Ofsted's Early Years inspection handbook, or a specific Ofsted inspection finding in the evaluation of practice quality signals Level 3 academic engagement — this is the evidence source that BTEC CPLD markers recognise as demonstrating professional currency

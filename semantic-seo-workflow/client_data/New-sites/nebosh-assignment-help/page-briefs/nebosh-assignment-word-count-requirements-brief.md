#### H2: FAQ — Word Count Representative Query Extensions

**Questions and Answer Directions**:
1. **Does NEBOSH have a maximum word count?** → Answer: No. NEBOSH does not enforce a maximum word count for OBE assessments. However, examiners are required to mark only relevant content — very long answers with significant padding may cause markers to miss valid points buried in verbose prose. Structured, concise answers are more reliably marked.
2. **Do headings and bullet points count toward the NEBOSH word count?** → Answer: NEBOSH's guidance does not specifically exclude headings or bullet points from word count. In practice, mark allocation is based on content points — headings themselves do not earn marks. Bullet points are acceptable in NEBOSH answers for "identify" and "outline" command word tasks but are insufficient alone for "explain" and "evaluate" responses.
3. **How long is a typical NEBOSH IG1 open book exam?** → Answer: The NEBOSH IG1 assessment is completed in a 24-hour window, but the active writing time for a fully developed response is typically 4–8 hours for most students. A complete IG1 submission answering all four tasks at distinction level is typically 1200–1800 words of substantive content.
4. **Can I write more than the recommended word count in NEBOSH?** → Answer: Yes, and NEBOSH does not penalise additional length. However, marks are awarded per distinct relevant point — additional words that repeat or elaborate existing points without introducing new valid observations do not earn additional marks. Spending extra time adding length takes time away from covering more distinct points on other tasks.
5. **Is the word count different for NEBOSH IGC and NEBOSH NGC?** → Answer: No. The IG1 (IGC) and NG1 (NGC) are both OBE-format units with the same marking approach. The word count benchmarks are equivalent. The key difference is that NG1 answers are expected to reference UK-specific legislation more explicitly, which naturally adds some length to answers covering regulatory requirements.

> **Content**: Brief explanatory section differentiating NEBOSH NGC from IOSH Managing Safely. ### FAQ SECTION

**Q: What UK legislation must I know for the NEBOSH NGC NG1 open book exam?**
The primary UK legislation for the NEBOSH NGC NG1 includes the Health and Safety at Work etc. Act 1974 (general duty of care — Section 2, 3, 7), Management of Health and Safety at Work Regulations 1999 (risk assessment — Regulation 3; competent persons — Regulation 7), RIDDOR 2013 (accident reporting thresholds and timescales), COSHH 2002 (hazardous substances — 8-step assessment process), Manual Handling Operations Regulations 1992, Display Screen Equipment Regulations 1992, CDM Regulations 2015, Working at Height Regulations 2005, and Noise at Work Regulations 2005 (action values: 80 dB(A) lower, 85 dB(A) upper, 87 dB(A) exposure limit). Each piece of legislation must be applied to the specific scenario in the OBE — not cited generically.

**Q: What is the difference between NEBOSH NGC and NEBOSH IGC?**
The NEBOSH NGC (National General Certificate) and NEBOSH IGC (International General Certificate) are structurally identical — both have two units with an Open Book Exam and a practical risk assessment, both use the same 48-hour OBE format and 15-task structure, and both use the same grading thresholds (Distinction 80%+, Credit 65–79%, Pass 45–64%). The sole substantive difference is the legislative framework: NGC references UK domestic legislation (HSWA 1974, MHSWR 1999, RIDDOR 2013, COSHH 2002 and others); IGC references international frameworks (ILO-OSH 2001 and ISO 45001:2018). NGC is designed for UK-based candidates; IGC is designed for international candidates.

**Q: Does the NEBOSH NGC qualify me for IOSH membership?**
NEBOSH NGC achievement supports an application for GradIOSH (Graduate Member of IOSH — the Institution of Occupational Safety and Health). GradIOSH is an IOSH membership grade — not a NEBOSH qualification. Candidates apply directly to IOSH after achieving their NGC, demonstrating the qualification and relevant work experience. For Chartered Member of IOSH (CMIOSH), the NEBOSH Diploma is the standard route — the NGC alone does not lead to Chartered status.

**Q: What does the NEBOSH NG2 practical risk assessment require?**
The NEBOSH NG2 practical risk assessment requires candidates to complete a workplace risk assessment report covering a minimum of 5 identified hazards. For each hazard, the report must describe the hazard source and location, identify who is at risk and how they are harmed, apply a risk matrix (Likelihood 1–5 × Consequence 1–5 to produce a Risk Rating), specify control measures following the hierarchy (Elimination, Substitution, Engineering controls, Administrative controls, PPE), and assign a priority action. The report is assessed on hazard identification breadth, risk matrix accuracy, control measure quality and hierarchy, and priority action logic. Distinction-level reports demonstrate variety across hazard types and highly specific, evidence-based control measures.

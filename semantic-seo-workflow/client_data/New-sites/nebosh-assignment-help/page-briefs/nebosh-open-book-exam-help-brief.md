## EAV Depth Map
**Primary Entity**: NEBOSH Open Book Exam (OBE)
**Main Attributes**: OBE format, command word application, scenario reading methodology, answer structure by command word, grading thresholds, time management, permitted materials
**Derived Attributes**:
- **OBE format**: The NEBOSH Open Book Exam replaced the traditional invigilated exam in 2020. Candidates receive a workplace scenario (typically 2,000–3,000 words describing a real-world health and safety context) and a question paper simultaneously. They have 24 hours (from the September 2023 update — previously 48 hours; confirm current window with the candidate's learning provider) to submit answers via NEBOSH's online platform. The OBE is used for NG1 (NGC), IG1 (IGC), and other NEBOSH unit examinations. It is open-book — candidates may use course materials, textbooks, and notes, but work must be their own.
- **Command words — the core differentiator**: NEBOSH uses specific command words that define exactly what type of answer is required. Using the wrong answer structure for the command word is the most common cause of low marks. The six primary NEBOSH OBE command words are: Identify (list or state — no explanation needed), Outline (brief description of key features — limited depth), Describe (more detail than outline — what it is, how it works, what it looks like), Explain (give reasons why — cause and effect, rationale), Suggest (put forward a reasoned recommendation — drawing on the scenario context), Justify (give reasons to support a recommendation — argue why this is the right choice)
- **Identify**: Requires a list or statement. No explanation needed. Example: "Identify FOUR hazard categories present in the scenario." Answer: list four — chemical, mechanical, electrical, ergonomic. A full explanation for an "Identify" question scores no additional marks but wastes time.
- **Outline**: Brief description of main features. More than a list, less than a full explanation. Example: "Outline the key features of a permit-to-work system." Answer: 4–6 sentences covering the main components.
- **Describe**: Full description — what it is, how it works, what it involves. Example: "Describe the hierarchy of control." Answer: elimination → substitution → engineering → administrative → PPE — with explanation of each level.
- **Explain**: The "why" answer. Example: "Explain why workers may fail to report near misses." Answer: fear of blame, lack of understanding of the reporting system, belief that nothing will change, peer pressure.
- **Suggest**: Apply knowledge to the specific scenario. Example: "Suggest improvements to the manual handling practices described." Answer must draw directly from the scenario details — not give a generic answer about manual handling.
- **Justify**: Support a recommendation with reasoning. Example: "Justify the appointment of a health and safety consultant for the organisation described." Answer: state the recommendation clearly, then give 3–4 reasons drawing from the scenario.
- **Grading thresholds**: Distinction 80%+; Credit 65–79%; Pass 45–64%; Fail below 45%. Distinction requires precise command word application, scenario-specific answers that reference named details from the scenario, and proportionate marks per question.
- **Time management**: Candidates often spend too much time on early questions and rush later ones. Strategy: read all questions first, allocate time proportional to marks (e.g., a 4-mark question should take 4–6 minutes maximum), answer the scenario-specific questions before generic knowledge questions.
- **Permitted materials**: Course textbooks, handouts, personal notes, NEBOSH study guides. Candidates may NOT use answers written by other people or AI-generated text (academic integrity rules apply). Internet use is permitted for fact-checking legislation but copying from websites is prohibited.
- **Scenario reading methodology**: Read the scenario once for general understanding. Read the questions. Re-read the scenario specifically looking for evidence relevant to each question. Highlight or note specific details (named individuals, locations, procedures, hazards) that can be referenced in answers — Distinction answers reference specific scenario details, not just generic health and safety knowledge.

> **Specific content**:
> - Command words define the type and depth of answer required — using the wrong structure is the single most common OBE failure

#### H2: How to Read and Use the Scenario
> **EAV to cover**: Scenario analysis method, referencing scenario specifics, how Distinction candidates integrate scenario details
> **Word count**: 260–300 words
> **Specific content**:
> - Step 1: Read the entire scenario once without looking at the questions — build a general picture of the workplace, the people, the hazards, the management culture
> - Step 2: Read all questions before going back to the scenario — know what you are looking for
> - Step 3: Re-read the scenario with questions in mind — highlight or note: named individuals and roles, specific locations or departments, documented incidents or near-misses, specific procedures (or the absence of them), management behaviours and culture indicators
> - Distinction-level answers use specific scenario details: "The scenario states that operatives in the fabrication area are not wearing PPE despite the PPE policy displayed on the wall — this indicates..." versus "Workers not wearing PPE suggests..."
> - Generic answers that do not reference the scenario score at Pass level at best — Credit and Distinction require demonstrating you have read and understood the specific workplace described
> - Scenario details to always look for: incident descriptions, near-miss reports, named supervisors or managers, training records mentioned, documented procedures or their absence

#### H2: OBE Time Management — How to Use Your Exam Window
> **EAV to cover**: Time allocation strategy, common timing mistakes, how to structure the exam period
> **Word count**: 230–270 words
> **Specific content**:
> - Step 1 (First 60–90 minutes): Read scenario + all questions + plan answers. Write brief notes on which scenario details are relevant to each question. Do NOT start writing full answers yet.
> - Step 2: Answer questions in order — allocate time proportional to marks. A 4-mark question should take roughly 4–8 minutes; a 15-mark question 15–25 minutes.

### FAQ SECTION

**Q: Can I use the internet during the NEBOSH OBE?**
Internet use is permitted for reference purposes — for example, checking legislation details or confirming a regulation number. However, copying text from websites, using AI tools to generate answers, or accessing pre-written model answers is prohibited under NEBOSH's academic integrity policy. Your answers must be your own work and demonstrate your personal understanding of the scenario and health and safety principles.

**Q: How long is the NEBOSH OBE exam window?**
The NEBOSH OBE exam window is currently 24 hours from the point the exam opens. This was reduced from 48 hours in September 2023 — always confirm the current duration with your learning provider before your exam date, as NEBOSH may update this. The 24-hour window does not mean you should spend 24 hours on the exam — most candidates who prepare well complete the OBE in 4–8 hours of focused work.

**Q: What is the difference between Outline and Describe in NEBOSH OBE?**
Outline requires a brief description of key features — the main points without full explanation. Describe requires a fuller account — what something is, how it works, what it looks like in practice. As a rule: Outline answers are typically 3–5 sentences or bullet points per point; Describe answers are typically 1–2 full paragraphs with more specific detail. Using a Describe-level answer for an Outline question is not penalised for having too much — but it wastes time that should be spent on other questions.

**Q: What happens if I fail the NEBOSH OBE?**
A fail (below 45%) results in a referral — candidates must resit the OBE. NEBOSH permits resits. The resit uses a different scenario and different questions — it is not possible to simply amend a previous submission. Understanding exactly which command words you mis-applied and which questions you did not reference the scenario in is essential for a successful resit. Our service reviews referral feedback and provides targeted guidance for resit preparation.

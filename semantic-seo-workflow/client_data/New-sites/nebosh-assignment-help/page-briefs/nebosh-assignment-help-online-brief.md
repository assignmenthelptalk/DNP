### FAQ SECTION

**Q: Which NEBOSH qualifications does this service support?**
This service covers all major NEBOSH qualifications: the NEBOSH International General Certificate (IGC), the NEBOSH National General Certificate (NGC), the NEBOSH National Diploma (DN1, DN2, and DN3), and the Open Book Exam (OBE) format used across multiple units. Specialist certificate support is also available for the NEBOSH Fire Certificate, Environmental Certificate, and Construction Certificate. If you are unsure which qualification type applies to you, include the qualification name in your enquiry form and we will confirm.

**Q: Is this service different from my NEBOSH learning provider?**
A NEBOSH learning provider delivers the taught content of your qualification — lectures, study materials, and formal assessment. This service provides supplementary expert guidance to help you understand what your assignment requires, structure your answers to meet the marking criteria, and address any gaps before submission or at resit. This service works alongside your learning provider and does not replace it.

**Q: How quickly can I get NEBOSH assignment help online?**
Standard turnaround is 24–72 hours from the point of sharing your assignment brief or question. For urgent deadlines — same-day or next-day — specify your deadline in the contact form. We accommodate urgent requests where possible. For OBE scenarios (which open on a specific date), contact us at least 48 hours before your exam window opens to arrange a preparation session.

**Q: Does online NEBOSH help work for referral and resit candidates?**
Referral recovery is one of the most common reasons candidates contact us. If your NEBOSH assignment has been returned with a referral grade, we review the specific assessor feedback, identify which marking criteria you did not meet, and provide targeted guidance for your resubmission. Resit support for the OBE includes reviewing what went wrong in the original attempt and practising command word application with new scenario questions.

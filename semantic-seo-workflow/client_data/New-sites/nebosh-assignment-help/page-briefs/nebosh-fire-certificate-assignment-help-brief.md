#### H2: FAQ — NEBOSH Fire Certificate Representative Query Extensions

**Questions and Answer Directions**:
1. **Is the NEBOSH Fire Certificate the same as the NEBOSH IGC?** → Answer: No. Both qualifications share the same assessment format (OBE + practical risk assessment) and grading bands, but they address different legislative frameworks and hazard domains. The Fire Certificate is governed by the Regulatory Reform (Fire Safety) Order 2005 and focuses specifically on fire hazard identification, means of escape, and fire protection measures. The IGC applies ISO 45001 or HSWA 1974 and covers the full range of occupational hazards.
2. **What is the Responsible Person in NEBOSH Fire assignments?** → Answer: The Responsible Person is a legal concept defined in Article 3 of the Regulatory Reform (Fire Safety) Order 2005. In most workplaces, the employer is the Responsible Person; in shared or multi-occupied premises, it may be the owner or person in control. FC assignments expect students to identify who holds this duty in the given scenario and to explain the specific fire safety obligations that flow from it.
3. **How many hazards do I need to identify in the NEBOSH FC2 fire risk assessment?** → Answer: NEBOSH does not publish a fixed minimum, but the assessment requires a thorough examination of ignition sources, fuel sources, and oxygen supply, plus an assessment of people at risk. A credible FC2 report identifies hazards across all three fire triangle categories and considers all occupant groups. Limiting identification to obvious ignition sources alone produces a thin assessment that will not meet the Credit or Distinction standard.
4. **Does the FC2 fire risk assessment have the same format as the IG2 practical?** → Answer: FC2 follows the same general structure as IG2 — practical on-site or nominated workplace assessment producing a written report with hazard identification, risk evaluation, control recommendations, and priority actions. The content differs: FC2 applies fire risk assessment methodology (five-step RRO 2005 process) rather than IG2's general workplace risk assessment methodology. Grading criteria are equivalent in structure.

#### H2: FAQ — IG1 Model Answer Representative Query Extensions

**Questions and Answer Directions**:
1. **Can I copy a NEBOSH IG1 model answer and submit it?** → Answer: No. Submitting work that is not your own violates NEBOSH's academic integrity policy and constitutes malpractice. Model answers are used as benchmarks to understand the structure, depth, and style of distinction-grade responses — students must apply the same approach to their own specific exam scenario.
2. **How many tasks are in the NEBOSH IG1 open book exam?** → Answer: NEBOSH IG1 typically consists of 4 tasks with a total mark allocation of 100 marks. Task mark allocations vary — typically higher-mark tasks require broader scenario analysis across multiple hazard categories, while lower-mark tasks focus on a specific element or hazard.
3. **Do NEBOSH IG1 answers need a bibliography or references?** → Answer: NEBOSH IG1 does not require a formal bibliography or Harvard referencing system (that applies to Diploma-level assignments). However, naming specific Acts, Regulations, and NEBOSH IGC elements within the answer text is expected and rewarded in marking.
4. **What workplace scenario does the NEBOSH IG1 use?** → Answer: NEBOSH releases a different workplace scenario for each IG1 OBE sitting. The scenario is provided to candidates at the start of the 24-hour window. It typically describes a specific workplace (e.g., a warehouse, construction site, office) with photographs or descriptions of conditions — candidates must apply their NEBOSH IGC learning to that specific context.
5. **Is the NEBOSH IG1 open book exam the same as coursework?** → Answer: No. The IG1 is an invigilated open book examination within a 24-hour window, submitted through the NEBOSH portal. It is not coursework in the traditional sense — it is a time-limited exam that permits the use of reference materials but requires original application to the given scenario.

#### H2: How NVQ Assessment Works — Portfolio Building, Assessor Review, and Performance Criteria
> **EAV to cover**: Portfolio structure; unit/element/PC hierarchy; assessor review process; evidence mapping (cross-referencing); what "completing a unit" means
> **Word count**: 300–350 words
> **Specific content**:
> - An NVQ qualification is divided into units. Each unit is divided into elements. Each element contains performance criteria (PCs) — specific, observable behaviours the candidate must demonstrate
> - Example: "Unit 4, Element 4.1, PCs a, b, c, d" — evidence must explicitly address PCs a, b, c, and d individually; a general statement about the element is not sufficient
> - The portfolio is a structured collection of evidence items, each cross-referenced to the specific unit, element, and PC codes it addresses
> - Assessors — who must hold TAQA, CAVA, or legacy D32/D33/A1/A2 qualifications and be occupationally competent in the subject — review each evidence item against the relevant PCs
> - If all PCs for an element are evidenced, the element is signed off. When all elements in a unit are signed off, the unit is complete. The qualification is awarded when all mandatory (and any optional) units are complete
> - Evidence must meet VACS criteria: Valid (relates to the unit standard), Authentic (the candidate's own work), Current (demonstrates present-day competence), Sufficient (covers all required PCs)

#### H2: NVQ Evidence Types — What Each Is and When It Applies
> **EAV to cover**: All eight evidence types; definition of each; validity hierarchy; when each type is appropriate
> **Word count**: 350–400 words
> **Specific content**:
> - **Direct Observation**: assessor attends the workplace and observes the candidate performing the competence in real time — the most valid evidence type; many awarding bodies require at least one direct observation per unit
> - **Professional Discussion**: a structured, planned conversation between assessor and candidate — assessor asks targeted questions to draw out knowledge and understanding; commonly used for K&U requirements
> - **Written Evidence**: includes personal statements (candidate describes their competence and practice) and reflective accounts (candidate analyses a specific workplace situation); must directly address named PCs
> - **Product Evidence**: actual work outputs produced by the candidate — completed care plans, correspondence, reports, case notes, risk assessments; must be annotated to show which PCs they address
> - **Witness Testimony**: written statement from a colleague, supervisor, or line manager who was present and observed the candidate; must be specific, dated, signed, and linked to named unit elements
> - **Questions and Answers**: written responses to questions set by the assessor, used primarily to evidence knowledge and understanding items
> - **Expert Witness**: a specialist (e.g., a registered nurse or social worker in a health context) who verifies the candidate's competence in a specific technical area; has higher evidential weight than standard witness testimony
> - **Recognition of Prior Learning (RPL)**: evidence from past experience or qualifications that demonstrates current competence without new demonstration — requires mapping to current unit standards

### FAQ SECTION

**Q: Do NVQ assignments involve written exams or academic essays?**
NVQ assignments are portfolio-based and entirely competence-focused — there are no written exams, and they are not academic essays. Each piece of evidence must demonstrate specific workplace behaviours against named performance criteria (PCs) within unit elements, rather than argue a position or reproduce knowledge in an exam format.

**Q: Which awarding bodies do you support for NVQ assignment help?**
All Ofqual-regulated NVQ awarding bodies are supported, including City & Guilds, Pearson (Edexcel), NCFE, CACHE, TQUK, Skillsfirst, and Highfield. The performance criteria structure and evidence requirements vary slightly between awarding bodies, and all guidance provided is specific to the candidate's actual unit specification.

**Q: What happens if my assessor returns my NVQ evidence as insufficient?**
Insufficient evidence means one or more performance criteria within a unit element are not adequately demonstrated by the evidence submitted. The assessor provides written feedback identifying the specific gaps. The candidate must produce additional or revised evidence that directly addresses each named PC gap before the element can be signed off. No formal fail grade is recorded — the unit remains open until all PCs are evidenced.

**Q: Can you help with NVQ portfolio evidence at Level 4 and Level 5?**
Level 4 and Level 5 NVQ portfolio evidence requires a management and strategic perspective — evidence must demonstrate decision-making, impact on organisational outcomes, and leadership context, not just task performance. Help is available for all levels including Level 4 (management) and Level 5 (senior management), with evidence structured to reflect the analytical and strategic depth required at those levels.

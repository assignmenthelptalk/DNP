## Sequencing Logic

**Phase 1 — Core Tier 1 (Evidence Type Foundation Pages + Main Service Hubs)**
Establish the five primary pages — the main service overview, portfolio evidence hub, and the two most universally applicable evidence writing pages (personal statements and reflective accounts). These are the pages every NVQ candidate needs regardless of subject or level, and they must be indexed before the subject-specific pages launch so they can receive internal links from day one. NVQ Health and Social Care is published in Phase 1 because it is the highest-volume NVQ subject — it must begin building ranking signals immediately.

**Phase 2 — Core Tier 2A (Level Pages + Witness Testimony + Referral Recovery)**
NVQ Level 2, NVQ Level 3, NVQ Business Administration, witness testimony guide, and referral and resubmission guide go live in Phase 2. These capture the three highest-converting NVQ audiences: Level 2 and Level 3 candidates (highest volume), candidates with returned evidence (referral guide — the most distressed and highest-converting search), and business administration candidates (second largest subject segment after H&SC).

**Phase 3 — Core Tier 2B (Subject Pages + High-Priority Gap Assets)**
NVQ Management and Leadership, NVQ Childcare, NVQ Customer Service, and the three flagship gap assets (H&SC personal statement examples, portfolio cross-referencing guide, medication administration evidence) are published in Phase 3. Publishing the gap assets after the subject pages are indexed maximises the internal link density they receive at launch.

**Phase 4 — Core Tier 2C (Remaining Gap Topics + Outer Supporting Pages)**
NVQ online learning gap page, City & Guilds specific guidance, NVQ assessment process explainer, and academic integrity page extend the Core Section to remaining audience segments before the September employer-mandated training window.

**Phase 5 — Outer Tier 1 (Qualification Discovery and Comparison)**
What Is an NVQ, NVQ Levels, NVQ vs BTEC, NVQ vs QCF/RQF, and City & Guilds overview pages — published from late July through August targeting prospective candidates and employers researching before September enrolments.

**Phase 6 — Outer Tier 2 and 3 + Remaining Gap Topics**
Apprenticeship context, funding, careers, construction NVQ, competence-based assessment guide, and remaining contextual border pages closing the content network.

## Internal Linking Priority

When publishing each page, apply these linking rules immediately:

- Every subject-specific page (H&SC, Business Admin, Childcare, Management, Customer Service) links to: NVQ Assignment Help (main), NVQ Portfolio Evidence Help, NVQ Personal Statement Writing Help, NVQ Reflective Account Writing Help, and the appropriate level page (Level 2 or Level 3)
- NVQ Portfolio Evidence Help links to: all five subject pages, both level pages, personal statement help, reflective account help, and witness testimony guide — it is the primary internal linking hub
- NVQ Personal Statement Writing Help and NVQ Reflective Account Writing Help both link back to each other and to NVQ Portfolio Evidence Help — these three form the evidence writing cluster
- NVQ Assignment Evidence Referral Guide links to: NVQ Personal Statement Writing Help, NVQ Reflective Account Writing Help, and the subject page most relevant to the returned evidence section
- NVQ Portfolio Cross-Referencing Guide links to: every Core Tier 1 subject page and both level pages — it is the most universally applicable practical tool and receives links from all subject pages
- Gap asset depth pages (H&SC Personal Statements, Medication Administration, Childcare EYFS Examples) link back to: their parent subject page, NVQ Portfolio Evidence Help, and the relevant level page
- Every Outer Tier 1 discovery page links to: NVQ Portfolio Evidence Help and at least one subject-specific or level-specific page (see topical_map.json contextual_bridges for exact anchor texts)
- NVQ Levels Explained links to: NVQ Level 2 Assignment Help and NVQ Level 3 Assignment Help — highest internal linking hub in the outer section

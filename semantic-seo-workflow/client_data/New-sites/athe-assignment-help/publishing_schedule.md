## Sequencing Logic

**Phase 1 — Core Tier 1 (Level Service Pages)**
Establish the five primary money pages — one per ATHE qualification level (Level 3, 4, 5, 6, 7) — plus the main overview. These are the highest-conversion pages in the site and must be indexed and building ranking signals before the grading intelligence and subject-specific depth pages launch. Level 4 and Level 5 are published in the first week because they represent the highest enrolment volumes in the ATHE qualification portfolio.

**Phase 2 — Core Tier 2A (Grading Intelligence + Academic Foundation)**
Grading criteria, Merit and Distinction strategy, referral and resubmission guide, and Harvard referencing pages go live immediately after Phase 1. These are the depth differentiators from thin competitors and capture the highest-converting audiences: referred students, grade-improvement seekers, and Level 4+ students encountering academic referencing for the first time.

**Phase 3 — Core Tier 2B (Subject-Specific Service Pages)**
ATHE Business Management, Healthcare Management, Tourism and Hospitality, and Computing subject pages extend Core Section width and create internal link density around the Level hubs. The Case Study Analysis gap page is elevated into Phase 3 because the case study format dominates Levels 4 and 5 across all subjects.

**Phase 4 — Core Tier 2C (Research Project + High-Priority Gap Topics)**
ATHE Research Project Help (Level 6 dedicated page), ATHE Level 7 Healthcare Management dedicated page, and the Nigerian/West African student gap page — three high-specificity assets published before the September new intake.

**Phase 5 — Outer Tier 1 (Qualification Discovery, Comparison, and Progression)**
ATHE overview, qualification levels guide, comparison pages, and progression route pages capturing prospective students in the July–August research window before September enrolment.

**Phase 6 — Outer Tier 2 and 3 + Remaining Gap Topics**
Academic integrity, assessment process, academic writing, international student page, career outcomes, study centre discovery, and qualification comparisons closing the content network.

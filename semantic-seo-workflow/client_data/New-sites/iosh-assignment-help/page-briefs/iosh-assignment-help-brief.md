#### H2: IOSH Managing Safely Project Sections: What You Must Complete
> **EAV to cover**: Project section names (Hazard Identification, Risk Assessment, Control Measures, Action Plan, Reflective Review), section sequencing, why each section matters for the score
> **Word count**: 300–400 words
> **Specific content**:
> - Section 1 — Hazard Identification: candidates must identify at least 4 workplace hazards (some providers require 6–8), categorised by hazard type (physical, chemical, biological, ergonomic, psychosocial), described precisely
> - Section 2 — Risk Assessment: for each hazard, a risk matrix is completed: Likelihood (1–5: Rare to Almost Certain) × Severity (1–5: Negligible to Catastrophic) = Risk Rating (1–25). Assessed before AND after controls
> - Section 3 — Control Measures: must follow the Hierarchy of Control (Eliminate → Substitute → Engineering Controls → Administrative Controls → PPE). PPE is last resort — recommending it as primary control is a common failure
> - Section 4 — Action Plan: SMART objectives (Specific, Measurable, Achievable, Relevant, Time-bound) for each improvement action identified
> - Section 5 — Reflective Review (provider-dependent): a short reflective statement on what was learned from completing the project
> - Each section contributes to the total score out of 100. Weak performance in Hazard Identification typically undermines all subsequent sections

### FAQ SECTION

**Q: What is the pass mark for the IOSH Managing Safely project?**
The IOSH Managing Safely project is marked out of 100. The pass threshold is approximately 60%. Course providers may describe results as a strong pass, borderline pass, or weak pass — IOSH does not award a formal distinction grade for the project.

**Q: How many hazards do I need to identify in the IOSH Managing Safely project?**
Most IOSH-approved providers require candidates to identify at least 4 workplace hazards, though many specify 6 to 8 hazards for a well-evidenced project. Each hazard must be precisely described, categorised by hazard type (physical, chemical, biological, ergonomic, psychosocial), and carried through the full risk assessment and control measures sections.

**Q: What is the IOSH Managing Safely test format?**
The IOSH Managing Safely test consists of 45 multiple-choice questions completed in 45 minutes. The pass mark is 36 correct answers out of 45 — equivalent to 80%. The test covers all Managing Safely module content including hazard identification, risk assessment, legislation, and incident investigation.

**Q: What legislation should I reference in my IOSH Managing Safely project?**
The key legislation to reference is the Health and Safety at Work Act 1974 (HSWA) and the Management of Health and Safety at Work Regulations 1999 (MHSWR), specifically Regulation 3, which creates the legal duty to conduct suitable and sufficient risk assessments. Employers with five or more employees must record the significant findings of their assessment.

#### H2: How Pearson's Criterion-Referenced Grading Works for HND Level 5
> **EAV to cover**: criterion-referenced vs norm-referenced grading, sequential grade application (Pass first), what the criteria grid in the assignment brief looks like, how assessors apply criteria, internal verification process overview
> **Word count**: 280–360 words
> **Specific content**:
> - Criterion-referenced grading: each student's work is assessed against the published descriptors — there is no marking curve and no comparison between students; if every student in a class meets Distinction criteria, every student achieves Distinction
> - Assignment brief criteria grid: the grid contains three columns (Pass, Merit, Distinction) and a row for each learning outcome; each cell contains a descriptor beginning with a command verb — the command verb determines the cognitive demand at that grade level
> - Sequential application: a student must fully meet all Pass criteria before any Merit criteria apply; a student must fully meet all Merit criteria before any Distinction criteria apply; a single unmet Pass criterion means the maximum possible grade is Referral (fail), regardless of how strong the rest of the work is
> - Internal verification: all HND assignments are internally verified by a second assessor within the college before grades are confirmed; Pearson conducts external quality assurance through Standards Verification, examining sample student work to ensure grading consistency with national standards
> - Implication: the sequential grading rule means students targeting Distinction must also ensure every Pass criterion is fully met — neglecting Pass criteria in favour of writing at distinction level is a common mistake that results in referral

#### H2: HND Pass Criteria at Level 5: Knowledge, Application, and Baseline Competence
> **EAV to cover**: what Pass criteria require — knowledge demonstration, theoretical framework application, task completion, output format compliance; Pass command verbs (describe, identify, state, outline, summarise, produce); what Pass-level work looks like in Business, Computing, and Health and Social Care subjects
> **Word count**: 300–380 words
> **Specific content**:
> - Pass criteria at HND Level 5 require: demonstrated knowledge of the relevant theoretical frameworks, accurate application of those frameworks to the given scenario or organisation, completion of all task requirements, and output in the specified format (report, essay, case study, portfolio)
> - Command verbs at Pass level: "Describe" (provide an account of the features, characteristics, or events — no interpretation required), "Identify" (name the relevant factor, theory, or element — no analysis required), "State" (provide the information directly), "Outline" (give a brief but comprehensive account), "Summarise" (condense key points accurately), "Produce" (create an output to the required specification)
> - What Pass looks like in HND Business: PESTLE analysis that correctly identifies and describes relevant macro-environmental factors affecting a named organisation, with reference to appropriate business frameworks — analysis of significance is not required at Pass level
> - What Pass looks like in HND Computing: a system design document that correctly identifies and describes appropriate hardware and software components for the specified requirements — evaluation of design trade-offs is not required at Pass level
> - What Pass looks like in HND Health and Social Care: a report that correctly identifies and describes the relevant legislation and policy framework affecting a given care scenario — critical evaluation of how the legislation works in practice is not required at Pass level
> - Pass-level work that is detailed does not become Merit-level work — the command verb in the criterion determines the grade, not the length or detail of the response; a 2,000-word description is still a description

#### H2: HND Merit Criteria at Level 5: Analysis, Comparison, and Evidence-Based Reasoning
> **EAV to cover**: what Merit criteria require — analysis of significance, comparison of competing frameworks, evidence-based justification, reasoned conclusions; Merit command verbs (analyse, compare, explain, justify, discuss, assess); what Merit looks like in practice across HND subjects
> **Word count**: 300–380 words
> **Specific content**:
> - Merit criteria at HND Level 5 require: analysis of the significance of the information or frameworks applied (why does this factor matter, not just what it is), comparison of competing frameworks or options (which approach is more appropriate and why), evidence-based justification of conclusions (why this conclusion follows from the evidence), and reasoned conclusions that go beyond description
> - Command verbs at Merit level: "Analyse" (break down the information to identify the key components and their significance — not just describe), "Compare" (identify the similarities and differences between two or more things and assess which is more appropriate), "Explain" (give a reason or account for something, making the causal relationship clear), "Justify" (provide evidence-based reasons for a decision or recommendation), "Discuss" (explore different aspects of an argument, weighing evidence), "Assess" (evaluate the significance or quality of something using criteria)
> - What Merit looks like in HND Business: PESTLE analysis that not only identifies macro-environmental factors but analyses which factors pose the greatest strategic threat or opportunity to the organisation — with reference to evidence (market data, financial performance, industry trends) that justifies the prioritisation
> - What Merit looks like in HND Computing: a system design document that not only identifies appropriate components but compares alternative technical approaches, justifying the chosen design with reference to functional requirements, performance criteria, and cost — not just stating that one option is "better"
> - The move from Pass to Merit is a move from description to analysis — the student must always be answering "why does this matter?" and "how do these factors compare?" rather than "what is this?"

#### H2: HND Distinction Criteria at Level 5: Synthesis, Critical Evaluation, and Independent Judgement
> **EAV to cover**: what Distinction criteria require — synthesis across multiple frameworks into a coherent analytical position, critical evaluation of framework strengths and limitations, independent judgement, professional-quality recommendations; Distinction command verbs (evaluate, critically evaluate, synthesise, critically analyse); what Distinction looks like in practice across HND subjects; degree-equivalent standard
> **Word count**: 360–440 words
> **Specific content**:
> - Distinction criteria at HND Level 5 require three distinct capabilities that go beyond merit-level analysis:
>   - **Synthesis**: combining knowledge from multiple theoretical frameworks and sources into a single coherent analytical position — not applying each framework in a separate section and then concluding, but weaving the insights from multiple frameworks into a unified argument
>   - **Critical evaluation**: assessing the strengths, limitations, and assumptions of the frameworks and evidence used — acknowledging where theoretical models have constraints, where evidence is limited, and where the conclusions drawn are probabilistic rather than certain
>   - **Independent judgement**: producing recommendations and conclusions that demonstrate the student's own evaluative stance, informed by evidence and academic literature — not generic conclusions that could apply to any organisation
> - Command verbs at Distinction level: "Evaluate" (make a judgement about the value or validity of something, using criteria and evidence), "Critically evaluate" (evaluate and also question the assumptions, limitations, and reliability of what is being evaluated), "Synthesise" (combine multiple sources and frameworks into a new, coherent analytical position), "Critically analyse" (analyse and also evaluate the validity of the analytical framework itself)
> - What Distinction looks like in HND Business: a strategic analysis that synthesises PESTLE, Porter's Five Forces, and VRIO frameworks into a single integrated assessment of the organisation's competitive position — then critically evaluates which strategic options are most viable given the organisation's resource capabilities and competitive context — then produces specific, evidence-based strategic recommendations with acknowledgement of the risks and assumptions involved
> - What Distinction looks like in HND Health and Social Care: a report that synthesises legislation, policy evidence, and academic research on care outcomes into a coherent evaluation of how effectively current care management approaches are meeting service user needs — with specific recommendations that draw on best practice evidence and acknowledge where the evidence base is limited or contested
> - Distinction at Level 5 is explicitly mapped to second-year undergraduate academic standard — the quality of argument, evidence engagement, and critical reflection expected is the same as that expected of a second-year university student in the equivalent subject
> - The move from Merit to Distinction is not a matter of writing more or adding more frameworks — it is a qualitative change in how knowledge is used: from applying and comparing (Merit) to synthesising and critically evaluating (Distinction)

#### H2: HND Command Verbs: What Each Verb Requires at Level 5
> **EAV to cover**: full taxonomy of command verbs used in HND Level 5 criteria grids, mapped to grade level and cognitive demand, with specific explanation of what each verb requires the student to produce
> **Word count**: 320–400 words
> **Specific content**:
> - Command verbs are the most reliable signal in the HND assessment criteria grid for identifying which grade level a task is targeting — students must identify the command verb in each criterion before planning their response

> | Command Verb | Grade Level | What It Requires |
> |---|---|---|
> | Describe | Pass | A factual account of the features, characteristics, or properties of something — no interpretation or significance |
> | Identify | Pass | Name or list the relevant element, factor, or theory — no analysis required |
> | Produce | Pass | Create an output (report, plan, design) to the specified requirements |
> | Explain | Merit | Give a clear account of how or why something works — causal or explanatory relationship required |
> | Analyse | Merit | Break down information to identify key components and their significance — answer "what does this mean and why does it matter" |
> | Compare | Merit | Identify similarities and differences between two or more things and assess which is more appropriate for the context |
> | Justify | Merit | Give evidence-based reasons for a decision, recommendation, or conclusion |
> | Evaluate | Distinction | Make a judgement about the value, quality, or validity of something using criteria and evidence |
> | Critically evaluate | Distinction | Evaluate and also question the assumptions, limitations, and reliability of what is being evaluated |
> | Synthesise | Distinction | Combine multiple frameworks, sources, or insights into a coherent analytical position that is more than the sum of its parts |
> | Assess | Context-dependent | Can appear at Merit or Distinction level depending on whether assessment of significance (Merit) or critical judgement (Distinction) is required — read the full descriptor carefully |

> - Students who write at Pass-verb level against a Distinction-verb criterion will not achieve Distinction regardless of how much they write — the cognitive demand of the verb must be met
> - The most common criterion mismatch: writing "analyse" responses (Merit level) against criteria that say "critically evaluate" (Distinction level) — this results in Merit, not Distinction

#### H2: How HND Overall Qualification Grades Are Calculated From Unit Grades
> **EAV to cover**: overall HND qualification grade calculation methodology, credit-weighted points system, how many Distinction-grade units are needed for overall Distinction, how HNC Level 4 grades affect the HND overall grade, UCAS tariff points for HND grades
> **Word count**: 280–360 words
> **Specific content**:
> - HND overall qualification grade is calculated using a credit-weighted points system, not a simple average
> - Each unit at Level 5 carries a credit value (typically 15 or 30 credits); each grade generates a point score per credit: Pass = 1 point per credit; Merit = 2 points per credit; Distinction = 3 points per credit
> - For example: a 30-credit unit at Distinction = 90 points; at Merit = 60 points; at Pass = 30 points
> - Overall qualification grade thresholds (indicative, varies by programme): total points below Merit threshold = Pass; above Merit threshold but below Distinction threshold = Merit; above Distinction threshold = Distinction
> - For students who completed HNC before progressing to HND: Level 4 unit grades are included in the overall HND qualification grade calculation alongside Level 5 units — this means that low grades from the HNC year carry forward and affect the final HND grade
> - Students targeting an overall Distinction typically need to achieve Distinction grades in the majority of their highest-credit units — including the research project, which is typically worth 30 credits
> - UCAS tariff points: the HND qualification generates UCAS points used in some university applications — Pass overall = 120 points, Merit overall = 240 points, Distinction overall = 360 points (for the full HND qualification on the standard tariff table)
> - Practical implication: every unit grade contributes to the overall qualification grade; a referral that is resubmitted and awarded only Pass will lose the Merit or Distinction points that the student needed — managing overall grade trajectory requires tracking unit grades from the start of the programme

### FAQ SECTION

**Q: What is the difference between Merit and Distinction in HND Level 5?**
Merit at HND Level 5 requires analysis — breaking down information to identify significance, comparing competing frameworks, and justifying conclusions with evidence. Distinction requires synthesis and critical evaluation — combining insights from multiple frameworks into a single coherent analytical position, critically evaluating the strengths and limitations of the frameworks used, and producing recommendations that demonstrate independent professional judgement. The move from Merit to Distinction is a qualitative change in how knowledge is used, not a matter of writing more or adding more frameworks to a Merit-level response.

**Q: Must I meet all Pass criteria before Merit criteria count?**
Yes. Pearson's HND grading system is sequential and criterion-referenced. A student must fully meet every Pass criterion in the assessment before any Merit criteria apply. A student must fully meet every Merit criterion before Distinction criteria apply. A single unmet Pass criterion caps the maximum grade at Referral regardless of how strong the rest of the work is. This means students targeting Distinction must begin by ensuring all Pass criteria are fully addressed — neglecting Pass criteria while writing at distinction level is the most common cause of unexpected referrals in HND Level 5.

**Q: What do command verbs like "critically evaluate" mean in HND assignments?**
"Critically evaluate" is a Distinction-level command verb at HND Level 5. It requires: (1) evaluation — making a judgement about the value, quality, or validity of something using specified criteria and evidence; and (2) critical analysis of the evaluation itself — questioning the assumptions, limitations, and reliability of the frameworks and evidence being used. A student who writes a detailed assessment of a topic without questioning the assumptions or limitations of the analytical frameworks they used has written an evaluation (Merit level), not a critical evaluation (Distinction level).

**Q: How is the overall HND qualification grade calculated?**
The overall HND qualification grade is calculated using a credit-weighted points system: Pass = 1 point per credit, Merit = 2 points per credit, Distinction = 3 points per credit. Each unit's point contribution is calculated by multiplying its credit value by the grade points, and the total is compared against the programme's grade threshold table. For students who completed HNC before progressing to HND, Level 4 unit grades are included in the total calculation alongside Level 5 units. This means that poor performance in Level 4 HNC units carries forward and can reduce the final HND overall grade even with strong Level 5 performance.

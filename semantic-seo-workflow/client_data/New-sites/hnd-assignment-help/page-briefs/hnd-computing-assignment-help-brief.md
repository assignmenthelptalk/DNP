### FAQ SECTION

**Q: What is the difference between HNC Computing and HND Computing assignment standards?**
HNC Computing Level 4 requires analytical writing — justifying design decisions, comparing alternatives, and writing technical evaluation reports. HND Computing Level 5 requires degree-equivalent critical evaluation — assessing architectural choices against enterprise standards (TOGAF, zero-trust, SAFe), evaluating methodology trade-offs with acknowledgement of limitations, and producing professional-quality recommendations that demonstrate independent technical judgement. The frameworks and protocols are more advanced at Level 5 (OSPF, BGP, SDN, enterprise architecture), and the analytical depth required for Distinction maps to second-year university Computing standards.

**Q: Do I need to use IEEE referencing or Harvard referencing for HND Computing?**
IEEE referencing is the standard in academic Computing publishing and is used in many HND Computing programmes — numbered citations [1] in text, with a numbered reference list ordered by first citation. Some HND Computing programmes use Harvard referencing instead. Students should confirm which system their institution requires and follow it consistently. Academic Computing sources — IEEE Xplore, ACM Digital Library, peer-reviewed Computing journals — are expected for Distinction-level work; Stack Overflow, vendor documentation, and general tech blogs are not sufficient as primary academic references.

**Q: Why does critical evaluation of design decisions matter more than technical implementation at HND Level 5?**
At Level 5, Pass criteria are met by implementing a technically correct solution. Merit and Distinction criteria require critical evaluation of the solution — assessing whether the architectural choices are appropriate for the given context, evaluating the trade-offs of the approach against alternatives, and producing professional-quality recommendations. This reflects the degree-equivalent standard: second-year university Computing students are expected to critically evaluate technical decisions, not just implement them. An enterprise architect, systems analyst, or senior developer is expected to justify their choices — the HND Level 5 assessment models that professional standard.

**Q: What types of Computing research are appropriate for the HND Professional Practice research project?**
Appropriate research approaches for the HND Computing research project include: experimental research (implementing and benchmarking alternative technical solutions), case study research (evaluating the implementation of a Computing system in an organisational context), survey research (gathering practitioner data on technology adoption or professional practice), and systematic literature review (synthesising existing research on a Computing topic). The choice of methodology must be justified with reference to research methods principles. Academic Computing sources from IEEE Xplore and ACM Digital Library are the preferred reference base for the literature review component.

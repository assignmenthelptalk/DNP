**Questions and Answer Directions**:

1. **How many studies do I need to include in an OT literature review assignment?** → Answer direction: This depends on the review type and qualification level. A BSc narrative review at Level 5 typically requires a minimum of 5–8 peer-reviewed studies (published within the last 10 years). A systematic review at Level 6 typically requires a minimum of 5–12 studies meeting the inclusion criteria (the number is determined by the search process, not pre-set). An MSc systematic review at Level 7 should include all studies meeting the PICO inclusion criteria regardless of how many that produces — exhaustive searching is the defining feature of a systematic review. Quality matters more than quantity; 6 well-appraised high-quality studies produce a stronger review than 20 studies included uncritically.

2. **What is the difference between a systematic review and a literature review in OT?** → Answer direction: A systematic review is a specific, highly rigorous type of literature review characterised by: a pre-specified PICO research question, a documented exhaustive search across multiple databases, pre-defined inclusion/exclusion criteria, quality appraisal of every included study using CASP or PEDro, PRISMA flow diagram documenting the search process, and a formal synthesis of appraised findings. A narrative literature review is less rigorous: it may not require exhaustive searching, may include studies selectively, and typically produces a thematic narrative argument rather than a formal synthesis. Most BSc Level 5 assignments are narrative reviews; BSc Level 6 and MSc Level 7 assignments are increasingly systematic or scoping in design.

3. **Can I use OTseeker alone for my OT literature review or do I need other databases?** → Answer direction: OTseeker is a valuable starting point for OT literature reviews because it is specifically designed for OT evidence and pre-appraises included studies. However, OTseeker alone is insufficient for a systematic review at Level 6 or above — the assignment requires searches across a minimum of 3 databases to demonstrate exhaustive searching. Recommended combinations: OTseeker + CINAHL + Cochrane Library for most OT intervention topics; OTseeker + CINAHL + PubMed/MEDLINE for broader rehabilitation topics; AMED can be added as a fourth source for allied health evidence. The PRISMA flow diagram must document all databases searched and the number of records retrieved from each.

4. **What is a CASP appraisal and how do I apply it to an OT study?** → Answer direction: CASP (Critical Appraisal Skills Programme) is a set of checklists used to evaluate the methodological quality of research studies. The CASP RCT checklist has 11 items answered Yes/No/Can't tell, grouped into validity (Section A, 3 items), results (Section B, 5 items), and applicability (Section C, 3 items). For an OT literature review, each included study is appraised using the relevant CASP checklist (RCT checklist for RCTs, qualitative checklist for qualitative studies). The scores are documented in a data extraction table and summarised in the synthesis. The appraisal must explain what any "No" answers mean for the study's findings — a No to allocation concealment (CASP item 2) introduces selection bias that may inflate the intervention effect.

5. **Does my OT literature review need to include a PRISMA flow diagram?** → Answer direction: A PRISMA flow diagram is required for systematic reviews at BSc Level 6 and MSc Level 7, and is increasingly expected at Level 5 for literature reviews that follow a structured search process. PRISMA 2020 (Page et al., 2021) is the current version and includes four stages: Identification (records identified from databases and other sources), Screening (records screened, duplicates removed), Eligibility (full texts assessed, reasons for exclusion recorded), and Included (studies meeting all inclusion criteria). For a narrative review at BSc Level 5, a search log (table documenting databases searched, search strings used, and records retrieved) may be accepted in place of a formal PRISMA diagram — check your assignment brief.

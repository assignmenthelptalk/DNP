#### H2: FAQ — Anatomy and Physiology Questions from OT Students
> **Critical rule**: Questions must NOT repeat Main Content. New representative query extensions for passage-level indexing.

**Questions and Answer Directions**:
1. **What is the difference between UMN and LMN signs in OT assignments?** → Answer direction: Upper motor neurone (UMN) signs occur with damage above the anterior horn cell — spasticity, hypertonia, hyperreflexia, Babinski sign, clonus. Lower motor neurone (LMN) signs occur with damage at or below the anterior horn cell — flaccidity, hypotonia, hyporeflexia, wasting, fasciculations. OT students need this distinction for stroke (UMN) vs peripheral nerve injury (LMN) assignment contexts.
2. **How do I cite developmental milestones in an OT assignment?** → Answer direction: Primary sources include Sheridan (2008) From Birth to Five Years; Gesell Institute developmental norms; MABC-2 and Sensory Profile 2 normative data. Cite specific developmental reference texts rather than relying on non-peer-reviewed websites. Provide example reference format.
3. **What is Zone II of flexor tendon injury and why does it matter for OT?** → Answer direction: Zone II (between A1 pulley and FDS insertion) is called "no man's land" because of the high risk of post-surgical adhesion formation. OT post-operative management in Zone II injuries typically follows an early controlled mobilisation (ECM) protocol within 48–72 hours of repair surgery. The zone determines the OT rehabilitation protocol.
4. **How does dopamine relate to MOHO volition in OT assignments?** → Answer direction: MOHO's volition construct (personal causation, values, interests) describes the motivational system that drives occupational choices. Neurologically, the mesolimbic dopaminergic pathway underpins reward and motivation. Students writing MOHO-based mental health OT assignments can acknowledge this neurobiological basis for volition without reducing volition to a purely pharmacological concept.
5. **Which brain area is responsible for neglect after stroke, and why is it important for OT?** → Answer direction: Unilateral spatial neglect (USN) most commonly results from right hemisphere parietal lobe damage (particularly the right inferior parietal cortex). For OT, neglect is assessed using specific assessments (Star Cancellation Test, Line Bisection Test, Behavioural Inattention Test) and affects ADL performance, safety, and rehabilitation potential — directly relevant to stroke OT case studies.

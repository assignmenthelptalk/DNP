#### H2: Frequently Asked Questions About OTPF-4 Assignments — *Supplementary Content*

**Questions and Answer Directions**:

1. **What is the difference between performance skills and client factors in OTPF-4?**
   → Answer direction: Performance skills are OBSERVABLE, transient actions performed during occupation — motor skills (gripping, walking), process skills (sequencing, organising), and social interaction skills (questioning, regulating). Client factors are the UNDERLYING capacities that make those skills possible — body functions (mental, sensory, neuromusculoskeletal) and body structures (anatomical structures). The key distinction: you OBSERVE performance skills during activity; you ASSESS client factors through standardised measurement. In an activity analysis, identify which performance skills the activity requires; in a case study, identify which client factors are impaired and how they affect the performance skills needed for the target occupation. Confusing these two is the most common error in OTPF-4 assignments and consistently costs marks.

2. **What is the difference between purposeful activity and occupation-based activity in OTPF-4?**
   → Answer direction: Both are sub-types of "Occupations and Activities" interventions. Purposeful activity is a goal-directed activity that leads toward an occupation but is not performed in its natural context — for example, practising grip and release exercises using therapy putty to prepare for tool use at work. Occupation-based activity is the ACTUAL occupation performed in its natural or simulated natural context — for example, performing woodworking in the OT workshop, or cooking a meal in a therapy kitchen. Occupation-based activity is considered the highest level of therapeutic intervention in the OTPF hierarchy because it most directly transfers to real life. In case study assignments, justify your intervention selection by noting where it falls on this continuum.

3. **What is an occupational profile and how do I write one in an assignment?**
   → Answer direction: The occupational profile is the subjective component of the OTPF-4 evaluation process. It captures who the client is, their history of occupational engagement, their current concerns and priorities, and the contexts in which they live and work. In an assignment, the occupational profile is written in the evaluation section before any standardised assessment data. It should include: presenting concerns in the client's own words; relevant occupational history; values and roles that are important to the client; contexts and environments relevant to occupational performance. It is NOT a demographics summary — it is a narrative description of the client as an occupational being. The occupational profile informs everything that follows — assessment selection, goal setting, and intervention planning.

4. **Does OTPF-4 apply to OTA (Occupational Therapy Assistant) assignments?**
   → Answer direction: Yes — OTPF-4 is the foundational framework for both OT and OTA programmes in the USA. OTA students should understand all domain components and intervention types; however, OTA scope of practice focuses on intervention implementation (action points 5–6 in supervised practice) rather than evaluation and intervention planning (which require OT supervision). In OTA assignments, OTPF-4 is used to document intervention rationale, identify occupation categories, and describe performance skills addressed — but evaluation and goal-setting sections will reference OT supervisor roles per AOTA scope of practice guidelines.

5. **What changed between OTPF-3 and OTPF-4?**
   → Answer direction: OTPF-4 (2020) introduced several updates from OTPF-3 (2014): (1) Intervention types were reorganised — "Occupations and Activities" replaced the prior grouping and now explicitly names preparatory methods, purposeful activity, and occupation-based activity as sub-types; (2) Self-advocacy was added as a distinct intervention type; (3) Group intervention was formalised as a distinct type; (4) The domain section was updated to better reflect current OT science and included more specific sub-items for occupations; (5) Virtual context was expanded in the context/environment section (reflecting telehealth and digital participation). In assignments, always cite OTPF-4 (AOTA, 2020) — not the 2014 edition — unless your assignment specifically asks you to compare editions.

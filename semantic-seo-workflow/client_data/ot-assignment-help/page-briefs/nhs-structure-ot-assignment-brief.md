#### H2: FAQ — NHS Structure and OT Questions from Students
> **Critical rule**: New representative query variants only.

**Questions and Answer Directions**:
1. **What is an Integrated Care Board and how does it relate to OT?** → Answer direction: ICBs (Integrated Care Boards) replaced Clinical Commissioning Groups (CCGs) under the Health and Care Act 2022. They are statutory NHS bodies responsible for commissioning most NHS services within a geographic area. OT services are commissioned by ICBs as part of community, acute, and mental health trust contracts. Students referencing the NHS structure in assignments should use "ICB" not "CCG" for current accuracy.
2. **What is the Additional Roles Reimbursement Scheme (ARRS) for OTs?** → Answer direction: ARRS is an NHS England funding scheme that reimburses Primary Care Networks for employing specified roles, including OTs. PCN OTs typically provide first-contact musculoskeletal OT, mental health OT support, and complex care coordination. This is a growing employment route for newly qualified and experienced OTs seeking primary care roles.
3. **What is the difference between NHS OT and local authority OT?** → Answer direction: NHS OT provides clinically-focused assessment and intervention; funded through NHS trust budgets. Local authority OT (adult social care) provides needs assessments under the Care Act 2014, focusing on equipment provision and home modifications (Disabled Facilities Grant). Both may be accessed following the same GP referral in some areas; in others they operate as separate pathways.
4. **What is a Band 5 OT and what does it involve?** → Answer direction: Band 5 is the newly qualified OT grade under NHS Agenda for Change. It represents approximately the first 1–2 years of practice. Band 5 OTs carry a caseload under supervision, develop clinical skills, and work toward Band 6 consolidation of competency. The salary range (approximately £28,407–£34,581 in England, 2023/24) is a common assignment reference point for professional development essays.
5. **How long do people wait for OT on the NHS?** → Answer direction: Waiting times vary widely. Acute (inpatient) OT typically works on same-day/next-day referral. Community OT waiting times have been reported at 6–18 months in some areas due to demand. Mental health OT waiting times vary by team. Paediatric OT via CAMHS can be among the longest waits. These waiting times are relevant to OT assignment discussions of occupational deprivation and service equity.

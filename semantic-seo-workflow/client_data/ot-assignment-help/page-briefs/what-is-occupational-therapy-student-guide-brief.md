#### H2: Frequently Asked Questions — What Is Occupational Therapy?
> **Critical rule**: These questions must NOT repeat Main Content. They expand toward new representative query variants — long-tail, People Also Ask, and voice-search optimised. Each answer must be self-contained enough to rank as a passage independently.

**Questions and Answer Directions**:

1. **Is occupational therapy a good career choice?**
   → Answer direction: OT is a regulated allied health profession (HCPC), in high demand across NHS, social care, and private sectors; NHS Band 5 entry salary plus career progression to Band 8; growing need in ageing population, mental health services, and paediatric services; highly varied scope of practice across settings. Do not make subjective career recommendations — present factual demand and variety data. 3–4 sentences.

2. **What is the difference between an occupational therapist and an occupational therapy assistant?**
   → Answer direction: An OT is a HCPC-registered professional with a degree-level qualification who independently assesses, plans, and leads intervention; an OTA (Occupational Therapy Assistant) works under OT supervision, implements defined aspects of the intervention plan, and does not hold autonomous professional registration. OTAs may study for foundation degrees or apprenticeship qualifications. 3–4 sentences.

3. **Can occupational therapists prescribe medication?**
   → Answer direction: No — OTs are not prescribers in the UK (unlike some nurse/pharmacist independent prescribers). OT prescribes in the OT sense — prescribing assistive technology, adaptive equipment, and home modifications; this is distinct from pharmaceutical prescribing. Clarify the difference clearly. 2–3 sentences.

4. **What is the difference between occupational therapy and speech and language therapy?**
   → Answer direction: SLT focuses on communication, swallowing, and language; OT focuses on occupational performance and functional independence. Both professions work in MDTs in similar settings (acute, paediatric, community). Brief comparison noting overlap in AAC (OT may prescribe the device; SLT may programme the language content). 3–4 sentences.

5. **What subjects do you study in an occupational therapy degree?**
   → Answer direction: Core modules typically include: anatomy and physiology for OT, occupational science, OT theory and models (MOHO, CMOP-E, OTPF), research methods, evidence-based practice, clinical skills, mental health OT, physical OT, paediatric OT, practice placement learning. At postgraduate level: advanced clinical reasoning, leadership, dissertation, specialist practice modules. 4–5 sentences. Signal that degree programme overview content is available on the site.

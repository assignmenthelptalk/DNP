### H1: Medical Clinic and Dental Practice Website Design in Kenya: Convert Patient Searches to Appointments

**Opening paragraph (50–80 words):**
When a Nairobi patient searches "dentist near me" or "pediatric clinic Westlands," the first thing they do when they find your website is look for three things: are you qualified, are you affordable, and can I book easily? A Kenyan clinic website that answers all three questions clearly and quickly converts searches into appointments. One that doesn't — sends that patient to your competitor.

---

### H2: What Kenyan Patients Look for on a Clinic Website Before Booking

**Contextual directive:** Start with patient behaviour research — what drives a Kenyan patient's decision to book at one clinic over another based on website content.

**EAV depth required:**
- Entity: Kenyan Healthcare Patient
- Decision factors patients look for on a clinic website:
  - Qualifications: doctor specializations, university degrees, years of experience — Kenya-specific: KMPDC (Kenya Medical Practitioners and Dentists Council) registration number prominently displayed
  - NHIF acceptance: "Do you accept NHIF?" is the first question from most Kenyan patients — must be answered on homepage and service pages, not buried in FAQ
  - Services and costs: explicit service listing with pricing in Ksh — Kenyan patients comparison-shop medical fees
  - Location and access: Google Maps embed, parking availability, nearest public transport (matatu routes) — especially important for clinics outside CBD
  - Opening hours: including Saturday and emergency/after-hours options — many Kenyan clinics operate Saturdays
  - Contact method: WhatsApp number for pre-booking questions — Kenyan patients prefer WhatsApp to phone calls for medical inquiries (privacy)
  - Patient reviews: Google reviews displayed or linked — social proof before first visit

**Word count target:** 280–340 words

---

### H2: Online Appointment Booking for Kenyan Clinics: WhatsApp vs Online Form

**Contextual directive:** Cover appointment booking options — the most critical conversion feature of a Kenyan clinic website. Address the WhatsApp-first reality of Kenyan patient behaviour.

**EAV depth required:**
- Kenyan patient booking behaviour: majority of Kenyan clinic appointments are made via phone call or WhatsApp — not via online forms. Website booking system must acknowledge this reality
- Booking options by practice size:
  - Small clinic (1–3 doctors): WhatsApp CTA button with pre-filled message ("I would like to book an appointment with [Dr Name]") — sufficient and preferred by Kenyan patients
  - Mid-size clinic (4–10 doctors): simple form (name, phone, preferred doctor, service, preferred date) + WhatsApp backup option
  - Larger practice (specialty hospital, multi-branch): full online booking system with real-time calendar availability, doctor selection, appointment type — can integrate with clinic management software (Daktari, HealthIT Kenya)
- SMS confirmation: booking confirmation via SMS to Kenyan phone number — Africa's Talking SMS gateway integration
- Appointment reminder: SMS 24 hours before appointment — significantly reduces Kenyan no-show rates
- NHIF pre-authorization note: for NHIF patients, include note about pre-authorization requirements on booking form

**Word count target:** 300–360 words

### H2: NHIF and Insurance Information: The Trust Signal Kenyan Patients Need

**Contextual directive:** Cover NHIF display as a conversion-critical element unique to Kenyan healthcare websites.

**EAV depth required:**
- NHIF importance in Kenya: NHIF (National Hospital Insurance Fund) covers 90%+ of formal sector Kenyan employees — patients actively search for "NHIF accredited clinics" and filter providers by NHIF acceptance
- NHIF accreditation display: NHIF logo, accreditation number, and explicit statement: "We accept NHIF for all covered services" — visible on homepage, service pages, and contact page
- NHIF coverage specifics page: dedicated page or section listing which NHIF schemes are accepted (NHIF capitation, fee-for-service, SHA — Social Health Authority 2024 transition)
- Private insurance: SHA (Social Health Authority) replacing NHIF from 2024 — clinic websites must update messaging to reflect the SHA transition and list covered providers (Jubilee, AAR, UAP, Resolution Health, Britam, APA)
- Cash patients: clear Ksh pricing for cash/out-of-pocket patients — transparency builds trust and captures the significant cash-pay segment of Kenyan healthcare

**Word count target:** 280–340 words

### H2: Doctor and Dentist Profile Pages: Qualifications and KMPDC Registration

**Contextual directive:** Cover doctor profile pages as a primary trust-building element for Kenyan medical websites.

**EAV depth required:**
- Doctor profile page elements:
  - Professional photo (formal, clinical setting — not stock photo)
  - Full name and title (Dr., Specialist designation)
  - KMPDC registration number: mandatory display for Kenya compliance and patient trust
  - Specialization and sub-specializations
  - Medical school and graduation year (Kenyan institutions: University of Nairobi, Moi University, Kenyatta University School of Medicine; international: recognized regional/UK/US institutions)
  - Years of practice in Kenya
  - Languages spoken (English, Swahili, local Kenyan languages if applicable — trust signal)
  - Conditions treated (links to relevant service pages — SEO benefit + patient clarity)
  - Personal statement: 2–3 sentences about clinical philosophy and approach to Kenyan patient care
- Dentist-specific: dental school, BDS/MBChB qualification, specialty (oral surgeon, orthodontist, prosthodontist, pediatric dentist)
- Privacy consideration: some Kenyan doctors prefer not to be individually profiled — offer team page as alternative showing the practice's collective credentials

**Word count target:** 280–340 words

### H2: Service Pages for Kenyan Medical and Dental Websites

**Contextual directive:** Cover the service page structure for healthcare websites — each service gets its own optimized page that targets specific Kenyan patient search queries.

**EAV depth required:**
- Medical clinic service pages:
  - General consultation, antenatal care, child wellness, immunization, chronic disease management (diabetes, hypertension — highly searched in Kenya), laboratory services
  - Each service page: description in patient-friendly language, NHIF coverage status, price range (Ksh), preparation instructions, booking CTA
- Dental practice service pages:
  - Teeth cleaning (scaling and polishing), tooth extraction, fillings, root canal treatment, orthodontics (braces), teeth whitening, dental implants, dentures, pediatric dentistry
  - Before/after dental photo gallery: high conversion for cosmetic dentistry — display with patient consent disclaimer
  - Price transparency: dental prices in Ksh (patients are price-sensitive in Kenya) — "Teeth cleaning from Ksh 2,500"
- Patient education content (SEO goldmine for Kenyan healthcare websites):
  - "How to clean your teeth properly" — 1,000+ monthly searches in Kenya
  - "Signs of diabetes in Kenya"
  - "Baby vaccination schedule Kenya 2025"
  - These informational pages drive discovery and build topical authority in Kenyan healthcare search

**Word count target:** 300–360 words

### H2: Local SEO for Clinics: Ranking When Nairobi Patients Search for Your Specialty

**Contextual directive:** Cover the local SEO opportunity for Kenyan clinic websites — the highest-ROI digital investment for most healthcare practices.

**EAV depth required:**
- Local search patterns for Nairobi clinics: "dentist Kilimani," "gynecologist Westlands," "pediatric clinic Karen," "dental clinic near me Nairobi"
- Google Business Profile for clinics: most critical local SEO tool — category selection ("Dentist," "Medical Clinic," "General Practitioner"), NHIF acceptance as attribute, photo strategy (clinic exterior, reception, treatment rooms — no patient photos without consent)
- Review strategy for Kenyan clinics: post-appointment WhatsApp message with Google review link — gentle, compliant, effective. Responding to all reviews (positive and negative) professionally
- Location pages: multi-branch clinic needs individual location pages for each branch
- Medical schema: HealthBusiness, MedicalOrganization, Physician schema markup for enhanced search appearance

### SUPP H2: Medical/Dental Website FAQs for Kenyan Practices

**FAQ entries:**
1. Q: How much does a medical clinic website cost in Kenya? A: A standard clinic website at Tupate Studio — service pages, doctor profiles, appointment booking (WhatsApp-based), NHIF information, and Google Maps integration — costs Ksh 25,000. Clinic websites with online booking systems, multi-doctor scheduling, and patient portals are quoted separately from Ksh 60,000.
2. Q: Does my dental practice website need to show teeth pricing? A: Yes — price transparency significantly increases conversions for Kenyan dental practices. Patients comparison-shop. A dental website that shows "Teeth cleaning from Ksh 2,500" converts more initial inquiries than one that says "Contact us for pricing."
3. Q: Can I use stock photos of doctors on my clinic website? A: Using stock photos is strongly discouraged for Kenyan clinic websites — Kenyan patients are discerning and will research your clinic on social media and Google Maps. Use professional photos of your actual doctors. Real photos build significantly more trust than stock images of non-specific medical professionals.
4. Q: How do Kenyan patients search for a specialist online? A: Most Kenyan patients search by specialty + location: "cardiologist Nairobi," "orthodontist Westlands," "pediatrician Karen Nairobi." Your clinic website needs individual service pages that specifically target the specialties you offer.

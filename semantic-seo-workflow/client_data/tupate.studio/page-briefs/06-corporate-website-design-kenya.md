### H2: Corporate Website Design Elements That Build Trust in Kenya

**Contextual directive:** Cover the specific design and content elements that establish corporate credibility for Kenyan companies.

**EAV depth required:**
- Trust elements:
  1. **Leadership section:** CEO and board members with professional headshots and credentials — critical for Kenyan corporate credibility
  2. **Regulatory displays:** KRA PIN, business registration certificate number, professional body membership (LSK for law firms, ICPAK for accountants, KEBS certification for manufacturers)
  3. **Physical address:** full physical address with map — not just Nairobi, but specific building/floor number
  4. **Annual reports:** PDF downloads for listed companies and larger NGOs — signals transparency
  5. **Press room:** media releases, awards, press mentions — signals legitimacy
  6. **Client logos:** Kenyan and international client logos where permitted — peer credibility
  7. **Physical credentials:** ISO certification badges, Dun & Bradstreet ratings, industry awards
  8. **SSL + security:** enterprise SSL certificate (not free Let's Encrypt for corporate tier), Kenyan Data Protection Act compliance statement
  9. **Professional photography:** building, team, equipment — not stock photos. Stock photos immediately signal "not a real large company" to Kenyan procurement officers.
- Typography for corporate sites: serif fonts for established institutions (law, finance); modern sans-serif for tech and NGO; body text 18px minimum for desktop readability

### H2: Kenya Data Protection Act Compliance for Corporate Websites

**Contextual directive:** The Kenya Data Protection Act 2019 (KDPA) is a mandatory compliance requirement for any Kenyan website collecting personal data. Corporate websites typically collect substantial data. This section demonstrates regulatory knowledge.

**EAV depth required:**
- Entity: Kenya Data Protection Act 2019 (KDPA)
- Applicable to: any Kenyan website collecting, storing, or processing personal data (name, email, phone, IP address, cookies)
- Compliance requirements for corporate websites:
  1. **Privacy Policy:** mandatory page explaining what data is collected, why, how long it's kept, and data subject rights. Must reference KDPA 2019 (not just GDPR).
  2. **Cookie consent:** if using cookies (analytics, marketing), must obtain explicit consent from Kenyan users
  3. **Data Subject Rights:** users must be able to request data access, correction, or deletion — this means a process (email address or form) for handling requests
  4. **Data breach notification:** KDPA requires notifying the Office of the Data Protection Commissioner (ODPC) within 72 hours of a breach
  5. **Data Processing Agreement:** if using third-party tools (Google Analytics, HubSpot), document data processing agreements
- Who enforces it: Office of the Data Protection Commissioner (ODPC) — penalties for non-compliance up to Ksh 5 million or 1% of annual gross turnover
- What Tupate Studio includes: privacy policy template, KDPA-compliant cookie consent banner, data request contact process, terms and conditions page

**Word count target:** 300–360 words

### H2: Multi-Department Website Architecture for Kenyan Companies

**Contextual directive:** Cover the information architecture challenge of corporate websites — multiple departments, stakeholders, and content types needing a coherent navigation.

**EAV depth required:**
- Navigation architecture patterns:
  - Mega menu: for large Kenyan companies with multiple product lines, divisions, or services
  - Audience-based navigation: "For Investors," "For Partners," "For Job Seekers," "For Media" — separates stakeholder journeys
  - Pillar page + hub structure: services pages with supporting sub-pages for each service line
- Kenya-specific sections:
  - **Tenders/Procurement:** many Kenyan companies list open tenders on their website — required by procurement regulations for public entities
  - **CSR/Sustainability:** Kenyan corporate audiences expect CSR content — increasingly scrutinized by NSE-listed companies
  - **Swahili version:** for Kenyan public-facing corporate content, Swahili language option expected by mass-market sectors (utilities, telecoms, government-adjacent companies)
- CMS architecture: corporate teams need to update content without developer access. Tupate Studio builds lightweight admin panels for content updates — NOT WordPress, but custom-built CMS with role-based access for PR team, HR team, and compliance team.
- SEO architecture for corporate sites: pillar pages target high-value terms; sub-pages target specific queries; cross-linking structure distributes PageRank systematically

**Word count target:** 300–360 words

### H2: Career Portal and Talent Acquisition Features for Kenyan Corporate Sites

**Contextual directive:** The careers section is a high-value corporate website feature. Cover it with Kenyan hiring market context.

**EAV depth required:**
- Job listings page: filterable by department, location (Nairobi, Mombasa, upcountry), contract type (permanent, contract, internship)
- Application form: integrated online application (not just email) — reduces friction vs "email CV to hr@company.co.ke"
- Employer branding content: "Why work with us," team culture photos, employee testimonials — critical for competing with NGOs and multinationals for Kenyan talent
- Integration: LinkedIn Easy Apply integration (for companies already using LinkedIn Recruiter), BrighterMonday job posting sync, MyJobMag Kenya
- ATS (Applicant Tracking System) integration: Workable, Zoho Recruit — for larger companies managing 100+ applications per role
- Mobile application: most Kenyan job seekers apply on mobile — career portal must be fully functional on smartphone

**Word count target:** 220–280 words

### H2: Content Management for Corporate Teams: Multi-Author Workflows

**Contextual directive:** Cover how the Tupate Studio corporate CMS works for teams — not for developers, but for communications, HR, and PR staff.

**EAV depth required:**
- Role-based access: communications officer can update news/press room; HR can manage job listings; finance can update investor relations PDFs; CEO office can update leadership bios
- Content approval workflows: draft → review → publish — prevents unauthorized content going live
- Revision history: every content edit is logged with timestamp and author
- Image management: built-in image compression and WebP conversion so non-technical staff don't upload 5MB photos that slow the site
- What is NOT included: the CMS is lightweight and purpose-built — it is not a full-featured platform like WordPress or Contentful. It covers content updates, not design changes.
- Maintenance retainer: Ksh 5,000/month covers CMS support, security patches, hosting monitoring, and one design change per month

- What local SEO does NOT include: social media marketing, Google Ads, influencer campaigns

### H2: Google Business Profile Setup and Optimization for Kenyan Businesses

**Contextual directive:** GBP is the single most impactful local SEO action. Cover it in depth — setup, optimization, and ongoing management. This section must demonstrate expertise and show Tupate Studio's hands-on approach.

**EAV depth required:**
- Entity: Google Business Profile (GBP)
- Setup attributes:
  - Business name: exact legal name (no keyword stuffing — Google penalizes this)
  - Category selection: primary category (most important) + secondary categories (e.g., Primary: "Dental Clinic," Secondary: "Teeth Whitening Service," "Orthodontist")
  - Address verification: Google postcard or video verification for Kenya businesses
  - Phone number: local Kenyan number (+254 format) — avoid toll-free numbers in local pack
  - Website URL: link to relevant location page (not homepage for multi-location businesses)
  - Business hours: include Kenya public holidays (Madaraka Day, Mashujaa Day, Jamhuri Day, Christmas, etc.)
- Optimization attributes:
  - Business description: 750-character limit; first 250 characters are visible without clicking "more"; include primary keyword, location, and USP in first 250 chars
  - Services/Products listing: add each service with name, description, and price range (even approximate)
  - Photo types: exterior photo (street-facing, daytime), interior, team photo (professional), product/service photos, 360° if possible. Minimum 10 photos.
  - Photo quantities: businesses with 100+ photos get 520% more phone calls than those with fewer photos (Google data)
  - Q&A section: seed 5–10 questions and answer them yourself (before customers ask negative ones)
  - Attributes: "Accepts M-Pesa," "Wheelchair accessible," "Free Wi-Fi," "Outdoor seating" — claim all relevant attributes
  - Custom short URL: claim google.com/maps/d/[businessname] for sharing
- Ongoing management:
  - Google Posts: weekly posts (offers, events, updates) signal an active business to Google's algorithm
  - Review responses: respond to every review within 24–48 hours — responses are indexed by Google
  - Message feature: enable WhatsApp-style messaging through GBP for Nairobi service businesses

**Word count target:** 450–520 words

### H2: Local Citation Building: Where Your Kenyan Business Must Be Listed

**Contextual directive:** NAP consistency and local citations are the foundation of local authority. Cover Kenya-specific citation sources — competitors in other markets cannot replicate this.

**EAV depth required:**
- What a local citation is: any online mention of business Name + Address + Phone number
- Why citations matter: Google cross-references business information across the web to verify it is real and consistent
- NAP consistency rule: the same format everywhere — "Tupate Studio, Nairobi, Kenya, +254 7XX XXX XXX" — not "+254 7XXXXXXXX" on some sites and "0700000000" on others
- Kenya-specific citation sources (Tier 1 — must be on all):
  1. Google Business Profile (primary)
  2. Yellow Pages Kenya (yellowpages.co.ke)
  3. Mocality Kenya (if operational)
  4. Kenya Business Directory (kenyabusinessdirectory.co.ke)
  5. PigiaMe Business listings
  6. BrighterMonday (for service businesses with team)
- Industry-specific Kenya citations:
  - Medical: Kenya Medical Association directory, SafariDoctors
  - Legal: Law Society of Kenya directory
  - Education: Ministry of Education Kenya school listing
  - Hospitality: TripAdvisor, Booking.com, Google Hotels
  - Manufacturing/Industrial: Kenya Association of Manufacturers (KAM) directory
- Social citations: Facebook Business Page, LinkedIn Company Page, Instagram Business profile — consistent NAP across all

**Contextual directive:** This section processes user behavior and its implications for local SEO strategy. High differentiation content — specific to Kenya's search behavior patterns.

**EAV depth required:**
- "Near me" growth: "near me" searches in Kenya have grown 150%+ in the past 3 years (Google trend data)
- Mobile and near me: 95%+ of "near me" searches happen on mobile — mobile-optimized GBP and website are mandatory
- Location modifiers Kenyan users use: "Nairobi CBD," "Westlands," "Industrial Area," "Karen," "Kilimani," "Mombasa town," "Nyali," "Kisumu town"

### H2: Getting More Google Reviews from Kenyan Customers

**Contextual directive:** Reviews are a direct local ranking signal AND a conversion signal. Cover how to ethically generate reviews from Kenyan customers.

**EAV depth required:**
- Review quantity threshold: businesses with 15+ reviews in a local category begin to noticeably outperform those with 0–5
- Review recency: 3 reviews this month outweigh 30 reviews from 2 years ago — freshness matters
- Review request methods for Kenyan customers:
  - WhatsApp message after service: "Thank you for choosing us! If you're happy with our service, a Google review takes 1 minute and helps other Kenyans find us" + direct GBP review link
  - QR code at business premises: printed card with QR → direct to Google review form
  - Email follow-up (for businesses with customer email list)
  - SMS via Africa's Talking (bulk SMS) for post-service review requests
- Review response strategy: respond to ALL reviews (positive and negative). Positive: thank by name, mention service. Negative: acknowledge, apologize professionally, offer to resolve offline — never argue publicly
- What NOT to do: buying fake reviews (Google detects and penalizes); incentivizing reviews (violates Google TOS); asking employees/family only
- Cultural note: Kenyan customers generally need a direct, personal request to leave reviews — passive "please review us" signs are less effective than direct WhatsApp asks

**Word count target:** 280–340 words

### H2: Local SEO for Nairobi, Mombasa, Kisumu, and Other Kenyan Cities

**Contextual directive:** Segment local SEO by Kenya's major markets. This section captures city-specific representative queries and demonstrates national coverage.

**EAV depth required:**
- Nairobi: highest competition, highest search volume. Sub-area targeting is critical. Industrial Area, Westlands, CBD, Karen, Kilimani have distinct business types and search volumes.
- Mombasa: coastal city, high hospitality and tourism searches. Arabic language queries relevant for some businesses. Mombasa Island vs Mainland distinctions in search behavior.
- Kisumu: Lake Victoria region. Growing digital economy. Lower competition than Nairobi — easier to rank. Key sectors: fishing industry, logistics, healthcare.
- Nakuru: industrial and agricultural hub. Rift Valley region searches. Growing SME digital adoption.
- Eldoret: North Rift hub. Agricultural businesses, logistics, healthcare. Very low digital competition — early movers rank easily.
- Upcountry towns (Nyeri, Meru, Thika, Machakos): minimal digital competition. Any local business with a GBP and basic website can rank #1 for its category.
- Rural areas: digital connectivity improving via Safaricom 4G expansion. WhatsApp-referred searches (click from WhatsApp link) becoming significant traffic source.

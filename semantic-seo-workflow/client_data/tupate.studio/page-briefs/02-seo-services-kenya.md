### H2: How Google Ranks Kenyan Business Websites

**Contextual directive:** Explain Google's ranking system in plain language for a Kenyan business audience. Use the Holistic SEO framework — responsiveness, relevance, historical data — translated into accessible terms. This section differentiates Tupate Studio from agencies that give vague answers.

**EAV depth required:**
- Relevance signals: does the page answer the search query? (content matching, keyword placement, entity coverage)
- Responsiveness: can Google extract a direct answer from the page? (structured data, heading hierarchy, FAQ format)
- Authority signals: what other sites link to this website? (backlinks, citations, directory listings)
- Historical data (accessible language: "user engagement signals"): does Google see users clicking, reading, returning? (CTR, dwell time, pogo-sticking — explained simply)
- Kenya-specific factor: Google's local algorithm weighs Google Business Profile signals heavily — businesses with an optimized GBP rank in the 3-pack, which appears above all organic results
- Algorithm update context: Broad Core Updates change how Google weights relevance — this is why SEO is ongoing, not once-off

### H2: Technical SEO: Building the Foundation for Search Visibility in Kenya

**Contextual directive:** Cover technical SEO at an introductory depth that demonstrates expertise without overwhelming. The deep dive lives on the Technical SEO Audit page — this is the summary. Bridge to that page in supplementary content.

**EAV depth required:**
- Crawlability: XML sitemap, robots.txt, internal linking structure — Google must find every page
- Indexation: canonical tags, noindex audits, Google Search Console coverage — is every important page indexed?
- Core Web Vitals: LCP (page load speed), INP (interaction response), CLS (visual stability) — Kenya relevance: slow Kenyan hosting fails LCP targets routinely
- Schema markup: LocalBusiness, Product, FAQ, BreadcrumbList — structured data that makes Google understand content type
- Mobile usability: mobile-first indexing means mobile errors kill rankings for desktop too
- HTTPS/SSL: Google penalizes non-HTTPS sites; Kenyan business owners must ensure SSL is active
- Kenya-specific technical issue: many Kenyan websites are hosted on low-quality shared hosting — TTFB (server response time) over 800ms is common and directly damages Core Web Vitals scores

**Word count target:** 320–380 words

### H2: How Long Does SEO Take to Work for a Business in Kenya

**Contextual directive:** Address the most common objection directly — "SEO takes too long." Be honest but frame correctly. Use real timelines. This section converts skeptical prospects.

**EAV depth required:**
- New website: 3–6 months for initial ranking traction (no historical data, trust-building phase)
- Established website: 1–3 months for measurable ranking improvements on optimized pages
- Local SEO: fastest results (GBP optimizations can show in Google Maps within 2–4 weeks)
- Factors that accelerate results: publishing frequency (Momentum), technical foundation quality, competitor weakness in the niche
- Historical data context (simplified): Google needs to see users engaging positively with your site over time — this is why month 6 results are stronger than month 1
- What "results" means: rankings → impressions → clicks → phone calls/form submissions — each stage has its timeline
- Comparison to Google Ads: Ads give instant traffic but stop when budget stops. SEO compounds over time — results at month 6 continue at month 18 without proportional cost increase.

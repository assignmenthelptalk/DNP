### H2: Photos That Drive Clicks: GBP Image Strategy for Kenyan Businesses

**Contextual directive:** Cover the role of GBP photos in converting Google Maps impressions to profile views and website clicks for Kenyan businesses. Include photo type, quantity, and quality standards.

**EAV depth required:**
- Photo types and minimum quantities:
  - Exterior: 3+ photos (street view, signage, entrance) — helps Kenyan customers identify the physical location
  - Interior: 5+ photos (reception, working areas, equipment) — builds trust before visit
  - Team: 3+ photos (real staff, not stock) — Kenyan customers trust businesses they can see are real
  - Products/services in action: 5+ photos
  - Logo: 1 (appears as profile icon)
  - Cover photo: 1 (800×600px minimum, representing the business purpose clearly)
- Format: JPG or PNG, minimum 720×720px — Google auto-rejects blurry or undersized images
- Update frequency: add new photos monthly — Google rewards active GBP profiles with better local pack positioning
- Kenya context: businesses with 10+ photos receive 35% more website clicks and 42% more direction requests than those with <5 photos (Google Business data)
- Video: up to 30 seconds, max 75MB — short shop tours or service demonstrations convert well in Kenya

**Word count target:** 300–360 words

### H2: Google Reviews Strategy: Getting and Responding to Reviews in Kenya

**Contextual directive:** Cover the full review lifecycle for Kenyan businesses — solicitation (compliant), responding, and handling negative reviews in Kenya's cultural context.

**EAV depth required:**
- Why reviews matter in Kenya: Kenyan consumers heavily rely on social proof — Google reviews are the digital equivalent of word-of-mouth, the primary trust mechanism in Kenyan business culture
- Solicitation methods (Google TOS compliant):
  - WhatsApp message with GBP review link (most effective in Kenya — highest open rate)
  - Receipt/invoice QR code linking to review page
  - Post-service email with review request
  - Physical signage with QR code at counter
- Review link generation: Google Maps → Share → Copy Link → shorten with bit.ly
- Responding to positive reviews: 1–2 sentence response including business name and service keyword
- Responding to negative reviews in Kenyan cultural context: acknowledge, apologize, offer resolution offline via WhatsApp — never argue publicly
- Fake review reporting: how to flag and dispute false reviews on Google Maps Kenya
- Review velocity: 2–5 genuine reviews per month is better than 20 reviews in one week (triggers spam filter)

**Word count target:** 320–380 words

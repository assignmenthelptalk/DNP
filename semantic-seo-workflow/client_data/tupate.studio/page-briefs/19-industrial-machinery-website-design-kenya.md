### H1: Industrial and Machinery Business Website Design in Kenya: Built for Procurement Managers and Operations Directors

**Opening paragraph (50–80 words):**
State the B2B reality immediately: a procurement manager at a Nairobi construction company or a Mombasa manufacturing plant does not browse websites the same way a consumer does. They are searching for: technical specifications, service capabilities, certifications, and evidence that your company has served similar Kenyan clients at scale. An industrial company website in Kenya that fails to communicate these within the first 10 seconds of a visit loses the tender to a competitor who does.

---

### H2: What an Industrial Company Website Must Do for Kenyan B2B Buyers

**Contextual directive:** Define the unique commercial function of an industrial website in Kenya — it is a procurement tool, not a brochure.

**EAV depth required:**
- Entity: Industrial Business Website (Kenya)
- Attributes and required outcomes:
  - Technical credibility: certifications, accreditations (ISO 9001, KEBS approval, NEMA certification, county business permit) displayed prominently on homepage and product pages
  - Capability communication: clear statement of what equipment is sold/serviced, service radius (Kenya counties covered), minimum order quantities
  - Contact priority: WhatsApp number, direct phone number, physical address with map (Nairobi Industrial Area, Mombasa Port area, Kisumu Industrial area — Kenya's industrial hubs) — procurement managers prefer phone before email
  - Download-readiness: technical spec PDFs downloadable without registration — procurement managers need to share specs internally without friction
  - Company history: years in Kenya market, major Kenyan clients served (by category, not necessarily named), project scale (e.g., "supplied generators to 15+ Kenyan county hospitals")
- Why standard B2C websites fail for Kenyan industrial businesses: template designs with stock photos of handshakes and generic "we are committed to excellence" language — procurement managers recognize and distrust generic design instantly

**Word count target:** 280–340 words

### H2: Equipment Catalog Design: Specs, Datasheets, and Technical Tables for Kenyan Industrial Sites

**Contextual directive:** Cover the product/equipment catalog as the most critical and most absent element from Kenyan industrial websites.

**EAV depth required:**
- Catalog structure: equipment categories → subcategories → individual equipment pages
- Equipment page must-haves:
  - Full technical specifications table (dimensions, weight, power rating, capacity, operating conditions)
  - Multiple product photos (360° views, detail shots, installation photos from Kenyan sites)
  - Downloadable PDF datasheet (link without registration gate)
  - "Request a Quote" button — opens RFQ form specific to that product
  - Related accessories / spare parts
  - Compatible applications (what Kenyan industries use this equipment: construction, mining, agriculture, food processing)
- Kenya-specific catalog factors:
  - Voltage specifications: Kenya operates at 240V 50Hz — equipment pages must state compatibility
  - Spare parts availability: Kenyan procurement managers prioritize suppliers with local spare parts stock — this must be stated explicitly
  - Service coverage: which Kenyan counties your service engineers can reach for maintenance and breakdown response
  - Currency: all pricing in Ksh (even if also quoting in USD for internationally sourced equipment)

**Word count target:** 320–380 words

---

### H2: Service and Maintenance Pages for Kenyan Industrial Businesses

**Contextual directive:** Cover the service offering section — often neglected but a major revenue driver for Kenyan industrial companies.

**EAV depth required:**
- Service page types for Kenyan industrial websites:
  - Preventive maintenance: scheduled maintenance contracts for equipment — show counties covered, response time SLA
  - Breakdown / emergency repair: 24-hour response option — critical for manufacturing clients who cannot afford downtime
  - Spare parts supply: stock availability, lead times for non-stock items (international shipping to Kenya timelines)
  - Calibration services: for measuring instruments — include calibration certificate template
  - Operator training: training programs for Kenyan equipment operators, OSHA Kenya compliance training
  - Installation services: commissioning at client sites across Kenya
- Service page conversion elements: WhatsApp service request button, service inquiry form with equipment model field, estimated response time stated
- Service area map: interactive Google Map showing counties where service engineers are based — critical for Kenyan industrial buyers outside Nairobi

**Word count target:** 280–340 words

### H2: Trust Signals That Win Tenders: Certifications, Case Studies, and Client Logos

**Contextual directive:** Cover the trust and credibility signals that win Kenyan industrial contracts — these are the most conversion-critical elements for B2B industrial websites.

**EAV depth required:**
- Certifications to display (with certificate scan images):
  - ISO 9001 (Quality Management System)
  - KEBS (Kenya Bureau of Standards) product approval for locally manufactured equipment
  - NEMA (National Environment Management Authority) environmental compliance
  - NCA (National Construction Authority) for construction equipment suppliers
  - Kenya Revenue Authority (KRA) PIN certificate — proves tax compliance for government tenders
  - County business permit (applicable county)
- Client logos / references:
  - Category-level client logos (construction companies, county governments, parastatals, manufacturing firms)
  - Named client case studies: "Supplied and commissioned 4× 500KVA generators for [County Hospital Name]"
  - Quantity indicators: "200+ Kenyan clients served," "15 years in the Kenya market"
- Project gallery: photos of equipment installed at Kenyan client sites — ground-level authenticity signal
- Awards and media: feature in Business Daily, Nation Media industrial supplements, membership in Kenya Association of Manufacturers (KAM)

**Word count target:** 280–340 words

### H2: RFQ and Inquiry Forms Designed for Kenyan Procurement Workflows

**Contextual directive:** Cover the Request for Quotation (RFQ) form as the primary conversion mechanism for industrial B2B websites in Kenya.

**EAV depth required:**
- RFQ form fields optimized for Kenyan procurement:
  - Company name + county of operation
  - Contact person name + WhatsApp number + email
  - Equipment/product needed (dropdown of catalog categories)
  - Quantity required
  - Required delivery date
  - Application / intended use
  - Current equipment in use (helps sales team quote accurately)
  - How did you hear about us (conversion tracking)
- Form submission confirmation: WhatsApp auto-reply with quote reference number (using WhatsApp Business API) — Kenyan procurement managers expect immediate acknowledgement
- Tender document download section: for businesses that participate in government tenders — IFMIS (Integrated Financial Management Information System) registration number displayed, ability to download company registration documents (CR12, KRA PIN, KEBS certificates) in a single ZIP for tender attachment
- Response time commitment: "We respond to all RFQs within 4 business hours" — stated on the form page

**Word count target:** 260–320 words

### H2: Local Kenyan Hosting vs International Hosting: Speed Implications

**EAV depth required:**
- TTFB (Time to First Byte): the time from a browser request to the first byte returned by the server — primary hosting performance metric for Kenyan websites
- Local Kenyan hosting (server in Nairobi data centre):
  - TTFB for Kenyan users: 50–200ms — fastest possible for domestic Kenyan traffic
  - Available providers: Truehost Kenya (TIER III data centre Nairobi), Kenya Website Experts (KWE), Wingu Africa (Nairobi PoP)
  - Limitation: local Kenyan hosting is less reliable than global providers in terms of redundancy and uptime
- International hosting without CDN:
  - TTFB for Kenyan users: 800–2,500ms (UK/US/India servers) — unacceptably slow
  - Major providers: SiteGround, Bluehost, NameCheap hosting — adequate globally, poor TTFB to Kenya
- International hosting WITH Cloudflare CDN:
  - TTFB for Kenyan users of cached content: 50–150ms (Cloudflare has Nairobi PoP since 2020)
  - This is the sweet spot: reliable international hosting infrastructure + Nairobi CDN edge = fast and reliable
  - Providers that work well with Cloudflare: SiteGround, Namecheap, LiteSpeed-based hosts
- Cloud hosting with African region:
  - AWS af-south-1 (Cape Town, South Africa): 40–100ms TTFB to Nairobi users — best latency of major cloud providers for Kenya
  - Google Cloud africa-south1 (Johannesburg): similar performance to AWS af-south-1
  - DigitalOcean (no African PoP — nearest Amsterdam/Singapore, use Cloudflare)

### H2: Shared Hosting vs VPS vs Cloud for Kenyan Businesses

**EAV depth required:**
- Shared hosting:
  - Definition: multiple websites share one server's resources — CPU, RAM, bandwidth
  - Kenyan providers: Truehost Kenya from Ksh 500/month, KWE from Ksh 800/month
  - Best for: brochure websites, low-traffic blogs, startup Kenyan businesses (under 500 daily visitors)
  - Limitation: "noisy neighbour" effect — another website on the same server getting heavy traffic slows yours
- VPS (Virtual Private Server):
  - Definition: dedicated portion of a physical server — guaranteed RAM and CPU
  - Kenyan providers: Truehost Kenya VPS from Ksh 2,000/month, DigitalOcean from ~Ksh 1,300/month
  - Best for: medium-traffic Kenyan business websites, WordPress sites with WooCommerce, custom web applications
  - Advantage: consistent performance regardless of other websites on the physical server
- Cloud hosting:
  - Definition: resources drawn from a network of servers — scales automatically
  - Providers: AWS, Google Cloud, Cloudways (manages cloud infrastructure for you)
  - Best for: high-traffic Kenyan websites, e-commerce during peak seasons, applications requiring auto-scaling
  - Cost: variable — can be cheaper than VPS at low traffic, more expensive at high traffic

### H2: What to Look for in a Kenyan Web Host: Uptime, Support, and SSL

**EAV depth required:**
- Uptime SLA: minimum 99.9% uptime — this means 8.7 hours of downtime per year maximum
  - Kenya context: Nairobi power outages and internet disruptions can affect local hosting — choose hosts with diesel generator backup and redundant internet connections
  - Verification: use UptimeRobot (free) to monitor your Kenyan website's actual uptime independent of host claims
- Customer support:
  - Kenyan time zone (EAT = UTC+3): support must be available during Kenyan business hours — some international hosts offer 24/7 chat but response quality varies
  - Local Kenyan hosts (Truehost, KWE): phone and WhatsApp support in Kenyan time — preferred by Kenyan business owners for urgent issues
- SSL certificate: HTTPS is mandatory — Google Chrome marks HTTP sites as "Not Secure"
  - Free SSL: Let's Encrypt SSL included by most Kenyan hosting providers
  - Premium SSL: EV (Extended Validation) or Wildcard SSL — from Ksh 5,000–15,000/year — recommended for e-commerce and sites collecting sensitive customer data
- Backup policy: daily automated backups retained for at least 7–30 days — critical for Kenyan businesses without separate backup management

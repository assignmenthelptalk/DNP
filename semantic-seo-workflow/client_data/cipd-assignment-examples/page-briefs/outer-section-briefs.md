[Contextual Border]
6. [SUPP] Organisational Structure in the CIPD 5CO01 Assignment (bridge + link)

8. FAQ (5 Qs)

**Key EAV:**
- Hierarchical: many layers (5–10+), narrow span of control (5–8 direct reports), centralised decisions, formal communication chains, clear career progression ladder, common in public sector/manufacturing
- Flat: few layers (2–3), wide span of control (10–20+ direct reports), distributed decisions, autonomous teams, informal communication, common in tech startups, creative agencies

- HR implications of structure: recruitment criteria differ (hierarchical values compliance; flat values initiative), reward design differs (grades/tenure vs skills/contribution), performance management differs (formal cycles vs continuous feedback)

6. [SUPP] Maternity Leave in the CIPD 5OS01 Assignment (bridge + link — CRITICAL)
7. [SUPP] Keeping in Touch Days and Return to Work Planning

**Key EAV:**
- OML: first 26 weeks — all employees entitled regardless of length of service; right to return to same job
- AML: weeks 27–52 — right to return to same or suitable alternative job
- SMP: 90% of average weekly earnings for first 6 weeks; then statutory flat rate (£184.03/week from April 2025) or 90% of AWE if lower — for 33 weeks; total 39 weeks SMP
- KIT days: up to 10 Keeping in Touch days during maternity leave — employee can work without losing SMP
- SPL vs maternity: maternity leave is automatic for eligible mothers; SPL must be opted into by both partners; SPL can be shared between partners in any split; maternity provides 52 weeks while SPL provides up to 50 weeks (2 weeks minimum compulsory maternity leave)
- Key difference for 5OS01 AC: maternity leave = automatic right of the mother; SPL = opt-in right shared between both parents, requires formal notification and curtailment of maternity leave

[Contextual Border]
6. [SUPP] CIPD Code of Conduct in the 5CO03 Assignment (bridge + link)
7. [SUPP] Professional Ethics at Level 7: Business Acumen and Ethical Dilemmas (link to 7CO03)
8. FAQ (5 Qs)

**Key EAV:**
- CIPD Code of Conduct: 4 principles — professional competence and integrity; honest, inclusive and fair working; respect and promote working in the public interest; champion better work and working lives
- CIPD Profession Map (2018): three layers — Core (purpose, values), Specialist Knowledge (28 specialist areas), Core Behaviours (8 behaviours: ethical practice, valuing people, professional courage, commercial drive, passion for learning, insights-focused, situationally decisive, skilled influencer)
- Ethical decision-making frameworks: consequentialism (greatest good for greatest number), deontology (duty-based — rules must be followed regardless of outcome), virtue ethics (what would a person of good character do?)
- Conflicts of interest: HR practitioners must declare where personal interests conflict with professional duty — e.g. recruiting a family member, managing a close personal relationship
- Whistleblowing: public interest disclosure — HR's role in creating a speak-up culture vs managing the risk of malicious disclosure

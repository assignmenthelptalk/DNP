## EAV Requirements

### Entity: CIPD Level 3 Pass Standard
**Attributes + Values:**
- Qualification: CIPD Level 3 Foundation Certificate in People Practice
- Assessment outcome: pass or refer only
- Pass requirement: every Assessment Criterion (AC) addressed
- Standard of response: practical, accurate, applied to an organisational context
- Theoretical depth: one relevant model or framework applied clearly is usually enough at Level 3
- Typical tone: operational, not strategic
- Expected learner: beginner in HR or L&D, career changer, early-stage practitioner
- Typical unit length: approximately 1,500-2,500 words total
- Typical per-question length: approximately 500-1,200 words depending on the brief

### Entity: Common Referral Triggers
**Attributes + Values:**
- Missing one AC entirely
- Defining a concept without applying it to a workplace example
- Writing generally instead of answering the exact command word in the brief
- Going significantly under the word count
- Using theory inaccurately
- Weak structure that makes it hard for the assessor to see where each AC is answered
- Referencing sources without clear connection to the actual question

### Entity: Useful Level 3 Writing Pattern
**Attributes + Values:**
- Define the concept accurately
- Apply it to a named organisation, case study, or workplace scenario
- Explain why it matters in practice
- Keep each paragraph tied to the AC being answered
- Make the assessor's job easy by signalling the criterion clearly

## Section Notes

### H2-1: What Does It Mean to Pass CIPD Level 3?
- Define Level 3 as the Foundation Certificate in People Practice.
- Clarify that pass standard is not about sounding academic; it is about being accurate, practical, and complete.
- State clearly that the single biggest rule is: address every AC.
- Distinguish Level 3 from Level 5: Level 3 requires application and clarity, not deep theoretical comparison.

### H2-2: How CIPD Level 3 Assignments Are Marked: Pass or Refer
- Explain pass/refer marking only.
- Make clear that one weak or missing AC can trigger referral.
- Explain that assessors look for direct answers to the brief, not generic HR knowledge.
- Reinforce that the marker needs to see clear evidence against each AC.

### H2-3: How to Read the Assessment Brief and Identify Every AC
- Explain the role of command words such as describe, explain, assess, compare.
- Show that students should break the brief into AC-by-AC tasks before writing.
- Reinforce that the unit brief takes priority over generic online advice.
- Encourage students to map each section of their answer to a criterion before drafting.

### H2-4: The Best Structure for Answering a CIPD Level 3 Question: Define, Apply, Explain
- Use a simple repeatable pass framework.
- "Define" = accurate concept or framework.
- "Apply" = connect to a real organisation, your workplace, or the scenario in the brief.
- "Explain" = show why it matters or what effect it has in practice.
- Keep this section practical and explicit because it is the highest-value part of the page.

### H2-5: How to Use Workplace Examples to Reach Pass Standard
- Explain that application is what turns theory into pass-standard evidence.
- Allow three valid sources of examples: current employer, previous employer, centre case study.
- Warn against generic examples such as "many organisations do this."
- Encourage specificity: name the organisation type, role, issue, and outcome.

### H2-6: Word Count, Referencing, and Presentation Basics for Level 3
- Include typical word count ranges already established on the site.
- Explain that under-writing usually signals missed depth.
- Keep referencing guidance practical and simple.
- Reinforce that presentation matters mainly because clear structure helps the assessor locate the AC response.

### Supplementary H2-7: The Most Common Reasons Students Are Referred at CIPD Level 3
- This section should convert anxiety into a checklist.
- Prioritise: missed ACs, no application, vague examples, weak structure, inaccurate theory, under word count.
- Keep framed as preventable errors rather than failure language.

### Supplementary H2-8: Which Level 3 Units Students Usually Find Hardest
- Briefly mention likely sticking points:
- 3CO01 because students must connect business context, culture, and change.
- 3CO02 because learners are less confident with data and metrics.
- 3CO04 because it spans several operational HR topics in one unit.
- Link outward to the Level 3 hub and relevant example pages.

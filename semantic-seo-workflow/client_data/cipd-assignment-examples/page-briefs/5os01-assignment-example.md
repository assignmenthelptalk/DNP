## EAV Requirements

### Entity: Maternity Leave (UK)
- Ordinary Maternity Leave (OML): first 26 weeks — all employees entitled from day one
- Additional Maternity Leave (AML): weeks 27–52 — continuous with OML
- Statutory Maternity Pay (SMP): 90% of average weekly earnings for first 6 weeks; then flat rate (£184.03/week as at 2025) or 90% of AWE if lower, for up to 33 weeks
- Right to return to same job after OML; right to return to same or equivalent job after AML
- Compulsory Maternity Leave: minimum 2 weeks off after birth (4 weeks for factory workers)
- Employer cannot make employee redundant solely because of pregnancy or maternity leave — automatically unfair dismissal and pregnancy discrimination

### Entity: Shared Parental Leave (SPL)
- Available to eligible mothers and their partners (or adopters)
- Eligibility: mother must qualify for maternity leave AND both parents must meet the earnings threshold
- Works by 'curtailing' (ending early) the mother's maternity leave to create a SPL pot
- SPL pot = up to 50 weeks leave and 37 weeks Statutory Shared Parental Pay (ShPP)
- Parents can take SPL separately, consecutively, or concurrently
- Statutory Shared Parental Pay rate: same flat rate as SMP (£184.03/week as at 2025) — no enhanced 90% period
- Key difference from maternity: more flexible in scheduling; can be taken in blocks; both parents can be on leave simultaneously

### Entity: Key Differences — Maternity Leave vs Shared Parental Leave

| Dimension | Maternity Leave | Shared Parental Leave |
|---|---|---|
| Who is entitled | Mother only | Mother and partner (both must qualify) |
| When available | From 11 weeks before EWC | After the first 2 weeks post-birth (compulsory maternity period) |
| Pay structure | 90% AWE for 6 weeks, then flat rate | Flat rate only (no enhanced first 6 weeks) |
| Flexibility | Fixed blocks — cannot be broken into non-continuous periods | Can be taken in separate blocks; concurrent use permitted |
| Default assumption | Mother is on leave unless she decides otherwise | Must be actively opted into by curtailing maternity leave |
| Employer enhanced pay | Maternity enhancement is common practice | SPL enhancement less common — equal treatment duty applies if offered |

### Entity: EU Employment Law Post-Brexit
- Retained EU Law Act 2023: EU-derived legislation is retained in UK law but can be amended/revoked by UK Parliament
- ECJ case law decided before 31 December 2020 is retained as UK domestic case law (binding on lower courts)
- ECJ judgments after 31 December 2020 are persuasive but not binding on UK courts
- Specific examples: Working Time Regulations 1998 (derived from EU), TUPE 2006, Part-Time Workers Regulations, Fixed-Term Employee Regulations — all remain in force as UK statute
- UK Supreme Court and Court of Appeal can now depart from retained EU case law where appropriate

## EAV Requirements

### Entity: TNA Three Levels
- Organisational TNA: driven by strategic plan, workforce plan, culture change — identifies what capabilities the organisation as a whole needs to build or close. Sources: strategy documents, SWOT, business leader interviews, HR analytics
- Team TNA: driven by team performance data, departmental objectives, manager assessment — identifies gaps at functional level. Sources: performance dashboard, manager surveys, team engagement scores, error/quality data
- Individual TNA: driven by appraisal conversation, self-assessment, 360 feedback, role change — identifies the specific person's development needs. Sources: performance review records, competency assessment, career conversation

### Entity: Competency Framework Structure
- Competency: a cluster of related knowledge, skills, and behaviours that enables effective performance
- Proficiency levels: typically 3–5 levels from Foundation to Expert (or Developing/Practising/Mastering)
- L&D application: gap = (required proficiency level) − (current assessed level); L&D activity closes the gap
- Succession planning link: identify which individuals are 1–2 proficiency levels below a critical senior role — target L&D investment to close that gap fastest

### Entity: L&D Prioritisation Criteria
- Strategic alignment: does this need connect to a current business priority?
- Performance risk: what is the cost of not closing this gap? (safety risk, compliance risk, revenue risk)
- Reach: how many people does this need affect?
- Feasibility: can this gap be closed through L&D, or is it a recruitment/structural issue?
- ROI potential: is the return on the L&D investment measurable?

## EAV Requirements

### Entity: Tuckman's Stages of Group Development
- Forming: group assembles — polite, cautious, dependent on leader for direction; facilitator role: provide structure, set norms, make introductions
- Storming: conflict emerges — competition, resistance, challenge to authority; facilitator role: acknowledge tension, refocus on task, model constructive disagreement
- Norming: cohesion develops — roles clarify, cooperation increases; facilitator role: step back, reinforce positive norms, encourage peer-to-peer interaction
- Performing: high productivity — self-managing, creative problem-solving; facilitator role: minimal direction, celebrate progress, stretch the group
- Adjourning: group disbands — reflection, closure, transfer of learning; facilitator role: consolidate learning, action planning, evaluation

### Entity: Facilitation Question Types
- Open questions: "What do you think about...?" — generate discussion, multiple valid responses
- Probing questions: "Can you say more about...?" — deepen a response, draw out reasoning
- Reflective questions: "How does that connect to your experience?" — link theory to practice
- Clarifying questions: "So you're saying that...?" — check understanding without leading
- Hypothetical questions: "What would happen if...?" — develop critical thinking

### Entity: Virtual Facilitation Engagement Techniques
- Polling (Mentimeter, Slido): real-time audience response, anonymous — increases participation safety
- Breakout rooms: small group discussion — replicates table work; assign roles (reporter, timekeeper) to maintain structure
- Digital whiteboard (Miro, Mural): collaborative visual thinking — replaces flip chart; use for brainstorming and mapping
- Chat function: parallel conversation stream — use deliberately, not as a substitute for verbal participation
- Non-verbal feedback (reactions, hand raise): quick check-ins without interrupting flow

## EAV Depth Map
**Primary Entity**: AGACNP DNP Capstone Project

**Main Attributes**: clinical settings, capstone topics, outcome metrics, data sources, frameworks, quality databases, IRB classification

**Derived Attributes**:
- **Clinical Settings**: medical-surgical ICU (MICU), surgical ICU (SICU), cardiac ICU (CICU), coronary care unit (CCU), step-down/progressive care unit, emergency department (ED), rapid response team (RRT), telemetry unit, hospitalist service
- **High-Value Capstone Topics**:
  - CLABSI prevention: central line bundle compliance (hand hygiene, CHG scrub, sterile technique, optimal catheter site, daily necessity assessment)
  - CAUTI prevention: CAUTI bundle compliance (aseptic insertion, securing device, closed system, daily need assessment, early removal)
  - VAP prevention: ventilator bundle compliance (HOB elevation, oral care, sedation vacation, spontaneous breathing trials, DVT/stress ulcer prophylaxis)
  - Sepsis 3-hour bundle adherence: lactate, blood cultures ×2, broad-spectrum antibiotics within 1 hour, IV fluids
  - ABCDEF bundle for ICU delirium prevention: Assess pain, Both SAT and SBT, Choice of sedation, Delirium assess/manage, Early mobility, Family engagement
  - Early mobility protocol for ICU patients (ICU-acquired weakness prevention)
  - Pressure injury prevention protocol (Braden scale documentation, repositioning compliance)
  - Palliative care integration in ICU (goals of care conversations, family meetings, comfort measures)
  - Nurse-driven foley catheter removal protocol
  - Transitions of care/discharge planning protocol (readmission reduction)
- **Outcome Metrics**: CLABSI per 1000 central line days, CAUTI per 1000 catheter days, VAP per 1000 ventilator days, sepsis bundle compliance rate (%), delirium incidence rate (CAM-ICU positive %), ICU length of stay (days), fall rate per 1000 patient days, pressure injury incidence rate (Stage 2+), 30-day readmission rate (%)
- **Quality Databases**: NHSN (National Healthcare Safety Network — HAI surveillance), NDNQI (Nursing-Sensitive Quality Indicators — ANA database), NSQIP (National Surgical Quality Improvement Program), AHRQ Patient Safety Indicators
- **IRB Classification**: hospital-acquired condition QI projects almost always qualify as QI non-research — routinely tracked infection data, no new data collection, findings limited to site improvement
- **Coordination Advantage**: AGACNP capstones often align with existing hospital quality department initiatives (Joint Commission standards, CMS Core Measures, Leapfrog standards) — coordinating with quality/infection control department is strategically valuable

### FAQ SECTION

**Q: What are the best AGACNP DNP capstone topics?**
The strongest AGACNP capstone topics address hospital-acquired conditions with established evidence-based bundles and existing quality tracking infrastructure. Top options include: CLABSI or CAUTI bundle compliance improvement, sepsis 3-hour bundle adherence, ABCDEF bundle implementation for ICU delirium prevention, and pressure injury prevention protocol. These topics have clear outcome metrics tracked by NHSN or NDNQI, strong published evidence bases, and typically qualify as QI non-research for IRB purposes.

**Q: Where does an AGACNP student get baseline outcome data for their capstone?**
AGACNP capstone projects typically use data already tracked by the hospital's infection control or quality department — NHSN HAI data, NDNQI nursing quality indicators, or EHR audit reports. The student's clinical site coordinator or quality department can usually provide de-identified aggregate data (rates per 1000 device days) that does not require individual patient chart review. This simplifies both data access and IRB classification.

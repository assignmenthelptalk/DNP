#### H2: What Must a DNP Capstone Proposal Include?
> **EAV to cover**: proposal structure overview, typical chapter count, length expectations, APA requirements
> **Word count**: 260–320 words
> **Specific content**:
> - Standard structure: 3-chapter format (Chapter 1: Introduction and Background; Chapter 2: Review of Literature and Theoretical Framework; Chapter 3: Methodology and Evaluation Plan)
> - Some universities use 5-section format — same content, different labeling
> - Length: 40–80 pages of text (excluding references and appendices); most proposals fall between 50–65 pages
> - APA 7th edition student paper format throughout — no running head, level 1–3 headings, author-date citations
> - Appendices typically include: evidence synthesis table, data collection instruments, IRB determination forms, timeline/Gantt chart, budget justification
> - Committee composition: faculty advisor (chair) + 2 committee members; all must approve before the proposal defense is scheduled

---

#### H2: Chapter 1: Background, Significance, and Problem Statement
> **EAV to cover**: all Chapter 1 sections with content requirements for each
> **Word count**: 400–480 words
> **Specific content**:
> - **Background of the Problem**: epidemiological data on the clinical problem at national, state, and local level; establishes significance and burden of the problem (e.g., national CAUTI rates, cost data, morbidity statistics)
> - **Significance to Practice**: why this problem matters for the specific clinical setting; connection to patient safety, quality metrics, or regulatory requirements (Joint Commission, CMS)
> - **Problem Statement**: one paragraph clearly stating what the problem is, where it occurs, and why it matters — specific to the student's clinical setting. Must be measurable (current rate or frequency of the problem)
> - **Purpose Statement**: states the aim of the project — "The purpose of this quality improvement project is to..."
> - **PICOT Question**: formally stated PICOT question in the standard sentence format — the culmination of Chapter 1
> - **Practice Questions**: 3–5 sub-questions that the PICOT generates (process questions about how implementation will occur)
> - **Scope and Limitations**: what the project will and will not address; setting-specific boundaries
> - **Definition of Terms**: operational definitions of key terms used throughout the proposal

---

#### H2: Chapter 2: Theoretical Framework and Literature Review
> **EAV to cover**: EBP framework selection, literature review methodology, evidence synthesis, practice gap identification
> **Word count**: 400–480 words
> **Specific content**:
> - **Theoretical/Conceptual Framework**: select one EBP framework (Iowa Model, PARIHS, ACE Star, Johns Hopkins, KTA, ARCC) and justify the selection based on the project type and setting; must explain how each stage of the framework maps to the capstone project components
> - **Literature Search Methodology**: databases searched (CINAHL Complete, PubMed/MEDLINE, Cochrane Library, PsycINFO where relevant); search terms (MeSH headings + CINAHL headings + keywords); Boolean operators (AND/OR); filters (date range 5–10 years, English language, peer-reviewed)
> - **PRISMA Flow Diagram**: required for systematic/integrative reviews — tracks records identified, screened, assessed, and included
> - **Inclusion/Exclusion Criteria**: peer-reviewed primary studies, defined population match, English language, date range — documented in the proposal
> - **Evidence Appraisal**: minimum 15–30 primary studies; appraised using JBI Critical Appraisal Checklists or CASP tools; evidence levels assigned (Johns Hopkins EBP Level of Evidence or GRADE)
> - **Evidence Synthesis Table**: presented as an appendix or in-chapter table — author, year, design, sample, intervention, outcomes, level of evidence, limitations
> - **Practice Gap Identification**: final paragraph of Chapter 2 — synthesises the evidence to identify what is NOT yet known or done in the specific clinical setting, justifying the capstone project

---

#### H2: Chapter 3: Methodology, IRB Plan, and Evaluation Design
> **EAV to cover**: project design, setting/sample, intervention, IRB determination, data collection, evaluation plan, Gantt chart, budget
> **Word count**: 420–500 words
> **Specific content**:
> - **Project Design**: state the design (e.g., "pre-post quality improvement project," "EBP implementation pilot study," "mixed-methods program evaluation") — must align with the PICOT
> - **Setting and Sample**: specific clinical unit (number of beds, patient census, staff count), patient population description, sample size justification (most DNP QI projects use convenience sampling with all eligible patients in the setting — power calculation not always required)
> - **Intervention Description**: step-by-step description of the practice change — specific enough for another practitioner to replicate; includes staff education plan, rollout schedule, and any tools or materials used
> - **IRB Determination**: QI vs human subjects research determination under 45 CFR 46; if QI: document basis for non-research classification; if human subjects: specify proposed review level (exempt, expedited, full board)
> - **Data Collection Tools**: name each instrument, describe reliability/validity (if validated), state who collects data, when, and how it is stored
> - **Evaluation Plan**: process measures (implementation fidelity — staff compliance rates) + outcome measures (clinical outcomes linked to PICOT); pre-implementation and post-implementation data collection schedule
> - **Statistical Analysis Plan**: identify specific tests (paired t-test, chi-square, descriptive statistics, SPC charts) — must align with the data type (continuous vs categorical) and sample size
> - **Timeline (Gantt Chart)**: 8–16 week implementation broken into phases (pre-implementation, implementation, post-implementation, data analysis, dissemination); presented as an appendix
> - **Budget**: materials, staff training time, technology costs; typically ≤$5,000 for most DNP QI projects

#### H2: How to Write a DNP Problem Statement That Passes Committee Review
> **EAV to cover**: problem statement structure, measurability requirements, common failures
> **Word count**: 240–300 words
> **Specific content**:
> - Must state: WHAT the problem is (specific clinical issue), WHERE it occurs (specific setting), HOW OFTEN or HOW SERIOUS (quantified — current rate or frequency), and WHY it matters (patient/system impact)
> - Strong example: "At [clinical setting], the rate of CAUTI per 1000 catheter days was 3.4 in the past 12 months — above the national benchmark of 1.2 (NHSN, 2023). This rate has not improved despite existing prevention protocols, resulting in extended patient stays, increased costs, and preventable patient morbidity."
> - Common failures: too vague ("patient safety is a problem"), no data to quantify the problem, no connection to the specific clinical setting, identical to published statistics without local contextualisation

#### H2: Common Reasons DNP Proposals Are Sent Back for Revision
> **EAV to cover**: top committee revision requests, how to avoid each
> **Word count**: 280–340 words
> **Specific content**:
> - PICOT too broad or unmeasurable — fix: narrow population + specify measurable outcome with baseline data
> - Literature review lacks synthesis — summaries of individual studies instead of synthesised evidence — fix: evidence synthesis table + gap identification paragraph
> - No theoretical framework or framework not justified — fix: name framework, describe its stages, map each stage to the project components
> - IRB section missing or insufficient — fix: complete QI determination + specify review level with rationale
> - Methodology misaligned with PICOT — fix: ensure project design (QI vs EBP vs program evaluation) matches the PICOT intervention and outcome type
> - Statistical analysis plan not specified — fix: name the exact tests with rationale based on data type and sample size

### FAQ SECTION

**Q: How long is a DNP capstone proposal?**
A DNP capstone proposal typically runs 40–80 pages of text, excluding references and appendices. Most proposals fall between 50 and 65 pages. The three-chapter structure (Background, Literature Review and Framework, Methodology) covers the problem, evidence base, and implementation plan in full detail. APA 7th edition student paper format is used throughout.

**Q: What does Chapter 2 of a DNP proposal include?**
Chapter 2 covers the theoretical or conceptual framework and the literature review. The framework section identifies the EBP framework guiding the project (such as the Iowa Model or PARIHS) and explains how each framework component maps to the capstone project stages. The literature review section documents the search methodology, evidence appraisal process, synthesis of 15–30 primary studies, and the identification of the practice gap justifying the project.

**Q: Why do DNP proposals get sent back for revision?**
The most common reasons are: a PICOT question that is too broad or lacks a measurable outcome, a literature review that summarises individual studies rather than synthesising evidence, a theoretical framework that is named but not applied to the project design, a missing or inadequate IRB determination section, and a statistical analysis plan that does not specify the exact tests to be used.

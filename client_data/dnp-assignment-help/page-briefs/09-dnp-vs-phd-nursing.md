## EAV Depth Map
**Primary Entity**: DNP vs PhD in Nursing

**Main Attributes**: degree focus, project type, methodology, clinical hours, capstone vs dissertation, AACN mandate

**Derived Attributes**:
- **DNP Focus**: practice doctorate — translates existing evidence into practice change; addresses clinical or healthcare system problems; does NOT generate new primary research knowledge
- **PhD Focus**: research doctorate — generates new knowledge through original primary research; contributes to nursing science through theoretical development, RCTs, qualitative inquiry, secondary data analysis
- **DNP Methodology**: EBP translation, QI methodology (PDSA/IHI), program evaluation — uses routinely collected data or publicly available datasets
- **PhD Methodology**: experimental design, RCT, grounded theory, phenomenology, ethnography, secondary analysis — generates new data through original data collection
- **Clinical Hours**: DNP requires 1000 post-baccalaureate clinical practice hours (AACN standard); PhD has no clinical hour requirement (may have teaching or research practicum)
- **AACN DNP Mandate**: AACN 2004 Position Statement called for DNP as entry level for advanced nursing practice by 2015; most NP, CRNA, CNM programs are moving or have moved to DNP level
- **DNP Capstone Output**: scholarly manuscript + practice change project or program evaluation report; local impact
- **PhD Dissertation Output**: 5-chapter dissertation with contribution to generalizable knowledge; peer-reviewed publication expected

### FAQ SECTION

**Q: Is a DNP harder than a PhD in nursing?**
DNP and PhD programs differ in focus rather than difficulty. DNP programs emphasise advanced clinical practice and system-level change, requiring sophisticated application of evidence-based practice frameworks, change management, and quality improvement methodology. PhD programs emphasise research design, statistics, and theoretical development. Both require doctoral-level scholarly work; the type of intellectual rigour required differs substantially.

**Q: Can a DNP become a nursing professor?**
Yes — many universities hire DNP-prepared faculty for clinical and practice-focused teaching roles. However, tenure-track research faculty positions at research-intensive universities typically require a PhD. The distinction matters for career planning: DNP prepares for clinical faculty and practitioner roles; PhD prepares for research faculty and principal investigator roles.

**Q: Does a DNP require a dissertation?**
No. A DNP requires a practice-focused capstone project, not a dissertation. The DNP capstone is a scholarly manuscript documenting the design, implementation, and evaluation of a practice change project. It is not a dissertation and does not require the generation of new primary research knowledge.

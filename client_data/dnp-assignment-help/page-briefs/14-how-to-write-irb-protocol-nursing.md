#### H2: What Is an IRB Protocol and When Do DNP Students Need One?
> IRB protocol = the formal document submitted to the Institutional Review Board; required when the project involves human subjects research; QI projects may need only a determination letter. Most DNP students need at minimum a QI determination request.

#### H2: The QI Determination Checklist: Research vs Quality Improvement Under 45 CFR 46
> The three-question checklist for QI vs research classification. What to do if uncertain — submit to the IRB for a formal determination letter rather than self-classifying. Why getting this wrong adds weeks to the timeline.

#### H2: IRB Protocol Sections: What Each Must Include
> Section-by-section walkthrough with content requirements for each section. Full list: title, PI, purpose, design, participants, recruitment, inclusion/exclusion, data collection procedures, risks/benefits, privacy plan, data security plan, consent procedures.

#### H2: HIPAA and Data Privacy in Nursing Practice Project Protocols
> PHI definition, de-identification options (Safe Harbor vs Expert Determination), Limited Data Set, when HIPAA is triggered vs not triggered (de-identified aggregate data = no HIPAA). Common oversight: storing patient data on a personal laptop without encryption = HIPAA violation.

#### H2: Writing the Risks and Benefits Section for a Low-Risk DNP Project
> Most DNP QI projects pose minimal to no direct risk to participants. How to write this section: acknowledge minimal risk, state patient benefit (improved care quality), state social benefit (evidence-based practice advancement). Do not overstate risk or benefit — committee reviewers notice inflated risk assessments.

#### H2: Common IRB Revision Requests for DNP Capstone Projects
> Top 5 revision causes: (1) Incomplete HIPAA section — missing de-identification plan; (2) Missing data security plan — who stores the data and how; (3) Missing or insufficient QI determination rationale; (4) Consent decision not justified — why waiver applies (or why consent is required); (5) Recruitment section omits how eligible participants will be identified.

### FAQ SECTION

**Q: Do I need to submit a full IRB application for my DNP QI project?**
Most DNP quality improvement projects qualify as non-research under 45 CFR 46 and require only a QI determination letter — not a full IRB application. A QI determination request is a shorter document that explains why the project meets the non-research criteria: it uses routinely collected data, its purpose is to improve care at a specific site, and the findings are not intended to contribute to generalizable knowledge.

**Q: What is the most common reason IRB protocols are sent back for revision?**
The most common revision requests for DNP capstone IRB protocols are: an incomplete HIPAA section that does not specify how patient data will be de-identified and stored, and a consent vs waiver decision that is not justified with reference to the federal criteria under 45 CFR 46.116(f).

## Writer Instructions Summary
1. 45 CFR 46 must be cited by name and number in the QI determination section.
2. The protocol sections must be presented as a numbered list or table — not prose.
3. HIPAA section must explain de-identification specifically (Safe Harbor vs Expert Determination) — not just say "protect patient privacy."
4. Common revisions section must be a numbered list with specific, actionable fixes — not vague warnings.

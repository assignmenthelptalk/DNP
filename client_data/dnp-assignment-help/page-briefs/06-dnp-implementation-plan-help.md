## EAV Depth Map
**Primary Entity**: DNP Capstone Implementation Plan

**Main Attributes**: implementation phases, change management framework, stakeholder mapping, staff education plan, process/outcome measures, Gantt chart, fidelity monitoring, sustainability plan

**Derived Attributes**:
- **Implementation Phases**: Pre-implementation (stakeholder analysis, needs assessment, staff education, policy draft, material preparation); Implementation (rollout schedule, data collection protocol, champion engagement, ongoing monitoring); Post-implementation (sustainability plan, policy adoption, dissemination)
- **Change Management Frameworks**: Kotter's 8-Step Model (most commonly used in DNP leadership/system-change projects); Lewin's 3-Stage Model (unfreeze-change-refreeze — simpler, good for unit-level QI); Rogers' Diffusion of Innovations (innovation adoption curve — good for EBP implementation projects)
- **Stakeholder Mapping**: sponsor (executive champion — CNO, department head); project champion (clinical unit leader who supports day-to-day rollout); frontline staff (nurses, MDs, APRNs who will implement); IT/informatics (if EHR change involved); administration (financial approval); patients/families (if patient-facing intervention)
- **Staff Education Plan**: content (what staff need to know and do differently), format (in-person, simulation, e-learning module, one-pager), timing (pre-implementation competency verification), return demonstration (for skill-based changes), competency assessment (pass/fail threshold)
- **Process Measures**: implementation fidelity — adherence/compliance rates (e.g., % of eligible patients receiving the intervention, % of staff completing education, % of checklists completed correctly)
- **Outcome Measures**: clinical outcomes linked to the PICOT O element — measured pre and post implementation
- **Fidelity Monitoring**: data collection protocol specifying who monitors, what tool is used, how often, and what threshold triggers corrective action
- **Gantt Chart**: visual timeline showing all project phases with start/end dates, responsible parties, and milestone markers — typically spans 8–16 weeks
- **Sustainability Plan**: how the practice change will continue after the formal project period — policy adoption, standard operating procedure update, ongoing competency assessment, leadership accountability structures

**Values per Attribute**:
- Kotter's 8 Steps: (1) Create urgency, (2) Build guiding coalition, (3) Form strategic vision, (4) Enlist volunteer army, (5) Enable action by removing barriers, (6) Generate short-term wins, (7) Sustain acceleration, (8) Institute change
- Lewin's 3 Stages: Unfreeze (raise awareness of need for change, reduce resistance) → Change (implement the new process) → Refreeze (embed change into standard practice, policy, culture)
- Rogers' Diffusion Curve: innovators (2.5%) → early adopters (13.5%) → early majority (34%) → late majority (34%) → laggards (16%) — implementation strategy tailored to each group
- Process measure example: "90% of eligible ICU patients will receive daily ABCDEF bundle assessment as documented in the EHR by week 4 of implementation"
- Fidelity monitoring threshold: if compliance falls below 80% at week 2, the project champion meets with unit manager to identify barriers

#### H2: Choosing a Change Management Framework: Kotter, Lewin, or Rogers?
> **EAV to cover**: framework comparison, when to use each, how to justify the selection
> **Word count**: 340–420 words
> **Specific content**:
> - **Kotter's 8-Step Change Model**: best for complex, system-level or multi-unit implementations with significant resistance expected; requires explicit stakeholder engagement at each step; most commonly used in Nurse Executive and healthcare leadership capstones; framework stages must be mapped to specific project activities
> - **Lewin's 3-Stage Model**: best for simpler, unit-level practice changes with moderate resistance; stages are straightforward to map — Unfreeze = staff education and awareness raising; Change = protocol implementation; Refreeze = policy adoption and standard embedding; used frequently in FNP and AGACNP capstones
> - **Rogers' Diffusion of Innovations**: best for EBP implementation projects where the challenge is adoption across a heterogeneous staff group; adoption curve informs the communication strategy for different adopter categories
> - Justification language: must explain WHY the selected framework fits this project — not just describe the framework. Example: "Kotter's 8-Step Model was selected because the proposed change requires unit-wide cultural shift and senior administrative support to overcome entrenched workflow habits."
> - Do not use multiple frameworks in the same project — select one and apply it consistently throughout the implementation plan and manuscript

---

#### H2: Stakeholder Analysis for Your DNP Capstone: Who Needs to Be On Board
> **EAV to cover**: stakeholder categories, mapping tools (power-interest grid), engagement strategies per category
> **Word count**: 280–340 words
> **Specific content**:
> - **Sponsor (Executive Champion)**: CNO, department head, or nursing director — provides organisational sanction and resource access; must be engaged early; key message: alignment with hospital quality goals and CMS/Joint Commission metrics
> - **Project Champion**: charge nurse, nurse manager, or unit educator on the target unit — day-to-day facilitator of the change; must be identified by name in the proposal
> - **Frontline Staff**: nurses, APRNs, physicians on the target unit — primary implementers; concerns are workload impact and workflow disruption; addressed via education and process simplification
> - **IT/Informatics Team**: required if EHR documentation change is involved; early engagement is critical — EHR build changes have long lead times
> - **Power-Interest Grid**: map each stakeholder on a 2×2 grid (high/low power × high/low interest) — High Power/High Interest = key player (manage closely); High Power/Low Interest = keep satisfied; Low Power/High Interest = keep informed; Low Power/Low Interest = monitor
> - Stakeholder resistance management: identify anticipated resistance sources (extra documentation burden, unfamiliarity with protocol) and document specific mitigation strategies in the implementation plan

#### H2: Writing the Staff Education Plan for Your DNP Intervention
> **EAV to cover**: education content, format options, timing, competency assessment
> **Word count**: 260–320 words
> **Specific content**:
> - **Content**: what staff need to know (evidence base for the intervention, why current practice is insufficient), what staff need to do differently (specific protocol steps), and how to document the change in the EHR
> - **Format options**: in-person classroom session (best for complex protocols), simulation or return demonstration (best for clinical skills), e-learning module (scalable, trackable — best for large units), pocket reference card or one-pager (reinforcement tool)
> - **Timing**: pre-implementation — education must be completed BEFORE the intervention begins; build 2–4 weeks of education time into the Gantt chart
> - **Competency verification**: required at most universities — pass/fail competency assessment (written quiz, return demonstration, chart audit); score threshold must be stated (e.g., "≥80% on a 10-item post-test")
> - **Attendance tracking**: sign-in sheets or LMS records; document that ≥80% of eligible staff completed education before implementation began — this is a process fidelity measure

#### H2: Process and Outcome Measures: Selecting the Right Data Points
> **EAV to cover**: process vs outcome measure distinction, examples, data collection schedule
> **Word count**: 260–320 words
> **Specific content**:
> - **Process measures**: measure implementation fidelity — HOW WELL the intervention is being delivered as planned. Examples: % of staff completing education, % of patients receiving the bundle, % of checklists completed correctly, % of EHR fields documented
> - **Outcome measures**: measure the CLINICAL EFFECT of the intervention — linked directly to the PICOT O element. Examples: CAUTI rate per 1000 catheter days, PHQ-9 screening completion rate, fall rate, HbA1c level, 30-day readmission rate
> - Collection schedule: baseline (pre-implementation, typically 4–8 weeks of historical data), midpoint check (week 4–6), final measurement (week 8–12 post-implementation)
> - Data sources: EHR audit reports, infection control dashboards, NDNQI, pharmacy records, patient satisfaction data — specify the exact report or database field name

#### H2: Building a DNP Project Timeline: Gantt Chart and Milestone Planning
> **EAV to cover**: Gantt chart structure, milestone categories, typical 8–12 week format
> **Word count**: 220–280 words
> **Specific content**:
> - Typical phases: Week 1–2: IRB approval, stakeholder meetings, materials preparation; Week 3–4: staff education; Week 5: implementation begins; Weeks 5–10: active implementation + weekly monitoring; Week 10–12: post-implementation data collection; Week 12–14: data analysis + manuscript writing; Week 14–16: dissemination/defense preparation
> - Gantt chart format: rows = tasks; columns = weeks; shaded bars indicate task duration; milestone markers (▼) at committee approval, IRB approval, implementation start, final data collection, defense
> - Tools: Microsoft Excel (most accessible), Smartsheet, Lucidchart, or hand-drawn table in APA format for submission
> - Must be included as an appendix in both the proposal and the final manuscript

#### H2: Fidelity Monitoring: Ensuring Your Intervention Is Delivered as Planned
> **EAV to cover**: fidelity definition, monitoring methods, threshold triggers, documentation
> **Word count**: 200–260 words
> **Specific content**:
> - Implementation fidelity = the degree to which the intervention was delivered as specified in the implementation plan
> - Monitoring methods: weekly chart audits (sample 5–10 records per week), direct observation (for skill-based protocols), EHR report (automated compliance tracking), staff self-report log
> - Threshold for corrective action: if compliance rate falls below a pre-specified threshold (e.g., <80%), the project champion holds a brief re-education session or posts a process reminder
> - Documentation: fidelity data becomes part of the results section — it demonstrates that any outcome changes (or lack thereof) can be attributed to the intervention actually being delivered

### FAQ SECTION

**Q: What change management framework should I use for my DNP capstone?**
The three most commonly used frameworks in DNP capstones are Kotter's 8-Step Change Model, Lewin's 3-Stage Model, and Rogers' Diffusion of Innovations. Kotter is best for complex, multi-level system changes. Lewin is best for straightforward unit-level practice changes. Rogers is best for EBP implementation projects where adoption across different staff types is the primary challenge. Select one framework and map every phase of your implementation to its stages — do not mix frameworks.

**Q: What process measures should I include in my DNP implementation plan?**
Process measures assess implementation fidelity — how well the intervention was delivered as planned. Common examples include the percentage of eligible patients who received the intervention, the percentage of staff who completed required education, the percentage of checklists or documentation fields completed correctly, and attendance rates at staff education sessions. Process measures are collected throughout the implementation period and reported alongside outcome measures in the results section.

**Q: How long should a DNP capstone implementation run?**
Most DNP capstone implementations run 8–12 weeks. This allows sufficient time for staff education, a full implementation period, and meaningful outcome measurement. Some EBP implementation projects run 12–16 weeks to allow for a pilot phase followed by a broader rollout. The implementation timeline must be reflected in the Gantt chart and must fit within the available semester schedule and clinical placement access period.

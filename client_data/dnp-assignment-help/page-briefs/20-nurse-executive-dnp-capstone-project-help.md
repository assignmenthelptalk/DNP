## EAV Depth Map
**Primary Entity**: Nurse Executive / Healthcare Leadership DNP Capstone

**Main Attributes**: capstone scope, common topics, frameworks, outcome measures, data sources, cost-benefit analysis, dissemination

**Derived Attributes**:
- **Capstone Scope**: organisational systems level — nursing unit, service line, or hospital-wide (not individual patient-level); addresses leadership development, workforce management, interprofessional collaboration, or strategic quality initiatives
- **High-Value Capstone Topics**:
  - Nurse turnover/retention program: structured onboarding, mentoring, flexible scheduling (outcome: turnover rate %, intention-to-stay scale)
  - Nurse residency program outcomes evaluation (program evaluation design)
  - Shared governance implementation or evaluation (staff engagement scores)
  - Interprofessional rounding protocol (HCAHPS scores, patient safety events)
  - Charge nurse leadership development program
  - Magnet readiness assessment and gap analysis
  - TeamSTEPPS communication training program (PSQ scores, near-miss rates, incident reports)
  - Compassion fatigue prevention program (ProQOL scale)
  - Nursing engagement survey-to-action initiative (Press Ganey NDNQI engagement scores)
  - Safe patient handling program implementation
- **Change Management Frameworks**: Kotter's 8-Step Change Model (most common), Lewin's Force Field Analysis, Rogers' Diffusion of Innovations, Transformational Leadership Theory (Bass & Avolio), Kouzes and Posner Leadership Practices Model
- **Outcome Measures**: nurse turnover rate (%), nurse vacancy rate (%), staff engagement/satisfaction score (Press Ganey, NRC Health, NDNQI), HCAHPS scores (patient experience), near-miss/adverse event rate, RN overtime hours per FTE, staff burnout/compassion fatigue (ProQOL-5), leadership competency assessment score
- **Data Sources**: HR system (turnover, vacancy, overtime, recruitment costs), Press Ganey or NRC Health survey platform, HCAHPS database, hospital incident reporting system (RL6, QuantrOS), NDNQI subscription data
- **Cost-Benefit Analysis**: leadership track capstones often require cost-benefit or return-on-investment analysis — calculate cost of nurse turnover (1.0–2.0× annual salary per RN), cost of the intervention (materials, staff time, training), and projected savings from reduced turnover or improved safety outcomes. This is a unique requirement not found in clinical track capstones.
- **Dissemination**: internal — presentation to CNO/CNE, executive leadership team, board of directors. External — nursing leadership conferences (AONE Annual Conference, Magnet Conference, Sigma Theta Tau).

### FAQ SECTION

**Q: What makes a nurse executive DNP capstone different from a clinical track capstone?**
Nurse Executive DNP capstones operate at the organisational systems level rather than the individual patient care level. They address workforce development, leadership programs, interprofessional communication, or strategic quality initiatives — not clinical protocols or disease-specific interventions. They typically use HR data (turnover, overtime, vacancy rates), staff satisfaction surveys (Press Ganey, NRC Health), or HCAHPS patient experience data rather than clinical outcome metrics.

**Q: Does a nurse executive DNP capstone require a cost-benefit analysis?**
Yes — most Nurse Executive and Healthcare Leadership DNP programs require a cost-benefit or return-on-investment analysis as part of the capstone proposal and manuscript. This analysis typically calculates the cost of the clinical problem (e.g., nurse turnover at 1.0–2.0× annual salary per position), the cost of the proposed intervention (materials, facilitator time, program development), and the projected financial benefit of the intervention (reduced turnover rate × cost savings). This section is a scored rubric element that distinguishes leadership track capstones from clinical track capstones.

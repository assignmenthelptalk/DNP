## EAV Depth Map
**Primary Entity**: CRNA DNP Capstone Project

**Main Attributes**: COA requirements, clinical settings, capstone topics, outcome metrics, quality databases, timeline considerations

**Derived Attributes**:
- **COA Requirements**: Council on Accreditation of Nurse Anesthesia Educational Programs (COA) — all COA-accredited DNP programs require an evidence-based practice project as the capstone; the DNP mandate for nurse anesthesia is fully implemented (all CRNA programs are at DNP level as of 2025); COA Standards specify scholarly project requirements including evidence synthesis, implementation, and dissemination
- **Clinical Settings**: operating room (OR), procedure suite, ambulatory surgery center (ASC), outpatient surgery center, labor and delivery (L&D) suite, pain management clinic, post-anesthesia care unit (PACU), pre-anesthesia evaluation clinic
- **High-Value Capstone Topics**:
  - PONV (postoperative nausea and vomiting) prevention protocol — multimodal prophylaxis bundle
  - ERAS (Enhanced Recovery After Surgery) protocol implementation — opioid-sparing anesthesia
  - Regional anesthesia utilisation protocol for opioid reduction (nerve blocks, neuraxial anesthesia)
  - Multimodal analgesia protocol (acetaminophen + NSAID + dexamethasone + local anesthetic)
  - Safe patient positioning protocol (prevention of peripheral nerve injury, pressure injuries)
  - Difficult airway management algorithm implementation (ASA DAS algorithm adherence)
  - PACU discharge criteria standardisation (modified Aldrete Score, MPADSS for ambulatory)
  - Oxygen therapy weaning protocol in PACU
  - Intraoperative blood pressure management protocol (MAP target maintenance)
  - Anesthesia provider hand hygiene compliance (prevention of anesthesia machine contamination)
- **Outcome Metrics**: PONV incidence rate (%), opioid consumption (morphine milligram equivalents — MME per case), PACU length of stay (minutes, mean), pain NRS/VAS score at PACU discharge, nerve block utilisation rate (%), Modified Aldrete Score at PACU discharge time points, perioperative adverse event rate
- **Quality Database**: NACOR (National Anesthesia Clinical Outcomes Registry) — AQI database for benchmarking anesthesia outcomes; AHRQ Patient Safety Indicators; SCIP (Surgical Care Improvement Project) measures
- **Timeline Considerations**: CRNA programs have tightly structured timelines; capstone proposals must be approved early in the program (typically during clinical year 1); implementation window is often constrained by OR access and anesthesia scheduling — 6–10 weeks typical

### FAQ SECTION

**Q: What is the best CRNA DNP capstone topic?**
The strongest CRNA DNP capstone topics address perioperative patient safety or quality improvement with measurable anesthesia-specific outcomes. Top options include PONV prevention protocol implementation (clear outcome: PONV incidence rate), ERAS protocol adoption for a specific surgical procedure (outcomes: MME, LOS, PACU time), and regional anesthesia utilisation to reduce opioid consumption (outcomes: nerve block rate, MME per case). The best topic aligns with a measurable gap at the student's clinical site and has a robust published evidence base.

**Q: What are the capstone requirements for CRNA DNP programs?**
The Council on Accreditation of Nurse Anesthesia Educational Programs (COA) requires all COA-accredited DNP programs to include a scholarly practice project that demonstrates evidence synthesis, implementation of an evidence-based practice change, and dissemination of findings. The project must address a perioperative or anesthesia practice improvement problem and meet doctoral-level scholarly standards. All nurse anesthesia programs in the United States are now required to be at the DNP level.

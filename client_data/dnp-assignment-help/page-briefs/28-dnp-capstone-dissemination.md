#### H2: Why Dissemination Is Required for DNP Capstone Completion
> AACN DNP Essentials Domain 4 (Scholarship for Nursing Practice) requires dissemination of DNP scholarly work. Most DNP programs require at minimum a poster or oral presentation as part of the capstone completion requirements. Some programs require a journal submission (not necessarily acceptance). Dissemination is not optional — it is a program requirement and a professional obligation.

#### H2: Designing an Academic Poster for a DNP Capstone Project
> Dimensions, sections, visual hierarchy, font sizes, colour scheme (institutional colours or professional palette), graphics vs text ratio (80% graphics/tables, 20% text), QR code to full manuscript. Layout options: tri-panel or single-sheet. Common template sources: BetterPoster format (Morrison, 2019 — central finding in large font, details around it) vs traditional column format.

#### H2: Conference Poster Dimensions and Section Structure for Nursing
> Standard dimensions by conference type. Section structure with recommended word counts: Title (project title + authors + affiliation — top banner), Background (problem and significance — 100 words), PICOT (1–2 sentences), Methods (project design, sample, intervention — 100 words), Results (primary outcomes with table or figure), Conclusions/Implications (3–5 bullet points), References (5–8 key citations only).

#### H2: Oral Presentation Structure for a DNP Capstone Defence
> 10–15 slide structure for a 10-minute presentation. Slide 1: Title + PICOT. Slides 2–3: Background and significance (one key statistic per slide). Slide 4: Evidence review (one synthesised finding per study design group). Slides 5–6: Methods (project design, sample, intervention timeline). Slides 7–8: Results (primary tables/figures). Slide 9: Discussion (key finding + connection to literature). Slide 10: Implications + Conclusion. Appendix slides: detailed tables for Q&A.

#### H2: Adapting Your DNP Capstone Manuscript for Journal Submission
> Word count target: 2,500–4,000 words for most nursing journals. What to cut: the full literature review (retain 5–8 key studies), appendices (reference in the text as "available from authors"), extended background sections. What to retain: the PICOT or purpose statement, concise methods, all primary results, full discussion. How to reframe the writing from a student scholarly manuscript to a practitioner-focused journal article — write for a clinical audience, not a committee.

#### H2: Target Journals for DNP EBP and QI Manuscripts
> Organised by track/topic: 2–3 journal options per track. Include: journal name, focus area, approximate word count limit, open access vs subscription. Recommend checking JANE (Journal/Author Name Estimator) for additional journal matching. Impact factor context: lower-impact clinical journals are more appropriate for DNP capstone manuscripts than high-impact research journals (NEJM, JAMA).

#### H2: Writing the Cover Letter and Responding to Peer Reviewers
> Cover letter: 3 paragraphs — (1) manuscript title + journal name + brief significance statement, (2) confirmation of original work + no competing interests + author information, (3) contact details. Responding to reviewers: response letter format (thank reviewers, address each point individually with specific revisions made and page/line numbers).

### FAQ SECTION

**Q: Is dissemination required to complete a DNP capstone?**
Yes — dissemination is required for DNP capstone completion at most programs. The AACN 2021 Essentials Domain 4 (Scholarship for Nursing Practice) specifies that DNP graduates must disseminate their scholarly work. Minimum requirements vary by program — some require a poster presentation only; others require an oral presentation; some require a journal submission. Students should confirm the specific dissemination requirement with their program handbook.

**Q: What journals publish DNP capstone project findings?**
The best journals for DNP capstone manuscripts depend on the project topic and specialisation track. For general QI and EBP projects: Journal of Nursing Care Quality and Worldviews on Evidence-Based Nursing are strong options. For primary care (FNP): Journal of the American Association of Nurse Practitioners. For acute care (AGACNP): AACN Advanced Critical Care. For nursing leadership: Journal of Nursing Administration. Select a journal whose scope matches the clinical topic and practice setting of the capstone.

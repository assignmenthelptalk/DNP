## EAV Depth Map
**Primary Entity**: IRB Proposal for DNP Capstone

**Main Attributes**: QI vs research determination, review levels, CITI training, protocol components, HIPAA, consent waivers, dual IRB, timeline

**Derived Attributes**:
- **QI vs Research Determination**: under 45 CFR 46, human subjects research = systematic investigation designed to contribute to generalizable knowledge. Most DNP QI projects qualify as QI (NOT research) because: they use routinely collected data, improve existing care processes, and findings are not intended for generalisation beyond the setting
- **Review Levels**: Exempt (Category 1–6 under 45 CFR 46.104 — minimal risk; no ongoing IRB oversight); Expedited (Category 1–9 — minimal risk but requires periodic review); Full Board (greater than minimal risk — full committee meeting required)
- **CITI Training**: Collaborative Institutional Training Initiative — Human Subjects Research course (Group 1 or 2 depending on involvement); GCP (Good Clinical Practice) required if any FDA-regulated intervention; must be current (within 3 years at most institutions)
- **IRB Protocol Components**: project title, PI information, purpose and significance, study design, participant identification, recruitment methods, inclusion/exclusion criteria, data collection procedures, risks and benefits, privacy/confidentiality plan, data security plan, informed consent procedures
- **HIPAA Considerations**: Protected Health Information (PHI) handling; de-identification standards (Safe Harbor: 18 identifiers removed, or Expert Determination); Limited Data Set (date elements + geographic data permitted with DUA); Business Associate Agreement required if third-party handles PHI
- **Informed Consent Waiver Criteria**: 45 CFR 46.116(f) — waiver criteria: research poses no more than minimal risk, waiver will not adversely affect rights/welfare, research could not practically be carried out without waiver, participants will be debriefed where appropriate. Most QI projects qualify.
- **Dual IRB**: when student's university IRB AND the clinical site's hospital IRB both require review; some hospital systems have reliance agreements with universities — check before submitting two separate applications
- **Timeline**: QI non-research determination: typically 1–2 weeks; Exempt review: 1–4 weeks; Expedited review: 4–8 weeks; Full board: 6–12 weeks

#### H2: Does Your DNP Capstone Project Need IRB Approval?
> **EAV to cover**: 45 CFR 46 definition of research, QI determination criteria, typical DNP project classifications
> **Word count**: 300–360 words
> **Specific content**:
> - Federal definition: human subjects research = systematic investigation designed to contribute to generalizable knowledge (45 CFR 46.102). QI projects = systematic investigation NOT designed to produce generalizable knowledge → typically NOT human subjects research
> - QI determination criteria: uses routinely collected clinical data (no new data collection), improves existing care processes at a specific site, findings intended for local quality improvement (not publication/generalisation), no individual identifiers collected
> - Most DNP QI projects: qualify as QI non-research → need only a QI determination letter from the IRB or a designated reviewer (not full IRB review)
> - Projects that DO require IRB: those collecting new data via surveys or interviews, testing a new drug or device, or intending to publish results as generalizable research
> - Common student error: automatically filing for Exempt review when the project qualifies as QI non-research — this adds unnecessary review time and committee burden

---

#### H2: QI vs Human Subjects Research: How IRBs Classify DNP Projects
> **EAV to cover**: classification criteria, examples of QI projects, examples of projects that cross into research
> **Word count**: 260–320 words
> **Specific content**:
> - **QI (non-research)**: CAUTI bundle implementation using EHR data; fall prevention protocol using existing incident reports; medication reconciliation process change using pharmacy and nursing records; staff education program using attendance and competency records
> - **Research requiring IRB**: patient satisfaction survey administered prospectively; new screening tool tested for validity; intervention with randomisation; qualitative interviews with patients or staff about their experience
> - **Gray area**: program evaluation that includes prospective surveys — may be expedited review; EBP implementation pilot with patient-reported outcomes collected prospectively — may be expedited review
> - The QI determination is made by the IRB or a designated reviewer — not the student. The student documents the rationale and submits a determination request.

---

#### H2: Exempt, Expedited, or Full Board Review: Which Does Your Project Need?
> **EAV to cover**: criteria for each review level with examples
> **Word count**: 280–340 words
> **Specific content**:
> - **Exempt Review (Categories 1–6 under 45 CFR 46.104)**:
>   - Category 2: research involving only surveys/interviews with no more than minimal risk
>   - Category 4: secondary analysis of existing data (de-identified records)
>   - Most DNP projects that require any IRB review qualify for Exempt — determined by the IRB, not self-designated
> - **Expedited Review (Categories 1–9 under 45 CFR 46.110)**:
>   - Used when the research involves more than minimal risk but qualifies for expedited categories
>   - Common DNP examples: prospective data collection with identifiable patient information; surveys with sensitive topics (mental health, substance use); chart reviews with limited PHI access
>   - Reviewed by IRB chair or designated reviewer — does not require full committee meeting
> - **Full Board Review**:
>   - Required when research involves greater than minimal risk, vulnerable populations, or deception
>   - Rarely required for DNP capstone projects unless the population includes children, prisoners, pregnant women in a research (not QI) context, or the intervention carries significant risk
>   - PMHNP capstone projects with vulnerable mental health populations may require expedited or full board even if the project is QI in intent

#### H2: Writing the IRB Protocol for a DNP Capstone: Section by Section
> **EAV to cover**: all protocol sections with specific content requirements
> **Word count**: 380–460 words
> **Specific content**:
> - **Project Title**: descriptive and accurate — must match the capstone proposal title
> - **Principal Investigator (PI)**: student is the PI; faculty advisor is listed as faculty supervisor; clinical site supervisor may be listed as co-investigator
> - **Purpose and Significance**: brief paragraph stating what the project will do and why it matters — can draw from Chapter 1 of the proposal
> - **Study Design**: state the design (QI, EBP implementation pilot, program evaluation) and methodology (pre-post comparison, retrospective chart review, prospective survey)
> - **Participant Identification and Recruitment**: how participants are identified (clinical census, patient list, staff roster) and how they are approached (verbal, written, flyer) — or state that no direct participant recruitment is required (for retrospective data use)
> - **Inclusion/Exclusion Criteria**: who is eligible and why; documented for IRB record
> - **Data Collection Procedures**: what data is collected, from where (EHR, paper records, survey), who collects it, when, and how it is stored
> - **Risks and Benefits**: articulate the risks (minimal to none for most DNP QI projects); state patient benefit (improved quality of care) and social benefit (evidence-based practice implementation)
> - **Privacy and Confidentiality Plan**: how PHI is protected; de-identification procedures; data access restrictions; storage location (encrypted, password-protected)
> - **Data Security Plan**: electronic data stored in encrypted drive or HIPAA-compliant server; paper data stored in locked file cabinet; data destroyed after [X] years per institutional policy
> - **Informed Consent Procedures**: consent form if required; or waiver of consent rationale citing 45 CFR 46.116(f) criteria if applicable

#### H2: HIPAA Compliance in DNP Capstone Projects
> **EAV to cover**: PHI definition, de-identification standards, Limited Data Set, BAA
> **Word count**: 240–300 words
> **Specific content**:
> - PHI = Protected Health Information: 18 identifiers including name, DOB, geographic data below state level, account numbers, phone numbers, dates (except year), Social Security number, IP address, biometric identifiers, photographs, MRN
> - **Safe Harbor de-identification**: remove all 18 identifiers — data is no longer PHI and HIPAA does not apply
> - **Expert Determination**: a qualified statistical expert certifies that re-identification risk is very small — acceptable alternative to Safe Harbor
> - **Limited Data Set**: removes direct identifiers but retains some indirect identifiers (dates, geographic units larger than city) — requires a Data Use Agreement (DUA)
> - For most DNP QI projects: de-identified aggregate data (counts, rates, percentages) is used — no PHI collected; HIPAA is not triggered
> - When PHI is accessed: the IRB protocol must include a HIPAA authorization or a waiver of HIPAA authorization (waiver criteria under 45 CFR 164.512(i))

#### H2: Consent vs Waiver of Consent: When DNP Projects Qualify for Waiver
> **EAV to cover**: consent requirements, waiver criteria, common waiver scenarios for DNP
> **Word count**: 220–280 words
> **Specific content**:
> - Consent required: when participants are identifiable, when data collection involves prospective interaction, when there is any more-than-minimal risk
> - Waiver criteria (45 CFR 46.116(f)): (1) research poses no more than minimal risk; (2) waiver will not adversely affect participants' rights and welfare; (3) research could not practically be carried out without waiver; (4) where appropriate, participants will be provided with additional pertinent information after participation
> - Most DNP QI projects qualify for waiver: they use routinely collected clinical data, pose no direct risk to patients, and obtaining individual consent from every patient whose chart is reviewed would be impractical
> - Staff survey or education evaluation: may require consent — typically a brief cover letter stating voluntary participation constitutes consent (implied consent)

#### H2: Hospital IRB and University IRB: Navigating Dual Review
> **EAV to cover**: when dual review applies, reliance agreements, sequencing
> **Word count**: 200–260 words
> **Specific content**:
> - When both apply: if the clinical site is not affiliated with the university, both the hospital IRB and the university IRB may require separate review
> - **IRB reliance agreement / authorization agreement**: many hospital systems have pre-negotiated agreements with universities that allow the university IRB to serve as the single IRB of record — eliminating the need for dual separate applications
> - Student must verify: whether their clinical site has a reliance agreement with their university BEFORE beginning the IRB application process
> - If no reliance agreement: submit both applications; the hospital IRB often reviews first; university IRB may accept the hospital approval via reciprocity
> - Timeline implication: dual review adds 4–8 weeks to the IRB timeline — must be factored into the Gantt chart

### FAQ SECTION

**Q: Does every DNP capstone project need IRB approval?**
No. Most DNP quality improvement projects qualify as non-research under the federal definition (45 CFR 46) and require only a QI determination letter rather than a full IRB review. A project qualifies as QI non-research when it uses routinely collected clinical data, improves care at a specific site, and the findings are not designed to contribute to generalizable knowledge. Projects that collect new data through surveys, test new interventions prospectively, or intend to publish generalizable findings typically require at least exempt IRB review.

**Q: What is the difference between exempt and expedited IRB review?**
Exempt review applies when the research poses minimal risk and falls into one of the six federally defined exempt categories — such as secondary analysis of existing de-identified data or minimal-risk surveys. Expedited review applies when the research involves more than minimal risk but still qualifies for one of nine expedited categories — such as prospective data collection with limited PHI access. Neither requires a full committee meeting; expedited review is conducted by the IRB chair or a designated reviewer.

**Q: Can my DNP project waive informed consent?**
Most DNP QI projects qualify for a waiver of informed consent when they use retrospectively collected clinical data, pose minimal to no risk to patients, and obtaining individual consent from all eligible patients is impractical. The waiver request must document that the four federal criteria under 45 CFR 46.116(f) are met. Staff surveys typically use implied consent via a cover letter rather than a signed consent form.

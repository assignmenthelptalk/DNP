## EAV Depth Map
**Primary Entity**: Literature Review Types for DNP Capstone

**Main Attributes**: systematic review definition, scoping review definition, integrative review definition, PRISMA versions, study count, selection criteria

**Derived Attributes**:
- **Systematic Review**: narrow PICO question, homogeneous study designs (preferably RCTs), protocol pre-registered (PROSPERO), PRISMA 2020 reporting, meta-analysis possible, Cochrane-level rigour — rarely feasible within DNP 1–2 semester timeline
- **Scoping Review**: broad topic mapping, answers "what is known about X" rather than answering a specific clinical question, includes all study designs, PRISMA-ScR (Scoping Review extension) reporting, no quality appraisal required, appropriate when evidence base is emerging or heterogeneous
- **Integrative Review**: combines quantitative and qualitative evidence on the same practice topic, PRISMA 2020 reporting (modified), includes both experimental and non-experimental studies, quality appraisal required (JBI or CASP), most appropriate for most DNP capstone topics
- **PRISMA 2020**: updated reporting standard for systematic and integrative reviews — 27-item checklist + flow diagram; used for reviews that synthesise and appraise evidence
- **PRISMA-ScR (2018)**: Scoping Review extension of PRISMA — 20-item checklist + flow diagram; used for scoping reviews that map evidence without quality appraisal
- **Study Count by Review Type**: systematic review: 10–50+ studies; integrative review: 15–30 primary studies (typical for DNP); scoping review: 20–50+ sources (less strict minimum)

> Definition, PRISMA 2020, PROSPERO pre-registration, meta-analysis possibility, rigour requirements. Honest statement: full systematic reviews are rarely feasible within a 1–2 semester DNP timeline because of the PROSPERO registration timeline, the homogeneous study design requirement, and the statistical pooling expectations.

#### H2: Scoping Review: Mapping What Is Known About a Clinical Topic
> Definition, PRISMA-ScR, when to use (emerging evidence base, broad topic, nursing informatics capstones), no quality appraisal required, 20–50+ sources typical.

#### H2: Integrative Review: Combining Quantitative and Qualitative Evidence
> Definition, Whittemore & Knafl (2005) foundational methodology, PRISMA 2020 modified reporting, quality appraisal required (JBI/CASP), appropriate for mixed-design evidence base, most common for DNP capstone proposals.

#### H2: PRISMA 2020 vs PRISMA-ScR: Reporting Standards by Review Type
> Side-by-side: which PRISMA version applies to which review type. Flow diagram differences. Checklist item counts. Where the PRISMA diagram is placed in the proposal.

#### H2: How Many Studies Are Enough for a DNP Literature Review?
> Integrative review: 15–30 primary studies standard for most DNP topics; fewer acceptable if evidence base is genuinely limited (must be documented in the proposal as a limitation); more is not inherently better. Systematic review: 10–50+. Scoping review: 20–50+.

#### H2: Which Review Type Is Right for Your DNP Project Design?
> Decision tree: Is the evidence base emerging/heterogeneous? → Scoping. Is there a narrow PICO with homogeneous RCTs? → Systematic (rarely chosen for DNP). Is the evidence mixed (quantitative + qualitative on one topic)? → Integrative. → Most DNP capstones use integrative review.

### FAQ SECTION

**Q: Can I call my DNP literature review a systematic review?**
Some DNP programs use the term "systematic review" loosely to describe any structured, methodologically documented literature review. In strict methodological terms, a true systematic review requires PROSPERO pre-registration, homogeneous study designs, and often meta-analysis — requirements that are rarely feasible within DNP capstone timelines. Most DNP programs accept an integrative review as meeting the "systematic" standard. Confirm with your faculty advisor which terminology your program uses.

**Q: What is the difference between PRISMA 2020 and PRISMA-ScR?**
PRISMA 2020 is the standard reporting checklist for systematic reviews and integrative reviews that synthesise and appraise evidence quality. PRISMA-ScR (2018 extension) is specifically for scoping reviews, which map evidence without conducting quality appraisal. DNP capstone proposals that use integrative or systematic review methodology use PRISMA 2020. Proposals that use scoping review methodology use PRISMA-ScR.

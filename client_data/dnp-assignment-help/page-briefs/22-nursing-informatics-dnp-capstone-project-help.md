## EAV Depth Map
**Primary Entity**: Nursing Informatics DNP Capstone

**Main Attributes**: informatics frameworks, capstone topics, outcome metrics, stakeholder complexity, evaluation tools, technology standards

**Derived Attributes**:
- **Informatics Frameworks**: Technology Acceptance Model (TAM — Davis, 1989: perceived usefulness + perceived ease of use as adoption determinants); TIGER Informatics Competency Collaborative (Technology Informatics Guiding Education Reform — basic computer competencies + information literacy + information management); ANIA (American Nursing Informatics Association) Practice Standards; Staggers & Nelson Informatics Competency Model
- **High-Value Capstone Topics**:
  - CDS alert fatigue reduction (alert override rates, clinician satisfaction)
  - EHR documentation burden reduction (documentation time, screen clicks, nursing notes length)
  - Telehealth implementation for chronic disease follow-up in primary care
  - Patient portal activation and engagement improvement (MyChart, FollowMyHealth activation rates)
  - Nursing documentation standardisation using NANDA-NIC-NOC terminologies
  - Predictive analytics/sepsis early warning alert implementation (SIRS/qSOFA alert accuracy, alert fatigue)
  - Barcode medication administration (BCMA) compliance improvement (scan rate, near-miss rates)
  - EHR-to-care transition communication tool implementation (discharge instruction completion rate, readmission rate)
  - Clinical decision support for preventive care reminders (mammography, colorectal screening rates)
  - Smart pump drug library update and compliance (IV pump alert compliance rate)
- **Outcome Metrics**: CDS alert override rate (%), documentation time (minutes per admission), patient portal activation rate (%), portal message response time (hours), BCMA scan compliance rate (%), near-miss medication error rate, telehealth visit completion rate (vs scheduled), sepsis alert positive predictive value (PPV), sepsis alert sensitivity
- **Stakeholder Complexity**: higher than clinical track capstones — requires engagement with IT/IS department (EHR build team), clinical informatics analysts, vendor representatives (Epic, Cerner/Oracle Health, Meditech), administration (IT governance committee), and frontline clinical staff. Early IT engagement is critical — EHR build changes have 6–12 week lead times at many institutions
- **Evaluation Tools**: System Usability Scale (SUS — 10-item; score ≥70 = acceptable, ≥85 = excellent); WAMMI (Website Analysis and Measurement Inventory — 20-item); TAM-based surveys (perceived usefulness, perceived ease of use scales); EHR system analytics reports (vendor-generated utilisation data)
- **Technology Standards**: HIMSS Electronic Medical Record Adoption Model (EMRAM — 7-stage); ONC Interoperability Standards; HL7 FHIR for data exchange; SAFER (Safety Assurance Factors for EHR Resilience) guides for EHR safety

#### H2: What Is a Nursing Informatics DNP Capstone Project?
> Define scope: technology-enabled care delivery improvement rather than clinical protocol change. Settings: any clinical setting with EHR or health IT infrastructure. Unique challenge: dual approval pathway — clinical site approval + IT governance committee approval (separate from IRB). Contrast with clinical track capstones: the intervention is a technology change, not a clinical process change.

#### H2: Common Nursing Informatics DNP Capstone Topics in EHR and Care Technology
> 8–10 topics with brief descriptions and primary outcome metric. Must include: CDS alert fatigue, EHR documentation burden, telehealth implementation, patient portal engagement, BCMA compliance, sepsis early warning, NANDA-NIC-NOC standardisation, discharge communication tool.

#### H2: Technology Acceptance Model and Informatics Frameworks for DNP Projects
> TAM: perceived usefulness + perceived ease of use → attitude → behavioral intention → actual use. How to apply TAM as the theoretical framework: pre-implementation survey measuring baseline perceived usefulness, post-implementation survey measuring change. TIGER competency framework for staff education component. Why TAM is the most commonly selected framework for informatics capstones.

#### H2: Measuring Outcomes in Informatics DNP Capstones: Alert Rates, Documentation Time, Errors
> Named metrics with measurement methods: CDS alert override rate (EHR analytics report — % alerts overridden without action), documentation time (EHR timestamp data — minutes from login to note completion), patient portal activation rate (vendor report — activated/total eligible patients %), BCMA scan rate (EHR compliance report), near-miss medication error rate (incident reporting system), SUS score (staff usability survey). How to extract outcome data from EHR analytics modules.

#### H2: Stakeholder Engagement in Informatics DNP Projects: IT, Clinical, and Vendor Partners
> Unique stakeholder landscape: IT/IS build team (EHR configuration changes require analyst time — must be requested 6–12 weeks before implementation), clinical informatics analyst (bridge between clinical and IT), vendor account representative (for Epic or Oracle Health product-specific questions), clinical governance committee (approval for EHR workflow changes). How to navigate the IT change management process — a formal IT change request is often required.

#### H2: Clinical Decision Support Implementation as a DNP Capstone Intervention
> CDS types: active (alerts, reminders — disruptive) vs passive (order sets, documentation templates — non-disruptive). Alert fatigue: when CDS override rate exceeds 70–80%, alerts lose clinical effectiveness. DNP capstone role: optimise existing CDS (reduce alert burden by improving specificity) OR implement new CDS (preventive care reminders, sepsis screening). Outcome metrics: alert override rate, alert sensitivity/specificity, time-to-action after alert.

### FAQ SECTION

**Q: What are the best nursing informatics DNP capstone topics?**
The strongest nursing informatics DNP capstone topics address a measurable health IT workflow problem with available technology utilisation data. Top options include CDS alert fatigue reduction (clear metric: override rate), patient portal activation improvement (clear metric: activation rate), BCMA compliance improvement (clear metric: scan rate), and EHR documentation burden reduction (clear metric: documentation time). The key advantage of informatics topics is that outcome data is often available directly from EHR analytics reports, simplifying data collection.

**Q: How is a nursing informatics DNP capstone different from other tracks?**
Nursing informatics DNP capstones involve technology as both the intervention and the data collection mechanism — the change is a technology workflow modification, and the outcome data is extracted from EHR analytics reports or vendor utilisation data. Stakeholder complexity is higher than clinical tracks because IT governance approval is required in addition to clinical and IRB approval. EHR build changes often have 6–12 week lead times, making early IT engagement critical to the project timeline.

### FAQ SECTION

**Q: What type of IRB review does a population health DNP project need?**
Population health DNP projects vary in IRB requirements depending on methodology. Projects using publicly available community data and de-identified aggregate EHR data typically qualify as QI non-research or exempt. Projects that involve prospective surveys of community members require at least expedited IRB review. Community-based participatory research (CBPR) that involves direct engagement with vulnerable community populations — including refugee populations, undocumented immigrants, or minors — may require full board IRB review. Students should consult both the university IRB and any community partner organisations' review requirements.

**Q: What is the PRAPARE tool and how is it used in a DNP capstone?**
PRAPARE (Protocol for Responding to and Assessing Patients' Assets, Risks, and Experiences) is a 21-item validated SDOH screening tool used in clinical settings to identify patients' social needs. In a DNP capstone, PRAPARE can serve as both an intervention (implementing routine SDOH screening) and an outcome measurement tool (tracking positive screen rates and referral completion rates). It is used in primary care, FQHCs, and community health centers.

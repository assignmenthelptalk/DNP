## EAV Depth Map

**Main Attributes**: student paper format, title page, headings, in-text citations, reference list, tables/figures, DOI, common errors

**Derived Attributes**:
- **Student Paper vs Professional Paper**: APA 7 introduced the distinction in the 7th edition (2020); student papers do NOT include a running head (unlike APA 6 where all papers had a running head); student papers have a simplified title page
- **Title Page (Student Paper)**: paper title (bold, centered, 3–4 lines below top of page), author name(s), institutional affiliation, course number and name, instructor name, assignment due date, page number (top right). No author note required.
- **Level Headings (5 levels)**:
  - Level 1: bold, centered, title case
  - Level 2: bold, left-aligned, title case
  - Level 3: bold italic, left-aligned, title case
  - Level 4: bold italic, indented (0.5"), title case, ends with period — text continues on same line
  - Level 5: italic, indented (0.5"), title case, ends with period — text continues on same line
- **In-Text Citations**: author-date format; one author: (Smith, 2022); two authors: (Smith & Jones, 2022); three or more authors: first citation and all subsequent citations use "et al.": (Smith et al., 2022); direct quote: page or paragraph number required: (Smith, 2022, p. 45)
- **Reference List**: alphabetical by first author last name; hanging indent (0.5"); DOI formatted as hyperlink: https://doi.org/xxxxx (not "doi: " prefix); URL without "Retrieved from" unless access date required (living documents)
- **Common APA 7 Errors in DNP Documents**: (1) including a running head (APA 6 habit — not required in student papers); (2) using "doi:" prefix instead of DOI URL format; (3) incorrect heading level hierarchy; (4) missing page number on direct quote; (5) UpToDate or ClinicalKey citations without correct APA format
- **Tables**: numbered above the table (Table 1, Table 2); bold title; note below table if needed; no vertical lines; no shading (or light grey only)
- **Figures**: numbered and titled below the figure (Figure 1. title); figure note below; used for charts, graphs, images, flow diagrams (PRISMA)

#### H2: Student Paper vs Professional Paper Format in APA 7
> The key change from APA 6 to APA 7: student papers no longer include a running head (the abbreviated title that appeared in the header of every APA 6 page). DNP proposals and manuscripts are student papers — no running head. Professional papers (submitted for publication) still include a running head. This is the #1 APA 6-to-7 transition error in DNP submissions.

#### H2: Title Page Requirements for DNP Proposals and Manuscripts
> Student paper title page elements in order: paper title (bold, centered, 3–4 lines from top), author name, institutional affiliation, course number and name, instructor name, assignment due date, page number (header, right-aligned). What NOT to include: author note, running head, abstract on title page.

#### H2: APA 7th Edition Level Headings for DNP Proposal Chapters
> All 5 heading levels described with formatting details and usage guidance. DNP proposals typically use Level 1 (chapter titles), Level 2 (major section headings), and Level 3 (sub-section headings). Levels 4 and 5 are used in the final manuscript for deep content organisation. Heading levels must be applied hierarchically — cannot skip from Level 1 to Level 3.

#### H2: In-Text Citations and Direct Quotes: APA 7 Rules for DNP Writing
> Author-date format for all sources. Rules: 1 author (Smith, 2022); 2 authors always cite both (Smith & Jones, 2022); 3+ authors always use et al. from first citation (Smith et al., 2022); no comma before "et al."; direct quote requires page number (p. 45) or paragraph number (para. 3) if no pagination. Paraphrase vs direct quote: prefer paraphrase; use direct quote only for exact wording matters.

#### H2: Reference List Format: Journal Articles, Books, Websites, and Databases
> Journal article format: Author, A. A., & Author, B. B. (Year). Title of article. *Journal Name*, *Volume*(Issue), page–page. https://doi.org/xxxxx
> Book format: Author, A. A. (Year). *Title of work: Capital letter also for subtitle*. Publisher.
> Government/organisation report: Author. (Year). *Title*. Organisation Name. URL
> UpToDate: Author, A. A. (Year, Month Day). Title of topic. In *UpToDate*. Retrieved Month Day, Year, from URL (UpToDate is a living document — retrieval date required)

#### H2: DOI vs URL: When and How to Include Each
> DOI: Digital Object Identifier — always include when available; format as hyperlink URL: https://doi.org/[DOI number] — NOT "doi: 10.XXXX". URL: include for sources without a DOI (websites, government documents, reports); no "Retrieved from" required unless the content may change over time (living documents). Do not include both DOI and URL.

#### H2: Tables and Figures in APA 7: Labels, Notes, and Placement
> Tables: Table number + bold title above; no vertical lines; note below starting with "Note." Figures (including run charts, SPC charts, PRISMA diagrams): Figure number + title below; note below. Placement in DNP documents: embedded in the results section OR listed after references at end of document per university requirement — check program handbook.

#### H2: Common APA 7 Errors in DNP Capstone Proposals and How to Fix Them
> Numbered list of top errors with fixes: (1) Running head included — remove entirely; (2) DOI formatted as "doi:10.xxxx" — change to "https://doi.org/10.xxxx"; (3) All heading levels bolded regardless of level — apply correct formatting per level; (4) "Retrieved from" on all URLs — remove unless source is a living document; (5) UpToDate/ClinicalKey cited without retrieval date — add "Retrieved [Month Day, Year], from [URL]".

### FAQ SECTION

**Q: Does a DNP capstone proposal or manuscript include a running head?**
No. Under APA 7th edition student paper format, running heads are not required for student papers — including DNP capstone proposals and final manuscripts. Running heads were required under APA 6th edition, and many students who learned APA 6 continue to include them out of habit. DNP proposals submitted with a running head will receive a formatting correction from most faculty advisors.

**Q: How do I cite UpToDate or ClinicalKey in APA 7th edition?**
UpToDate and ClinicalKey are considered "living documents" that update regularly — they require a retrieval date in the APA 7 reference. Format for UpToDate: Author, A. A. (Year, Month Day). Title of topic. In UpToDate. Retrieved [Month Day, Year], from [URL]. The retrieval date is required because the content may have changed since you accessed it.

## EAV Depth Map
**Primary Entity**: DNP Policy Change Capstone

**Main Attributes**: policy scope, frameworks, stakeholder engagement, evidence base, deliverables, organisational approval, dissemination

**Derived Attributes**:
- **Policy Scope**: unit-level protocol (e.g., foley removal protocol on one unit), departmental policy (e.g., medication reconciliation policy for all nursing units), organisational policy (hospital-wide policy — requires highest level of stakeholder engagement), state-level advocacy (legislative advocacy — rarely the focus of a DNP capstone; more common in public health/policy tracks)
- **Policy Analysis Frameworks**:
  - **Longest Policy Cycle**: policy issue identification → policy agenda setting → policy adoption → policy implementation → policy evaluation → policy modification. Most commonly used in nursing policy capstones.
  - **Kingdon's Multiple Streams Model**: three streams — problem stream (problem definition and recognition), policy stream (policy alternatives/proposals), political stream (political will, public opinion, leadership change) — converge at a "policy window" (opportunity for change). Used when the capstone addresses an advocacy or agenda-setting challenge.
  - **PDSA for Policy Testing**: adapted from QI methodology — plan the policy change, implement on a pilot basis, study outcomes, act to refine and adopt permanently.
- **Evidence Base Requirements**: literature review supporting the policy change (same methodology as EBP: 15–30 studies; PICOT for the policy question); evidence synthesis table; level of evidence ratings; gap identification showing why a policy change is needed
- **Stakeholder Engagement**: key informant interviews (gather expert perspectives), focus groups (gather staff perspectives), Delphi technique (consensus building for policy content among experts); stakeholder analysis (power-interest grid)
- **Policy Document Deliverables**: draft policy document (following the institutional policy template — numbered sections, purpose statement, scope, definitions, procedure, references, approval signatures), procedure document, quick reference/job aid, education materials
- **Organisational Approval Process**: policy approval pathway varies by institution — may require: unit manager approval → director of nursing → CNO → legal review → P&T committee (for medication policies) → board approval (for organisation-wide policies). The proposal must describe the actual approval pathway at the student's clinical site.
- **Dissemination**: internal (policy approval meeting, presentation to leadership), external (nursing professional organisations — ANA, specialty organisation position statements, state board of nursing, joint commission comment periods)

#### H2: What Is a DNP Policy Change Capstone Project?
> Define policy change capstone: developing, piloting, and evaluating a clinical, organisational, or advocacy policy change grounded in evidence. Distinguish from QI (process change) and EBP (practice change): policy change produces a formal policy document and navigates an institutional or governmental approval process. Most common at unit or organisational level in DNP capstones.

#### H2: Levels of Policy Change in DNP Capstones: Unit, Organisational, and State
> Unit-level: feasible within one semester; approval from unit manager + director of nursing; narrowest scope but most achievable for DNP timeline. Organisational: hospital-wide or system-wide policy; multiple approval layers; longer timeline. State/legislative: advocacy work, testimony, position statement — rare for DNP capstone but applicable in Population Health and Nurse Executive tracks. Recommend unit or departmental level for most DNP timelines.

#### H2: Policy Analysis Frameworks for DNP Students: Longest Cycle and Kingdon's Streams
> Longest Policy Cycle: 6 stages — issue identification, agenda setting, policy adoption, implementation, evaluation, modification. Most appropriate when the student is moving through all stages within the capstone. Kingdon's Multiple Streams: problem stream + policy stream + political stream converging at a policy window. Most appropriate for advocacy-focused capstones where the challenge is agenda-setting. Selection rationale: which framework stage does the capstone project primarily occupy?

#### H2: Building the Evidence Base for Your DNP Policy Change Proposal
> Literature review methodology same as EBP: PICOT for the policy question, integrative review of 15–30 studies, evidence synthesis table. Policy-specific evidence sources: Institute of Medicine (IOM)/National Academy of Medicine (NAM) reports, professional organisation position statements (ANA, AACN, specialty organisations), regulatory guidance (Joint Commission, CMS, state health department), national quality data (NHSN, NDNQI, AHRQ). These carry higher authority in policy arguments than individual peer-reviewed studies.

#### H2: Stakeholder Analysis and Engagement for Policy Change
> Power-interest grid for policy stakeholders. Policy-specific stakeholders: champion (usually unit manager or CNO), opponent identification (predict resistance sources), gatekeepers (legal, compliance, P&T committee for medication policies), potential allies (nursing staff, quality department, patient safety officer). Engagement methods: key informant interviews, Delphi consensus technique, focus groups, town hall meetings.

#### H2: Writing the Draft Policy Document as Your DNP Deliverable
> Structure of a clinical policy document: policy number, title, purpose statement, scope (who this policy applies to), definitions, policy statement (the actual rule), procedure (step-by-step instructions), responsibilities (roles identified), references (evidence citations), approval signatures (with titles and dates), revision history. Must follow the clinical site's existing policy template — do not create a unique format.

#### H2: Getting Policy Approved: Navigating Organisational Review Processes
> Approval pathway must be documented in Chapter 3 of the proposal. For medication policies: Pharmacy and Therapeutics (P&T) committee approval required. For nursing practice policies: CNO or VP of Nursing approval. For hospital-wide policies: may require executive leadership or board quality committee. Timeline implication: full organisational policy approval can take 3–6 months — the capstone may demonstrate stakeholder buy-in and committee submission rather than final approval, depending on the program timeline.

### FAQ SECTION

**Q: What is the best type of policy change for a DNP capstone project?**
For most DNP capstone timelines, unit-level or departmental-level policy changes are the most feasible. These require approval from the unit manager and director of nursing — a 4–8 week approval process. Hospital-wide or system-level policy changes require multiple approval layers (CNO, legal, executive leadership, possibly the board) and can take 3–6 months — too long for most DNP capstone implementation schedules.

**Q: What frameworks are used in DNP policy change capstone projects?**
The two most commonly used frameworks are Longest's Policy Cycle (for projects that move through the full policy development and implementation process) and Kingdon's Multiple Streams Model (for advocacy-focused projects where the challenge is creating a policy window for change). The selection should be justified based on which framework stage the capstone project primarily occupies.

#### H2: What Is CMI Assignment Tutoring and Who Is It For? — Major Query Need: Service definition
> **Context vector**: Defining tutoring as guided support — student-led, tutor-directed
> **Main entity + attributes to cover**:
> - Tutoring = expert guidance for the student's own writing, not a replacement for it
> - Who needs it: students who want to write themselves but lack academic writing experience; students on resubmission who need targeted help; students stuck on structure or command verb application
> - Alternative to the full writing service — both options available
> - Works at every stage: planning (before writing), mid-draft (structure review), post-draft (feedback before submission), resubmission (after referral)
> **Predicate to use**: "guides" / "supports" / "reviews" / "coaches"
> **First word of first paragraph**: "CMI assignment tutoring guides you through..."
> **Writer note**: Make the distinction explicit and positive — tutoring is not the "lesser" option. Many students prefer to retain authorship. Frame tutoring as empowerment: "develop your skills and submit work you're proud of."
> **Word count target**: 240–300 words
> **Internal links**: Link to writing service: "If you need the assignment fully written for you, see our CMI assignment writing service" → `/cmi-assignment-writing-service/`

##### H3: Who Benefits Most from CMI Assignment Tutoring?
> **Attribute + Value**:
> - Students who can write but need structure: know their topic but unsure how to present it academically
> - Resubmission students: have received referral feedback and need targeted guidance on what to fix
> - Students wanting career development: studying CMI for professional growth, not just the certificate
> - First-time Level 5 Diploma students: transitioning from basic report writing to analytical academic writing

#### H2: What CMI Assignment Tutoring Includes — Major Query Need: Session content and format
> **Context vector**: Tutoring session types and deliverables — what the student actually receives
> **Main entity + attributes to cover**:
> - Assignment planning session: tutor reviews unit brief → identifies Learning Outcomes → proposes structure → student gets a clear roadmap before writing
> - Draft feedback: student submits their draft → tutor provides written critique — command verb depth, argument structure, referencing, LO coverage mapped against Assessment Criteria
> - Resubmission coaching: assessor feedback reviewed → specific missed criteria identified → tutor prescribes exactly what to rewrite and why
> - Command verb coaching: specific deep-dive on how to Analyse, Evaluate, Critically Analyse at Level 5 or 7 depth
> **Predicate to use**: "reviews" / "provides" / "identifies" / "coaches"
> **First word of first paragraph**: "CMI assignment tutoring is available in three formats..."
> **Writer note**: Three session types = three clear offers. Use a card or tab format in the page design if possible. Each type should have its own description.
> **Word count target**: 300–360 words
> **Internal links**: Link to command verb guide: "understanding CMI command verbs" → `/guides/cmi-command-verbs-explained/`

##### H3: Assignment Planning — Structuring Before You Write
> **Attribute + Value**: Student sends unit brief → tutor maps Learning Outcomes → proposes assignment structure (heading outline with argument direction per section) → student receives a written plan → student writes to plan
> **Writer note**: This is the lowest-risk entry-point for new tutoring clients. Name the deliverable clearly: "written assignment outline with section-by-section direction."

##### H3: Draft Review and Feedback — Improving What You've Written
> **Attribute + Value**: Student submits draft → tutor reviews against Assessment Criteria → provides written feedback identifying: sections where LOs are not met, areas where command verb depth is insufficient, referencing gaps, structural improvements → student revises independently
> **Writer note**: Name the specific feedback categories — LO coverage, command verb depth, referencing, structure. This specificity builds trust.

##### H3: Resubmission Coaching — Getting Past Assessor Referral Feedback
> **Attribute + Value**: Student shares assessor's referral comments → tutor identifies exactly which LOs were missed → provides targeted correction guidance → student rewrites with precision → addresses referral without rewriting the entire assignment
> **Writer note**: "Referral" is the CMI term for failing and needing to resubmit. Students who have just received referral feedback are in urgent need — position resubmission coaching as the fastest path to a pass.

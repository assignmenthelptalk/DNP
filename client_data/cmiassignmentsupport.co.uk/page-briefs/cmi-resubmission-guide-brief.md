## FAQ Items (for FAQPage schema)

1. What does Refer mean on a CMI assignment? → Refer is CMI's grade for an assignment that has not yet met the required standard. It is not a fail in the traditional sense — it means the work needs to be revised and resubmitted. You will receive written assessor feedback explaining which assessment criteria were not met.
2. Can I get Merit or Distinction on a resubmission? → Policy varies by centre. Most CMI centres cap resubmissions at Pass grade — meaning even if your revised submission would have achieved Merit, it is graded as Pass. Check with your centre before resubmitting. This is one reason to aim for Merit or Distinction on first submission.
3. Why do most CMI assignments get referred? → The most common reasons are: failing to meet the command verb (describing instead of evaluating; evaluating instead of critically analysing); not addressing all assessment criteria; insufficient or non-academic sourcing; applying frameworks as recipes rather than critically engaging with them; and AC3 being underdeveloped.
4. How do I read CMI assessor feedback? → Assessor feedback identifies the specific assessment criteria that were not met, with comments on why. Common phrases include "you have described rather than evaluated," "your recommendation is not SMART," "insufficient academic evidence," and "AC3 requires further development." Each comment maps to a specific gap — address each one explicitly in your resubmission.
5. How long do I have to resubmit a CMI assignment? → Resubmission deadlines are set by your CMI-registered centre, not by CMI centrally. Most centres allow 4–8 weeks from the date of feedback. Check your centre's resubmission policy immediately on receiving your feedback — missing the deadline may require starting the unit again.
6. Can you help me with a CMI resubmission? → Yes — resubmission is one of our most common requests. We read your assessor feedback, identify exactly what needs to change, and either guide you through fixing it yourself or rewrite the specific sections that are not meeting the assessment criteria. WhatsApp us with your feedback document and we'll tell you exactly what the submission needs.

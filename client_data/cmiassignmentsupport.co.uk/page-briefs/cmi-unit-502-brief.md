**Key theories:**
- Tuckman's 5-stage team development model (Forming, Storming, Norming, Performing, Adjourning)
- Belbin's 9 team roles (Plant, Resource Investigator, Co-ordinator, Shaper, Monitor Evaluator, Teamworker, Implementer, Completer Finisher, Specialist)
- Katzenbach and Smith — high-performance team definition and characteristics
- Psychological safety — Amy Edmondson — the team climate required for peak performance
- Situational leadership applied to individuals within a team (Hersey and Blanchard — link to 501)

**Common failure points:**
1. Narrating Tuckman's stages as a story rather than evaluating them as a development framework
2. Listing Belbin roles without connecting them to team composition and performance outcomes
3. No criteria established before evaluating team development approaches
4. Missing the individual vs team distinction — AC3 requires approaches for managing individuals within the team context, separate from managing the team as a whole
5. Executive summary written as an introduction

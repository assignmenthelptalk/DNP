**Key frameworks:**
- Resource planning — human, physical, financial, information, and technological resources; capacity planning; resource allocation models; the connection between strategic objectives and resource allocation decisions
- Budget types — incremental budgeting (build on last year), zero-based budgeting (justify every line from scratch), activity-based budgeting (align budget to activities and outputs), rolling budgets (continuous 12-month horizon); each with strengths and limitations
- Budget variance analysis — favourable vs adverse variance; volume variance, price variance, efficiency variance; the management response to variance — investigating root cause vs accepting expected variance
- Cost-benefit analysis (CBA) — total costs vs total benefits; limitations: how do you quantify intangible benefits? how do you handle uncertainty? discounting future costs and benefits (net present value concept); CBA vs cost-effectiveness analysis (CEA) when benefits are non-monetisable
- Financial statements for managers — income statement (P&L), balance sheet, cash flow statement; what each tells a manager and what it does not; working capital management
- Return on Investment (ROI) — calculating ROI for resource decisions; limitations of ROI as a single metric; investment appraisal (payback period, IRR, NPV) at management level
- Resource efficiency — value for money (economy, efficiency, effectiveness — the three E's from public sector); waste identification (lean principles applied to resource management); sustainability of resource use
- Accountability and governance — budget holder responsibility; delegated financial authority; internal controls; audit trail requirements; fraud prevention

**Common failure points:**
1. AC2 describing what a budget is rather than Evaluating approaches — the difference between incremental and zero-based budgeting needs to be Evaluated (in what contexts is each appropriate?) not described
2. Financial jargon used without management application — mentioning NPV or IRR without explaining how they guide resource decisions
3. AC3 reduced to "financial information helps managers decide" — Evaluating the use of financial information means examining its limitations (historical data, forward-looking uncertainty, non-financial factors excluded) as well as its uses
4. No engagement with resource constraints in public sector or NHS — many CMI students manage resources in organisations where ROI is not the primary metric; the three E's (economy, efficiency, effectiveness) and value for money framework are directly applicable
5. Resource management treated as purely operational — at Level 5, resource decisions connect to strategic priorities; the Evaluate command verb requires examining how resource allocation reflects and enables organisational strategy

## FAQ Items (for FAQPage schema)

1. What is CMI Unit 506? → Managing Resources and Finance — Evaluating approaches to resource planning and management, budget setting and control, and the use of financial information in management decision-making at Level 5
2. What format does CMI 506 take? → Management report, 3,500–4,500 words, 10–12 sources with Harvard referencing
3. What is the difference between incremental and zero-based budgeting in CMI 506? → Incremental budgeting builds on the previous year's budget with adjustments; zero-based budgeting requires every budget line to be justified from scratch each cycle. ZBB is more resource-intensive but eliminates historical inefficiencies; incremental budgeting is faster but perpetuates past allocation patterns regardless of current priorities.
4. What is variance analysis in CMI 506? → The comparison of actual financial performance against the budgeted figures, identifying favourable (better than budget) and adverse (worse than budget) variances. Managers use variance analysis to investigate performance gaps, identify root causes, and take corrective action.
5. What does AC3 require in CMI 506? → Evaluate the use of financial information in management decision-making — examining how financial data (P&L, variance reports, cost-benefit analysis, ROI) informs decisions, but also its limitations: historical rather than predictive, excludes non-financial factors, subject to manipulation or misinterpretation.
6. Can you help with CMI 506 resubmission? → Yes — most common gaps are AC2 describing budget types rather than Evaluating when each is appropriate, and AC3 treating financial information as straightforwardly useful without examining its limitations.

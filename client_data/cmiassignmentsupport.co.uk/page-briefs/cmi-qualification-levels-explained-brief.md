## FAQ Items (for FAQPage schema)

1. What are the CMI qualification levels? → CMI offers qualifications from Level 2 to Level 8 on the Regulated Qualifications Framework. The most common are Level 3 (First Line Management), Level 5 (Management and Leadership Diploma), Level 6 (Professional Management), and Level 7 (Strategic Management and Leadership — equivalent to Master's level).
2. Which CMI level should I do? → Your level should match your management experience and responsibilities: Level 3 for team leaders and junior managers; Level 5 for experienced managers leading teams or departments; Level 7 for senior managers with strategic responsibility or those aspiring to board-level roles.
3. What is CMI Level 5 equivalent to? → CMI Level 5 is equivalent to a Higher National Diploma (HND) or the second year of an undergraduate degree on the Regulated Qualifications Framework. It is not a degree but is a professionally recognised management qualification.
4. What is CMI Level 7 equivalent to? → CMI Level 7 is equivalent to a Master's degree on the Regulated Qualifications Framework. The Diploma in Strategic Management and Leadership is the full Level 7 qualification.
5. What is the difference between a CMI Award, Certificate, and Diploma? → Award: 1–2 units, the smallest qualification, suitable for targeted professional development. Certificate: 2–3 units, a medium-sized qualification. Diploma: the full qualification, covering the most units and providing the most comprehensive professional recognition. The Diploma is the most valuable for career development and is required for the Chartered Manager (CMgr) pathway.
6. Can I do CMI Level 7 without Level 5? → Yes — CMI level entry requirements are based on your management experience and role, not on prior CMI qualifications. Many senior managers enter at Level 7 without having completed Level 5. Your CMI centre will assess your suitability for the level based on your professional background.

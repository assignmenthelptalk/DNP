##### H3: Critically Analyse at Level 7 — What It Requires at Strategic Depth
> **Attribute + Value**:
> - Critically Analyse at Level 7 → break down strategic concepts into components → evaluate each component against empirical evidence → challenge the dominant theory with counter-evidence → consider sector-specific applicability → synthesise original strategic position
> - Must cite conflicting research → cannot just apply one theory without acknowledging competing frameworks
> - Example: "Critically Analyse transformational leadership in a strategic context" requires engaging with critiques of transformational leadership (e.g., the leader-centric bias, applicability in crisis management) — not just describing Bass and Avolio's model
> **Writer note**: Level 7 "Critically Analyse" is the most demanding command verb in CMI. Students who achieve distinction demonstrate original thinking — a synthesis of evidence that goes beyond reviewing what others have said. This H3 must make that distinction clear.

##### H3: Strategic Papers vs Management Reports — Level 7 vs Level 5 Format
> **Attribute + Value**:
> - Level 5 management report → operational scope → team or department level → Evaluate with criteria → SMART recommendations for a specific management scenario
> - Level 7 strategic paper → organisational or sector scope → board or executive level → Critically Analyse with competing evidence → strategic options with organisational alignment → implications for stakeholders, governance, and long-term performance
> **Writer note**: Students sometimes approach Level 7 assignments as extended Level 5 reports. This misunderstanding costs them marks. The strategic paper requires a fundamentally different frame — demonstrate this difference explicitly.

**Key theories and frameworks:**
- Armstrong's Performance Management Cycle — planning, acting, monitoring, reviewing; the integrated cycle that connects goal-setting to appraisal to development
- Appraisal approaches — annual appraisal vs continuous performance management; 360-degree feedback; manager-led vs self-assessment appraisal
- SMART objectives and performance standards — the technical framework for defining performance expectations (connects to CMI 503)
- Performance conversations — coaching approaches (GROW model) applied to performance discussion; distinguishing development conversations from formal performance management
- Managing underperformance — the sequence from informal performance conversation to formal PIP (Performance Improvement Plan) to disciplinary; ACAS Code of Practice on disciplinary and grievance; informal vs formal approaches
- Kirkpatrick's Four-Level Evaluation Model — for evaluating learning and development outcomes connected to performance improvement

**Common failure points:**
1. Conflating appraisal with performance management — appraisal is one component of a performance management system, not synonymous with it
2. Missing AC3 (managing underperformance) — students often cover AC1 and AC2 in depth but give AC3 only one paragraph
3. Describing appraisal formats without evaluating their effectiveness against criteria
4. No organisational context — performance management approaches must be evaluated in relation to a defined management context
5. Legal framework for managing underperformance absent — ACAS guidance and basic employment law context is expected at Level 5 when discussing formal underperformance processes

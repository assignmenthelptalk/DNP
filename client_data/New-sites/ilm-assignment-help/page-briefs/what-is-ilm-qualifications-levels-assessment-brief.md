## EAV Depth Map
**Primary Entity**: ILM (Institute of Leadership and Management)

**Main Attributes**: organisational identity, qualification range, qualification levels, assessment model, employer recognition, professional membership pathways

**Derived Attributes**:
- **Organisational Identity**: ILM = Institute of Leadership and Management. City & Guilds Group company. Ofqual-regulated awarding organisation. Founded 1947. Specialist in leadership and management qualifications. Not a regulatory body — awards qualifications.
- **Qualification Range**: Leadership and Management pathway (Levels 2, 3, 4, 5, 6, 7); Coaching and Mentoring pathway (Levels 3, 5, 7); each level available as Award, Certificate, or Diploma.
- **Assessment Model**: Entirely work-based — no exams. Candidates evidence learning from own workplace. Criterion-referenced: every assessment criterion must be individually addressed. Missing one criterion = referral regardless of submission quality elsewhere.
- **Level Positioning**: Level 2 = entry-level team skills; Level 3 = team leader/supervisor; Level 4 = junior middle manager; Level 5 = middle manager (most enrolled level); Level 6 = senior manager; Level 7 = senior leader/director (postgraduate equivalent).
- **Employer Recognition**: NHS, civil service, major corporates, SMEs. City & Guilds Group brand recognition globally. Professional development standard for employer-sponsored management training.
- **Professional Membership**: ILM membership grades — Affiliate, Associate, Member, Fellow. Recognition from CMI for credit towards Chartered Manager status at senior levels.

**Values per Attribute**:
- Founded: 1947
- Parent organisation: City & Guilds Group
- Regulatory status: Ofqual-regulated
- Levels available: 2, 3, 4, 5, 6, 7
- Assessment type: Work-based only — no exams
- Most enrolled level: Level 5 (middle managers)
- Qualification types: Award, Certificate, Diploma
- Pathways: Leadership & Management; Coaching & Mentoring

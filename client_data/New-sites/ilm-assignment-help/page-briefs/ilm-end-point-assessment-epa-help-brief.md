## EAV Depth Map
**Primary Entity**: ILM End Point Assessment (EPA)

**Derived Attributes**:
- **EPA Components**: Professional discussion (structured interview — 60–90 minutes — with independent end point assessor not connected to the training provider). Some standards also include a work-based project report or presentation. The professional discussion is the primary EPA component for most management standards.
- **Independent Assessor**: Not the employer, not the training provider, not the ILM assessor. An independent professional appointed by the EPA organisation (often ILM itself or another approved EPA provider). Their role is to assess whether the apprentice meets the apprenticeship standard — they are not assessing the ILM qualification separately.
- **Portfolio Review**: The independent assessor reviews the portfolio during or before the professional discussion. The portfolio must be comprehensive and signed off at gateway. The professional discussion questions are drawn from portfolio evidence — gaps in the portfolio become gaps in the discussion.
- **Professional Discussion Format**: Questions are structured around KSBs (Knowledge, Skills, Behaviours). The assessor asks the apprentice to explain, justify, and reflect on specific portfolio entries. Distinction requires going beyond describing what happened to evaluating impact, demonstrating strategic awareness, and showing independent judgement.
- **Gateway Requirements**: Must complete all ILM units, receive employer confirmation, achieve English and maths requirements, have portfolio signed off by training provider, and receive training provider sign-off on readiness. Gateway is a formal milestone — EPA cannot proceed without it.
- **Distinction Criteria**: Varies by standard but generally: demonstrates consistent high performance across KSBs; shows wider organisational impact beyond immediate role; provides evidence of independent judgement in complex, ambiguous management situations; demonstrates commitment to continuous professional development.
- **Common EPA Failure Causes**: Portfolio evidence too vague to support specific KSBs; unable to expand on portfolio entries under questioning; lack of critical self-reflection in responses; insufficient evidence of strategic awareness at Level 5/7.

**Values per Attribute**:
- Professional discussion duration: 60–90 minutes (standard-dependent)
- Assessor type: Independent (not trainer or employer)
- EPA grading: Pass/Distinction (most management standards)
- Gateway: formal milestone requiring multiple sign-offs
- Portfolio: must be comprehensive before gateway
- Distinction requirement: wider organisational impact + independent judgement

## EAV Depth Map
**Primary Entity**: ILM qualification level progression

**Derived Attributes**:
- **Level 2**: RQF Level 2. Entry-level team skills — team members moving toward supervisory responsibility. Award format typical. Basic team communication, problem-solving, personal development. Short assignments (~500–800 words).
- **Level 3**: RQF Level 3 (A-Level equivalent). Team leaders, supervisors, first-line managers. Award, Certificate, Diploma. Operational focus — applying motivational theory, managing team conflict, planning work. Assignments 800–1,500 words. Harvard referencing not typically required.
- **Level 4**: RQF Level 4. Junior middle managers — managing departments, implementing change. Bridge level between operational (L3) and strategic (L5). Assignments 1,200–2,000 words. Some Harvard referencing expected.
- **Level 5**: RQF Level 5 (degree-level credit). Middle managers, operations managers, project managers. Most enrolled ILM level. Critical analysis of management theory required. Harvard referencing mandatory. Assignments 2,000–3,500 words.
- **Level 6**: RQF Level 6. Senior managers — uncommon qualification level. Very limited uptake compared to L5 and L7.
- **Level 7**: RQF Level 7 (Master's-level credit). Senior leaders, directors, NHS executives. Postgraduate academic standard. Peer-reviewed journal citation expected. Strategic-level analysis spanning whole organisation. Assignments 3,000–5,000 words.
- **Assignment Depth Escalation**: L3 = describe and apply; L5 = critically analyse; L7 = critically synthesise across multiple strategic frameworks.

**Values per Attribute**:
- Level 2 word count: ~500–800 words
- Level 3 word count: ~800–1,500 words
- Level 4 word count: ~1,200–2,000 words
- Level 5 word count: ~2,000–3,500 words
- Level 7 word count: ~3,000–5,000 words
- Harvard referencing: Not required L2/L3; expected L4; mandatory L5+
- Most enrolled level: Level 5

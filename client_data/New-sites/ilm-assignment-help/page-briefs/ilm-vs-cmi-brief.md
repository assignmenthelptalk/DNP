## EAV Depth Map
**Primary Entity**: ILM and CMI (compared as a pair)

**Derived Attributes**:
- **ILM Assessment**: Entirely work-based — no exams. All evidence from own workplace practice. Criterion-referenced units. Purely portfolio-based.
- **CMI Assessment**: Combination of work-based assignments and some written assessments. More academically oriented at higher levels. CMI Level 7 involves academic essays and research projects alongside management assignments.
- **ILM Professional Outcome**: ILM membership grades. At higher levels, recognition toward CMI's Chartered Manager (CMgr) status. Fellowship of ILM at senior levels.
- **CMI Professional Outcome**: CMI membership grades (Member, Fellow). Direct route to Chartered Manager (CMgr) designation — the UK standard for senior management professional recognition. CMgr is the CMI's primary differentiator.
- **Learner Profile (ILM)**: Better suited to practitioners who want to evidence current management practice. Strong employer-sponsored take-up. Work evidence is the primary demonstrator of competence.
- **Learner Profile (CMI)**: Better suited to candidates who want Chartered Manager status or who have a preference for academic writing alongside management practice. CMI Diploma at Level 7 is a direct pathway to CMgr without further assessment.
- **Level Equivalence**: ILM Level 3 ≈ CMI Level 3; ILM Level 5 ≈ CMI Level 5; ILM Level 7 ≈ CMI Level 7. RQF levels are equivalent — both are Ofqual-regulated.
- **Assignment Depth**: At Level 5 and 7, both require critical analysis of management theory applied to real practice. CMI typically has longer word counts per assignment. ILM focuses more tightly on criterion-specific evidence; CMI assignments are often more essay-structured with broader analytical scope.

**Values per Attribute**:
- ILM parent body: City & Guilds Group
- CMI parent body: Chartered Management Institute (independent)
- Both: Ofqual-regulated
- ILM flagship credential: ILM membership + qualification
- CMI flagship credential: Chartered Manager (CMgr)
- ILM assessment: Work-based evidence only, no exams
- CMI assessment: Work-based + academic assignments (level-dependent)

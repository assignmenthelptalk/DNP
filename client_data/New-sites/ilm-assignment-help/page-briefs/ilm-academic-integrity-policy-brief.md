## EAV Depth Map
**Derived Attributes**:
- **City & Guilds Malpractice Definition**: Submitting work that is not the candidate's own as if it were. Plagiarism (unattributed copying). Collusion (sharing work between candidates without attribution). Fabricating evidence (work products or witness statements that did not occur).
- **Authentication Declaration**: Candidates sign a declaration confirming the work is their own at submission. This is a formal statement with defined consequences if false.
- **Legitimate Help**: Understanding what each assessment criterion requires; understanding what work-based evidence types are acceptable; receiving feedback on a draft that identifies gaps against criteria (not prescriptive rewrites); reviewing model answers or exemplars to understand what a strong submission looks like; guidance on reflective frameworks (Gibbs, Kolb) to structure own experience.
- **Assessor Feedback Rules**: ILM assessors can give formative feedback identifying gaps but cannot prescribe what to write. Feedback must develop the candidate's ability to produce their own evidence — not produce it for them.
- **Consequences**: Malpractice finding = unit grade removed. Repeated or serious malpractice = exclusion from qualification. Centre malpractice = centre sanctions from City & Guilds.
- **What Is NOT Malpractice**: Getting help understanding criteria. Using a framework guide to structure reflective writing. Having grammar/spelling corrected. Discussing your management experiences to help identify evidence.

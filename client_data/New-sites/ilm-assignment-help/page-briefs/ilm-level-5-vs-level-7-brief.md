## EAV Depth Map
**Primary Entity**: ILM Level 5 and Level 7 assignment comparison

**Derived Attributes**:
- **RQF Levels**: Level 5 = RQF Level 5 (degree-level credit); Level 7 = RQF Level 7 (Master's-level credit). Two levels apart — significant difference in expected academic standard.
- **Level 5 Assignment Standard**: Critical analysis of management theory applied to own management practice. Harvard referencing from academic textbooks. Analysis of team/department-level issues. Assignments 2,000–3,500 words per unit.
- **Level 7 Assignment Standard**: Postgraduate critical synthesis across multiple strategic frameworks. Peer-reviewed journal citations expected alongside textbooks. Whole-organisation strategic-level analysis. Assignments 3,000–5,000 words per unit. Independent academic judgement expected.
- **Theory Depth**: Level 5 — apply and critically analyse individual theories (Kotter's 8 Steps, Bass transformational leadership, Gibbs reflection). Level 7 — synthesise competing theoretical perspectives (Mintzberg's strategy schools vs Ansoff, adaptive vs transformational leadership debate, Kaplan/Norton Balanced Scorecard vs McKinsey 7S).
- **Career Applicability**: Level 5 = middle managers, team managers, project managers, operations managers with 3–10 direct reports or budget responsibility. Level 7 = directors, senior leaders, NHS band 8+, executives, board-level managers.
- **Referencing Standard**: Level 5 = Harvard referencing from management textbooks is sufficient. Level 7 = peer-reviewed academic journals (Strategic Management Journal, Leadership Quarterly, Academy of Management Review) expected as primary academic foundation.
- **Employer Funding**: Level 5 commonly employer-funded via training budget. Level 7 typically requires senior management sign-off or direct board sponsorship; sometimes funded via apprenticeship levy (Operations/Departmental Manager Apprenticeship at Level 7).

**Values per Attribute**:
- Level 5 RQF level: 5 (degree credit)
- Level 7 RQF level: 7 (Master's credit)
- Level 5 word count: 2,000–3,500 per unit
- Level 7 word count: 3,000–5,000 per unit
- Level 5 referencing: academic textbooks
- Level 7 referencing: peer-reviewed journals
- Level 5 scope: team/department
- Level 7 scope: whole organisation / strategic

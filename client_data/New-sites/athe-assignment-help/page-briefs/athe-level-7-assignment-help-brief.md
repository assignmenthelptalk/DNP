#### H2: ATHE Level 7 Grading Criteria: Pass, Merit, and Distinction Explained
> **EAV to cover**: Three-tier grading system, specific descriptors at each grade, what Distinction requires at Level 7 in concrete terms
> **Word count**: 350–420 words
> **Specific content**:
> - Pass: All learning outcomes are addressed. The response meets the minimum standards — correct subject knowledge applied, LOs covered, referenced. Description and explanation at an appropriate level. No significant gaps in LO coverage.
> - Merit: All LOs met plus Merit descriptors. At Level 7, Merit requires a coherent and sustained argument, consistent application of relevant management or healthcare theory to specific organisational or policy contexts, and structured analytical reasoning supported by properly cited academic and professional sources. The argument must hold across the whole assignment — not just in selected sections.
> - Distinction: All LOs and Merit descriptors met plus Distinction descriptors. At Level 7, Distinction requires: (1) critical evaluation — not just applying a framework but assessing its limitations, comparing it to competing frameworks, and justifying which is most appropriate in context; (2) synthesis — drawing conclusions that integrate evidence from multiple academic and professional sources rather than reporting them separately; (3) professional strategic recommendations — specific, justified, and feasible recommendations that demonstrate applied judgement, not generic conclusions; (4) reflective or evaluative depth — showing awareness of the complexity of real-world strategic or healthcare management decisions.
> - Concrete example of the Distinction standard for a Strategic Leadership unit: a Pass-level response describes Bass's (1985) transformational leadership model. A Merit response applies it to a named organisational change scenario. A Distinction response critically evaluates Bass (1985) against Burns's (1978) original transactional/transformational framework, assesses the appropriateness of transformational leadership in the specific NHS or corporate context, integrates evidence from peer-reviewed journals, and makes a specific recommendation about leadership approach with justification.

#### H2: ATHE Level 7 Strategic Management Assignments: Frameworks and Theory Required
> **EAV to cover**: Core theoretical frameworks for Strategic Management route, how to apply them at Level 7 standard, unit-specific content
> **Word count**: 320–400 words
> **Specific content**:
> - Strategic Leadership: Bass's transformational leadership model (1985) and Burns's earlier transactional/transformational distinction (1978). Distinguish between the two — Bass extended Burns's work. Goleman's emotional intelligence dimensions (1995) are frequently relevant in leadership unit assignments. Servant leadership (Greenleaf, 1970) as a contrasting model.
> - Leading Organisational Change: Kotter's 8-Step Change Model (1996) — the eight stages: create urgency, build guiding coalition, form strategic vision, enlist volunteer army, enable action, generate short-term wins, sustain acceleration, institute change. Lewin's Three-Stage Model (1951) — Unfreeze, Change, Refreeze — simpler but useful for comparative analysis. McKinsey 7S Framework for assessing organisational readiness for change.
> - Strategic Management: Porter's Generic Strategies (1980) — Cost Leadership, Differentiation, Focus — used for strategic positioning analysis, not as a description exercise. PESTLE analysis at strategic level means identifying which macro-environmental factors create strategic risk or opportunity, not listing factors generically. SWOT at Level 7 must connect internal capabilities to external opportunities and threats and derive strategic choices — not produce a four-box list.
> - Strategic HRM: Ulrich's HR Business Partner model (1997) — four HR roles: Strategic Partner, Change Agent, Administrative Expert, Employee Champion. Resource-Based View of the firm (Barney, 1991) — competitive advantage from internal, valuable, rare, inimitable, non-substitutable (VRIN) resources. Linking HR strategy to organisational strategy.
> - Harvard referencing at this level requires peer-reviewed journal articles alongside textbooks. Relying only on textbooks is unlikely to meet Distinction criteria for source quality.

#### H2: Harvard Referencing for ATHE Level 7 Assignments
> **EAV to cover**: Harvard referencing format, source quality requirements at Level 7, common errors, what constitutes an acceptable academic source at this level
> **Word count**: 250–300 words
> **Specific content**:
> - Harvard referencing uses author-date in-text citations: (Bass, 1985) or Bass (1985) depending on sentence structure. Reference list is alphabetical by author surname. Every in-text citation must have a corresponding reference list entry; every reference list entry must have a corresponding in-text citation.
> - Source quality at Level 7: peer-reviewed academic journals are expected alongside textbooks. Key journals for Strategic Management: Journal of Management, Harvard Business Review, Strategic Management Journal, Academy of Management Review. For Healthcare Management: BMJ, Health Policy, Journal of Health Services Research and Policy, NHS England and NHS Digital official publications.
> - Government and NHS publications are acceptable primary sources for policy — e.g., NHS Long Term Plan (NHS England, 2019), NHS People Plan (NHS England, 2020). CIPD and CMI reports are acceptable for HRM topics.
> - Common referencing errors at Level 7: citing only textbooks (insufficient for Distinction); citing websites without author or date; citing "NHS" as an author without identifying the specific publication; failing to include page numbers for direct quotations.
> - Direct quotations should be used sparingly at Level 7 — paraphrasing and synthesis are expected. A Distinction-level assignment that reads as a series of quotations has not demonstrated synthesis.

### FAQ SECTION

**Q: What is the difference between a Merit and a Distinction in an ATHE Level 7 assignment?**
Merit requires all learning outcomes to be met alongside a coherent and sustained argument with consistent application of relevant management or healthcare theory to a specific organisational context, supported by properly referenced academic sources. Distinction requires all Merit criteria plus critical evaluation of competing theoretical frameworks, synthesis of evidence from multiple academic and professional sources, and professional strategic recommendations with clear justification — demonstrating postgraduate-level evaluative and reflective depth throughout the assignment.

**Q: Does ATHE Level 7 require peer-reviewed journal sources?**
Distinction-level ATHE Level 7 assignments require peer-reviewed journal articles alongside textbooks. Government policy documents — such as NHS England publications and CIPD reports — are also accepted primary sources for relevant topics. Assignments that rely solely on textbook citations are unlikely to meet Distinction descriptors for source quality and critical engagement. Key journals for Strategic Management include the Strategic Management Journal and Harvard Business Review; for Healthcare Management, the BMJ and Journal of Health Services Research and Policy are appropriate.

**Q: What theoretical frameworks are most commonly assessed in ATHE Level 7 Strategic Management assignments?**
Strategic Management route assignments most frequently assess Bass's (1985) transformational leadership model, Kotter's (1996) 8-Step Change Model, Lewin's (1951) Three-Stage Change Model, Porter's (1980) Generic Strategies, the Ulrich (1997) HR Business Partner model, and Barney's (1991) Resource-Based View of the firm. Distinction-level responses require critical evaluation of these frameworks — assessing their limitations and comparing them to alternative models — rather than descriptive application alone.

**Q: Can ATHE Level 7 be studied without having completed Level 5 first?**
Entry to ATHE Level 7 is possible via a professional experience route — a prior ATHE Level 5 qualification is not a mandatory requirement for all centres. Centres assess individual learner suitability based on professional background, experience, and prior academic or vocational achievement. Regardless of entry route, the analytical standard required for Level 7 assignments remains the same: postgraduate-equivalent critical evaluation, synthesis, and strategic recommendation at every grade above Pass.

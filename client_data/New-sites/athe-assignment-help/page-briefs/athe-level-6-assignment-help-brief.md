### FAQ SECTION

**Q: What subjects are available at ATHE Level 6?**
ATHE Level 6 Diploma is available in Business Management and Healthcare Management. Not all subjects that are offered at lower ATHE levels (such as Computing and Tourism and Hospitality) continue to Level 6. Both subjects are assessed by written coursework and a research project — no written exams are required at any point.

**Q: Does ATHE Level 6 include a research project?**
ATHE Level 6 includes a research project as a key qualification component. Students must design a research question, write a literature review, justify their research methodology (qualitative, quantitative, or mixed), collect and analyse data, and produce a written report with findings, discussion, and evidence-based conclusions. The research project is assessed against the same criterion-referenced Pass/Merit/Distinction grading system as other units.

**Q: Is ATHE Level 6 equivalent to a university degree?**
ATHE Level 6 Diploma is a qualification at RQF Level 6 — the same academic level as a bachelor's degree. It is not a university-awarded degree; it is awarded by ATHE, an Ofqual-regulated UK awarding body. In academic level, it is the equivalent of a full undergraduate degree, which is why it is widely used by professionals seeking a degree-level credential without attending university.

**Q: What does Distinction require at ATHE Level 6?**
Distinction at ATHE Level 6 requires all learning outcomes to be met, all merit descriptors satisfied, and the additional distinction descriptors demonstrated — which at degree level include original analytical thinking, critical evaluation of the limitations of theoretical frameworks, synthesis of independently researched academic literature, and specific professional recommendations evidenced from that research. Research project Distinction additionally requires methodological rigour and analytical depth in data interpretation.

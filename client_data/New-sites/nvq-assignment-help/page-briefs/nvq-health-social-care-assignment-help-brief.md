## EAV Depth Map
**Primary Entity**: NVQ Health and Social Care qualification portfolio
**Main Attributes**: Level 2 vs Level 3 differences, core unit areas, key legislation, evidence types required, person-centred values, safeguarding requirements, duty of care, how portfolio assessment works
**Derived Attributes**:
- **Level 2 vs Level 3 differences**: NVQ Level 2 in Health and Social Care — entry level, typically held by care assistants and support workers who carry out direct care tasks under supervision. Evidence is more descriptive — demonstrating that the candidate can carry out specific care activities to a standard. NVQ Level 3 — for senior carers, team leaders, and those working with greater autonomy. Requires more reflective and analytical evidence — evaluating own practice, demonstrating professional judgement, showing ability to mentor others in some units. Level 3 portfolios are larger (more units, more evidence types required per unit) and take longer to complete.
- **Core unit areas in NVQ H&SC Level 2**: Communication in H&SC settings (verbal, non-verbal, written, using records systems); Equality and Diversity (Equality Act 2010 — nine protected characteristics, person-centred values); Safeguarding Adults (Care Act 2014 — types of abuse, recognition signs, reporting procedures, Multi-Agency Safeguarding Adults framework); Duty of Care (what it means in practice, duty of care dilemmas — balancing choice and risk); Person-Centred Values (individuality, independence, privacy, partnership, choice, dignity, respect, rights — IPPCDRR); Health and Safety (Health and Safety at Work etc. Act 1974, manual handling, COSHH for care settings, PPE); Personal Development (reflective practice introduction, CPD, supervision)
- **Core unit areas in NVQ H&SC Level 3**: Same foundation units at greater analytical depth, plus: Supporting individuals with specific conditions (dementia, mental health, learning disabilities), Safeguarding Children (where working in mixed-provision settings — Children Act 1989 and 2004), Mentoring and Coaching (for senior care workers), Medication Administration (local administration policy, MAR charts, oral medication procedures), Risk Assessment and Management in care settings
- **Key legislation for H&SC portfolio evidence**: Care Act 2014 (adult social care duties, safeguarding adults, eligibility criteria), Mental Capacity Act 2005 (assessing capacity, best interests decisions, Deprivation of Liberty Safeguards — DoLS), Health and Safety at Work etc. Act 1974 (employer and employee duties), Manual Handling Operations Regulations 1992, COSHH Regulations 2002 (care setting — cleaning products, body fluids), Data Protection Act 2018 / UK GDPR (confidentiality of care records), Equality Act 2010 (nine protected characteristics), Human Rights Act 1998 (dignity, privacy, autonomy)
- **Person-centred values — IPPCDRR**: Individuality (each person is unique), Independence (support autonomy), Privacy (right to private space and information), Partnership (working with the person not for them), Choice (the person decides), Dignity (treating people with respect), Rights (legal and human rights upheld), Respect (valuing the person's beliefs, culture, preferences). These values underpin every H&SC unit and must appear in portfolio evidence — not as a list, but as demonstrated practice.
- **Safeguarding evidence specifics**: Personal statements for safeguarding units must describe specific actions taken when safeguarding concerns arose or could have arisen. Evidence must demonstrate: ability to recognise signs and indicators of abuse (physical, emotional, sexual, financial, neglect, institutional — Care Act 2014 categories); knowledge of local safeguarding adult board (LSAB) procedures; correct reporting channels; not sharing suspicions with the alleged abuser.
- **Duty of care dilemmas**: A common H&SC reflective account topic. Example: Mr X wants to go out alone despite a risk assessment identifying he poses a risk of falls when unsupported. Duty of care (maintaining safety) vs supporting choice and autonomy (person-centred values, Mental Capacity Act — if he has capacity, he has the right to take risks). Reflective accounts on duty of care dilemmas are expected to reference the MCA 2005 and demonstrate the balancing act.
- **Evidence types in H&SC portfolio**: Direct observation (assessor observes candidate giving personal care, administering medication, conducting risk assessment) — the primary evidence type; personal statements (describing activities not directly observed); witness testimonies (from senior carer, supervisor, registered manager — confirming competence in specific activities); professional discussion (assessor Q&A verifying understanding of legislation, policy); reflective accounts (for personal development and evaluative PCs)

### FAQ SECTION

**Q: What legislation must I reference in NVQ Health and Social Care portfolio evidence?**
The most important legislation for NVQ H&SC portfolios is: the Care Act 2014 (adult safeguarding and care planning), the Mental Capacity Act 2005 (capacity assessment and best interests), the Equality Act 2010 (non-discrimination in care), and the Data Protection Act 2018 (confidentiality of care records). You do not need to quote statutes verbatim — demonstrate that you applied the relevant principles in your practice and name the Act when describing your reasoning.

**Q: How does NVQ Level 2 Health and Social Care differ from Level 3?**
NVQ Level 2 is designed for care assistants and support workers carrying out direct care tasks under supervision. Evidence is primarily descriptive — personal statements and witness testimonies showing you can carry out specific care activities to the required standard. NVQ Level 3 is for senior carers, team leaders, and those working with greater autonomy. Level 3 requires more reflective and analytical evidence — evaluating your own practice, demonstrating professional judgement, and showing competence in higher-responsibility tasks like medication administration and contributing to risk assessments.

**Q: Can my senior carer or registered manager provide a witness testimony for my NVQ?**
Supervisors, senior carers, team leaders, and registered managers are all suitable witnesses for NVQ H&SC portfolios. The witness must have directly observed the candidate carrying out the activity they are testifying to — they cannot testify to practice they haven't personally witnessed. The testimony must be written on the awarding organisation's witness testimony form (or an equivalent) and signed and dated. The witness's job title and their relationship to the candidate must be stated on the form.

**Q: How long does an NVQ Level 3 Health and Social Care portfolio take to complete?**
NVQ Level 3 Health and Social Care typically takes 12–18 months to complete, depending on how many working hours the candidate has available for assessment, how quickly observations can be scheduled, and whether the candidate is completing the qualification through an apprenticeship or self-funded route. The timeline is driven by evidence generation — candidates must accumulate observed practice across multiple units, which requires ongoing assessment visits from the assessor across the qualification period.

#### H2: FAQ — Marking Criteria Representative Query Extensions

> **Critical rule**: These questions must NOT repeat Main Content. They must target new representative query variants optimised for Passage Indexing and People Also Ask.

**Questions and Answer Directions**:
1. **Is NEBOSH marked out of 100?** → Answer: No. NEBOSH uses a band system (Pass/Merit/Distinction/Refer) with percentage thresholds varying by unit. IG1/NG1 threshold is approximately 45% for pass. The examiner applies a marking scheme with allocated marks per task, but the final result is reported as a band, not a percentage score to the student.
2. **Can you fail the NEBOSH open book exam and still pass?** → Answer: Depends on unit structure. For IGC, IG1 and IG2 must both pass. Failing IG1 means a referral for that unit even if IG2 is satisfactory. Both units must meet pass threshold independently.
3. **How many attempts do you get at a NEBOSH referral?** → Answer: NEBOSH allows re-submission of referred work. The number of permitted re-submissions and the timeframe depend on the learning provider and NEBOSH's assessment regulations — typically up to 2 further attempts within a defined window.
4. **Does word count affect NEBOSH marking?** → Answer: Word count is not directly penalised as a metric, but under-writing typically results in insufficient depth to meet command word requirements. NEBOSH examiners report that many referred answers are too short to demonstrate full understanding — especially on "explain" and "evaluate" tasks.
5. **What does NEBOSH consider a 'good' answer?** → Answer: NEBOSH marking guidance defines a good answer as one that: directly addresses the command word, applies the principle to a realistic workplace context, names relevant legislation or industry standard where applicable, and demonstrates that the student understands the WHY not just the WHAT.

#### H2: What Does a NEBOSH Referral Actually Mean? — *Major Query Need: referral definition*
> **Context vector**: Referral as a specific, defined assessment outcome — not a final failure
> **Main entity + attributes to cover**:
> - NEBOSH uses the term "Refer" (not "Fail") — this is deliberate; it signals that re-submission is the expected path
> - A referral means the submitted work did not meet the pass threshold for one or more assessed components
> - For OBE units (IG1, NG1): referral means the total mark across tasks fell below the pass threshold (~45%)
> - For IG2/NG2 practical: referral can also mean a mandatory section is incomplete, regardless of overall quality
> - What appears on the NEBOSH certificate: only Pass, Merit, and Distinction grades appear on certificates — a referral is not recorded on the final qualification certificate
> - Referral vs fail: a "fail" would mean no further opportunity to submit; a "refer" explicitly invites re-submission
> **Predicate to use**: "requires" — "a NEBOSH referral requires the student to re-submit..."
> **Word count target**: 280–350 words
> **Internal links**: None

#### H2: FAQ — Referral Recovery Representative Query Extensions

**Questions and Answer Directions**:
1. **Can I appeal a NEBOSH referral?** → Answer: NEBOSH has a formal appeals process for candidates who believe their work was marked incorrectly. Appeals must be submitted through the approved learning provider within the timeframe specified in NEBOSH's appeals policy. Grounds for appeal are limited to administrative errors, procedural irregularities, or evidence that marking guidance was not correctly applied — examiners' academic judgements on answer quality are not normally grounds for appeal.
2. **How many times can I re-sit a NEBOSH assignment?** → Answer: NEBOSH qualification regulations set the maximum number of attempts. The specific limit depends on the qualification and the approved learning provider's programme terms. Most providers allow up to two re-sits (three total attempts) within the programme enrolment period. After exhausting permitted attempts, candidates may need to re-enrol in the full programme.
3. **Will I have to pay for my NEBOSH re-sit?** → Answer: Re-sit fees are charged by the approved learning provider for new OBE window registrations. The exact cost varies by provider but typically ranges from £150 to £350. Some providers include one re-sit attempt in the original course fee — check your programme terms before assuming you will be charged.
4. **How long do I have to re-submit a NEBOSH assignment after a referral?** → Answer: The timeframe is set by your approved learning provider in accordance with NEBOSH's qualification regulations. For OBE-format units, the re-submission requires booking into a new exam window — availability depends on when NEBOSH schedules OBE sittings for your qualification. Contact your learning provider as soon as possible after receiving a referral result to confirm the available re-submission windows.
5. **What is the difference between a NEBOSH referral and a NEBOSH fail?** → Answer: NEBOSH does not use the word "fail" in its grading system. "Refer" is the specific term for a result below the pass threshold. This is meaningful — a referral explicitly signals that re-submission is the expected and permitted next step. A "fail" in standard academic terminology implies a closed result; a NEBOSH "refer" implies an open one. This distinction matters for how students frame their next steps.

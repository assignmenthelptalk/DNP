## EAV Depth Map
**Primary Entity**: NEBOSH examination schedule and registration process

**Main Attributes**: OBE window frequency, registration process, registration deadlines, study timeline by qualification, deferral rules, practical assessment submission, Diploma assignment deadlines

**Derived Attributes**:
- **OBE Window Frequency**: NEBOSH runs multiple OBE windows per year — typically 4–6 assessment periods annually for most certificate units (IGC, NGC, Fire, Environmental, Construction). Exact dates are published by NEBOSH on their website and communicated to candidates via their Approved Learning Partner (ALP). Windows are not uniformly spaced — candidates should check NEBOSH's current assessment schedule for the specific unit.
- **Registration Process**: Candidates register for OBE windows through their Approved Learning Partner (ALP) — not directly with NEBOSH in most cases. The ALP enters the candidate's registration with NEBOSH on their behalf. Candidates studying without an ALP (direct NEBOSH registration) submit their own registration via the NEBOSH candidate portal.
- **Registration Deadlines**: Typically 4–6 weeks before the OBE window opens. Missing the registration deadline means the candidate cannot sit that OBE window and must register for the next available window. Registration deadlines are absolute — NEBOSH does not offer late registration.
- **Study Timeline (IGC/NGC)**: Recommended study time is 100–130 guided learning hours. Working full-time, most candidates study over 10–16 weeks. An 8-week intensive study timeline is achievable for candidates with prior H&S experience. A 20-week timeline suits candidates studying with limited weekly time availability. The OBE window should be scheduled with 2 weeks of scenario-specific practice time built in before the window opens.
- **Study Timeline (Diploma)**: DN1, DN2, and DN3 are each substantial academic assignments. Total guided learning hours: 400–500 across all three units. Typical completion: 12–18 months for working professionals. Each unit assignment should be allocated a minimum of 8–12 weeks of dedicated research and writing time.
- **Deferral Rules**: Candidates who have registered for an OBE window but cannot sit it can request a deferral to a future window. Deferral conditions and fees vary — candidates should contact their ALP or NEBOSH directly before the window opens. Deferrals after the window has opened are not available.
- **Practical Assessment Submission (IG2/NG2)**: Submitted separately from the OBE — not time-constrained to a specific window. Candidates can complete the practical assessment at any time after enrolling and submit when ready. However, the qualification is not awarded until both the OBE and the practical assessment have been passed.
- **OBE Window Timeline for Candidates**: Scenario document made available to candidates typically 5–7 days before the 48-hour window opens. The 48-hour window opens on a specific date and time — the candidate has exactly 48 hours from that moment to submit all completed tasks.

### FAQ SECTION

**Q: How many times a year can you sit the NEBOSH OBE?**
NEBOSH runs 4–6 OBE windows per year for most certificate units (IGC, NGC, Fire, Environmental, Construction). The exact number and schedule varies by unit and by year. Current window dates are published on the NEBOSH website (nebosh.org.uk) under Assessment Dates — candidates should check the official schedule for their specific unit.

**Q: How far in advance do you need to register for a NEBOSH OBE?**
Registration typically closes approximately 4–6 weeks before the OBE window opens. The exact deadline varies by unit and window — check the NEBOSH website or confirm with your Approved Learning Partner. Registrations are not accepted after the deadline.

**Q: How long does it take to study for the NEBOSH IGC?**
Most working professionals complete the NEBOSH IGC in 10–16 weeks of structured study. Candidates with prior H&S experience may be ready in 8–10 weeks. The recommended guided learning hours are 100–130. The final 2 weeks before the OBE window should be dedicated to OBE-specific practice — working through scenario-based tasks under time pressure — rather than general subject revision.

**Q: What happens if I miss the NEBOSH OBE deadline?**
If you miss the registration deadline, you cannot sit that OBE window and must register for the next available window. If you have registered but cannot sit the window, contact your ALP or NEBOSH as soon as possible to request a deferral — this must be done before the window opens; deferral is not available once the 48-hour window has started.

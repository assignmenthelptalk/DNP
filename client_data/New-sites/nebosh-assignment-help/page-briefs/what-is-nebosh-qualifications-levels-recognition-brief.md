### FAQ SECTION

**Q: What does NEBOSH stand for?**
NEBOSH stands for National Examination Board in Occupational Safety and Health. It is an Ofqual-regulated awarding organisation founded in 1979 and headquartered in Coventry, UK. It awards occupational health and safety qualifications studied by candidates in 130+ countries.

**Q: What is the difference between NEBOSH and IOSH?**
NEBOSH is an awarding organisation — it designs and awards health and safety qualifications. IOSH (Institution of Occupational Safety and Health) is a professional membership body — it grants membership grades to qualified practitioners. They serve different functions: you study for and pass a NEBOSH qualification, then use it to apply for IOSH membership. NEBOSH and IOSH are separate organisations, though NEBOSH qualifications are mapped to IOSH membership grades.

**Q: Is NEBOSH a degree?**
No. NEBOSH awards qualifications at several levels. The Certificate qualifications (IGC, NGC) are at RQF Level 3 — equivalent to A-Level standard. The Diploma is at RQF Level 6 — equivalent to degree standard — but is not itself a degree. It is a professional qualification that requires the same level of academic rigour as degree-level study.

**Q: Which NEBOSH qualification should I start with?**
Most candidates begin with the NEBOSH IGC (if working internationally or outside the UK) or the NEBOSH NGC (if UK-based). Both are entry-level certificates at RQF Level 3 that provide a comprehensive foundation in health and safety management. The Diploma is the natural progression after completing a certificate and gaining several years of practical H&S experience.

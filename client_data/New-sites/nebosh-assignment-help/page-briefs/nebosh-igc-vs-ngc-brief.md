### FAQ SECTION

**Q: What is the main difference between NEBOSH IGC and NGC?**
The primary difference is the legislative framework each qualification requires candidates to reference. The IGC (International General Certificate) uses international frameworks — ILO-OSH 2001 and ISO 45001:2018. The NGC (National General Certificate) uses UK domestic legislation — HSWA 1974, MHSWR 1999, COSHH 2002, RIDDOR 2013, and several other UK regulations. The assessment structure is identical: both have an OBE and a practical assessment, both use the same command words, and both have the same grading thresholds.

**Q: Can I study NEBOSH IGC if I am based in the UK?**
Yes. UK-based candidates can study the IGC — it is not restricted to non-UK candidates. However, if your current role is regulated by UK health and safety law and your employer requires knowledge of UK-specific legislation, the NGC is the more appropriate choice. If you are UK-based but working for an international employer or planning to work internationally, the IGC's broader geographic recognition may make it the better option.

**Q: Is NEBOSH IGC harder than NEBOSH NGC?**
No. Both qualifications are at RQF Level 3, use identical assessment formats (48-hour OBE + practical report), have identical grading thresholds (Distinction 80%+, Pass 45%+), and require the same level of study commitment. The difficulty difference is in the legislative content — NGC requires knowledge of a specific body of UK regulations; IGC requires knowledge of international management frameworks. Neither is objectively harder.

**Q: Can I do both NEBOSH IGC and NEBOSH NGC?**
You can study both, but most candidates do not need to. The qualification appropriate to your workplace and jurisdiction is the one that will be most useful. Holding both is rare unless a role genuinely requires demonstrating competency in both UK and international H&S frameworks simultaneously.

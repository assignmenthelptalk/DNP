#### H2: Worked Example 1: How to Answer an "Explain" Task at Distinction Level
> **Context vector**: The "Explain" command word in practice — the most commonly under-answered command word in NEBOSH OBE; worked comparison of pass vs distinction answer
> **Main entity + attributes to cover**:
> - Sample scenario extract: warehouse operation — worker slipped on wet floor near loading bay; investigation found no wet floor signage, poor drainage design, and compressed schedule preventing routine cleaning
> - Sample task: "Explain why the slip incident in the scenario was likely to occur." (8 marks)
> - **Pass-grade answer** (worked — approx. 120 words):
>   - States general causes: wet floor, no signage, poor housekeeping
>   - Identifies hazard correctly but does not explain mechanism
>   - No scenario detail beyond what is directly stated
>   - No legislation or standard referenced
>   - Mark achievable: approximately 4–5/8 (passes, below distinction)
> - **Distinction-grade answer** (worked — approx. 240 words):
>   - References specific scenario details: loading bay location, compressed schedule, drainage design
>   - Explains each causal chain: compressed schedule → cleaning not completed → residual water from loading bay operations → increased likelihood of wet floor → slip event
>   - Names the relevant regulatory standard: Management of Health and Safety at Work Regulations 1999 reg.3 (suitable and sufficient risk assessment) — explains why a risk assessment would have identified the drainage and scheduling interaction as a foreseeable risk
>   - Connects to hierarchy of control: drainage design (engineering control) vs signage (administrative control) — distinction answer notes that eliminating the wet floor source at design stage is preferable to reliance on warning signs
>   - Proactive frame: identifies that the combination of contributory factors (not any single cause) explains why the incident was likely to occur, not merely possible
> - **Annotation**: explains WHY the distinction answer earns more marks — causal chain depth, scenario specificity, legislation integration, hierarchy of controls reference
> **Predicate to use**: "demonstrates" — "the distinction answer demonstrates causal chain analysis"
> **First word of first paragraph**: "Explain" — begin with "Explain tasks in NEBOSH OBE..."
> **Writer note**: Write the pass answer and distinction answer as actual answer text in blockquote or similar formatting, then annotate. Do not describe what the answers would contain — write them.
> **Word count target**: 420–500 words
> **Internal links**: Anchor: "NEBOSH assignment marking criteria" → /nebosh-assignment-marking-criteria-explained

#### H2: Worked Example 2: How to Answer a "Suggest" Task at Distinction Level
> **Context vector**: The "Suggest" command word — justified recommendations grounded in scenario context
> **Main entity + attributes to cover**:
> - "Suggest" task characteristics: no single correct answer; answers assessed on whether the suggestion is reasonable, justified by scenario context, and specific enough to be actionable
> - Sample task (continuing warehouse scenario): "Suggest TWO improvements to the management of slip risks in the warehouse described in the scenario." (4 marks — 2 per suggestion)
> - **Pass-grade answer**:
>   - "Install wet floor signs near the loading bay" — correct but generic; no justification; no connection to scenario-specific drainage or scheduling issue
>   - "Provide slip-resistant footwear" — correct but defaults to PPE without considering hierarchy; no scenario connection
>   - Mark achievable: 2/4 (pass, insufficient for distinction)
> - **Distinction-grade answer**:
>   - Suggestion 1: Redesign loading bay drainage to eliminate standing water at source — justified by scenario reference to poor drainage design and the principle that engineering controls (elimination/reduction) precede administrative measures; references the finding that the compressed schedule prevented routine cleaning, making a design solution more robust than a procedural one
>   - Suggestion 2: Revise the warehouse cleaning schedule to ensure loading bay cleaning occurs immediately after each delivery cycle, not at the end of the shift — justified by scenario reference to compressed schedule causing cleaning deferral; specific and actionable rather than generic "improve housekeeping"
>   - Mark achievable: 4/4
> - **Annotation**: the distinction answers earn full marks because each suggestion is (a) grounded in a specific scenario detail, (b) justified with reasoning, and (c) positioned within the hierarchy of controls rather than defaulting to PPE or signage
> **Predicate to use**: "justifies" — "a distinction suggest answer justifies each recommendation using scenario evidence"
> **First word of first paragraph**: "Suggest" — begin with "Suggest tasks require candidates to..."
> **Word count target**: 380–460 words

#### H2: Worked Example 3: How to Answer a "Describe" Task at Distinction Level
> **Context vector**: The "Describe" command word — full characteristic account, not naming only
> **Main entity + attributes to cover**:
> - "Describe" task characteristics: requires full detail of characteristics, features, or components — not a list of names; the full account distinguishes describe from outline and identify
> - Sample task: "Describe the investigation process that should be followed after a slip incident in the workplace described." (6 marks)
> - **Pass-grade answer** (worked):
>   - Identifies stages: report, investigate, identify causes, implement controls
>   - Correct sequence but each stage stated as a label, not described in detail
>   - No specific investigation steps within each stage
>   - No reference to investigation methodology (fault tree, fishbone, 5 Whys)
>   - Mark achievable: 3–4/6
> - **Distinction-grade answer** (worked):
>   - Describes immediate response: secure the scene, seek first aid for the injured worker, preserve physical evidence (do not clean up until documented), notify responsible persons
>   - Describes fact-gathering: photograph the scene (drainage point, floor surface, signage absence), interview witnesses separately and promptly, retrieve any CCTV footage, collect maintenance records for the drainage system, review risk assessment for the loading bay
>   - Describes root cause analysis: use a structured method (5 Whys or fishbone — names the method) to identify immediate causes, underlying causes, and root causes — scenario-specific: why was the drainage inadequate (design stage decision?), why was cleaning not completed (scheduling pressure — who authorised the compressed schedule?)
>   - Describes output: investigation report with corrective actions, RIDDOR notification if injury is reportable (Reporting of Injuries, Diseases and Dangerous Occurrences Regulations 2013 — slip injuries resulting in over 7-day absence must be reported)
>   - Mark achievable: 6/6
> - **Annotation**: distinction answer describes each stage in enough detail that the reader could follow the process; names the investigation methodology used; integrates RIDDOR as the legislative notification requirement
> **Predicate to use**: "describes" — "a distinction describe answer describes each investigation stage in sufficient detail..."
> **First word of first paragraph**: "Describe" — begin with "Describe tasks in NEBOSH OBE require..."

#### H2: How to Integrate Scenario Information Into Every OBE Answer
> **Context vector**: The anchor technique — using scenario-specific details to satisfy the scenario-application requirement in every task
> **Main entity + attributes to cover**:
> - Rule: every answer to an Explain, Suggest, or Justify task must reference at least one specific scenario detail — the industry, the workplace description, the incident described, the management system context mentioned
> - Anchor technique: before writing each answer, identify 2–3 specific details from the scenario that are relevant to the task; write one of these details into the opening sentence of the answer; use at least one more in the body
> - Generic vs anchored answer: generic answer — "Employers have a duty to assess risks under MHSWR 1999 reg.3"; anchored answer — "The warehouse described in the scenario presents slip risks from loading bay operations that require a suitable and sufficient risk assessment under MHSWR 1999 reg.3, including assessment of the drainage design and the cleaning schedule"
> - NEBOSH examiner report pattern: the most cited reason for mark loss across OBE examiner reports is "candidate described general H&S principles without applying them to the scenario described" — the anchor technique directly addresses this failure
> - Application to all command words: Identify tasks require only a name (scenario reference not always required); Outline and Describe tasks benefit from scenario grounding but do not always require it; Explain, Suggest, Justify, and Evaluate tasks require scenario application for marks at Credit and Distinction level
> **Predicate to use**: "connects" — "the anchor technique connects general H&S knowledge to the scenario"
> **First word of first paragraph**: "Integrating" — begin with "Integrating scenario information into OBE answers..."
> **Word count target**: 280–340 words

#### H2: FAQ — NEBOSH OBE Sample Questions Representative Query Extensions

**Questions and Answer Directions**:
1. **Does NEBOSH publish official OBE sample questions?** → Answer: NEBOSH does not release live OBE scenario papers after sittings, but it publishes examiner reports for each OBE sitting that contain commentary on how candidates answered specific task types and where marks were commonly lost. These reports are the closest available source to official marking intelligence. NEBOSH also provides learner support materials through its website, including guidance on command words and answer structure, which provide indicative examples.
2. **How long should a NEBOSH OBE answer be?** → Answer: Answer length should be proportional to the marks available — a 2-mark task requires 2 developed points (approximately 40–80 words); an 8-mark task requires 8 developed points (approximately 300–450 words for full credit). Over-writing on low-mark tasks wastes time that should be allocated to high-mark extended tasks. NEBOSH examiners do not award extra marks for length — marks are awarded for distinct developed points that meet the command word requirement.
3. **Can I use bullet points in a NEBOSH OBE answer?** → Answer: For Identify and Outline tasks, bullet points are acceptable — the command word requires brief points, not extended prose. For Describe and Explain tasks, NEBOSH examiners expect prose answers that develop each point with detail and causation — bullet-point answers to Describe and Explain tasks typically achieve Pass at best because they cannot adequately demonstrate causal chains and scenario application. For Suggest and Justify tasks, a structured paragraph format (suggestion + justification) is preferred.
4. **How do I know if my OBE answer is at distinction level?** → Answer: A distinction-level OBE answer satisfies five criteria: (1) it directly addresses the command word at the required depth; (2) it references at least one specific scenario detail; (3) it provides a causal chain (for Explain tasks) or a justified recommendation (for Suggest tasks); (4) it names a relevant legislation or standard and applies it to the scenario; (5) it uses proactive or preventive framing where possible. If the answer could have been written without reading the scenario document, it is unlikely to achieve Credit or Distinction.

**Values per Attribute**:
- IG2 structure: (1) Observation and description of workplace activities (2) Risk assessment of identified hazards (typically 5–10 hazards with full assessment per hazard) (3) Management report — prioritised recommendations with cost-benefit justification
- "Suitable and sufficient" definition (HSE/Management of H&S at Work Regs 1999 Reg 3): risk assessment must identify all significant risks relevant to the activities being carried out — not every conceivable hazard, but all reasonably foreseeable ones
- Risk rating: Likelihood (1–5) × Severity (1–5) = Risk Score (1–25) — common matrix; examiner expects a system to be applied consistently
- Management recommendations: NEBOSH examiners expect recommendations framed as "In order to reduce the risk of [harm] from [hazard], [specific action] should be taken by [person responsible] within [timeframe], because [justification]"
- Referral rate for IG2 is often linked to incomplete mandatory sections or insufficient hazard justification depth

> **Context vector**: Observation and documentation methodology for the IG2 site visit

#### H2: NEBOSH IG2 Risk Assessment Format: What Examiners Expect — *Major Query Need: risk assessment format*
> **Context vector**: IG2 risk assessment as a specific assessed document with required components per hazard
> **Main entity + attributes to cover**:
> - Per-hazard entries must include: hazard description → who is at risk and how many → existing controls → likelihood rating (1–5) → severity rating (1–5) → risk score → recommended additional controls → residual risk score after controls
> - "Suitable and sufficient" standard (HSE Management of H&S at Work Regulations 1999, Regulation 3): not about perfection — about identifying all reasonably foreseeable risks relevant to the work activities
> - Hierarchy of controls application: elimination → substitution → engineering controls → administrative controls → PPE — recommendations must follow this hierarchy
> - Justification depth: examiner reports consistently note that students who only list controls without justifying them against the hazard score lower marks
> - Distinction-level entries name the specific legislation relevant to each hazard (e.g., Working at Height Regulations 2005 for height work, COSHH Regulations 2002 for chemical hazards)
> **Predicate to use**: "demonstrates" — "a distinction-level risk assessment entry demonstrates..."

#### H2: IG2 Management Report: How to Write Recommendations That Score Marks — *Major Query Need: management report*
> **Context vector**: Management report as the most-marked component of IG2 — where distinction is won or lost
> **Main entity + attributes to cover**:
> - Report structure: executive summary → prioritised recommendations → justification per recommendation → implementation timeline → resource implications
> - Prioritisation methodology: highest risk score hazards addressed first; use risk matrix data from the assessment to justify prioritisation order
> - SMART recommendations format: not "improve housekeeping" but "implement a daily walkthrough inspection checklist for the warehouse floor, completed by the site supervisor by 0800 each morning, to reduce slip/trip risk from 12 to 4 on the risk matrix within 30 days"
> - Cost-benefit framing: distinction reports acknowledge the cost of the recommended control measure and justify it against the potential cost of harm (HSE publishes injury cost data that can be referenced)
> - Linking recommendations to legislation: each recommendation should reference the legal duty that requires or supports it
> **Predicate to use**: "prioritises" — "the management report prioritises recommendations by risk score..."

#### H2: FAQ — IG2 Practical Assessment Representative Query Extensions

**Questions and Answer Directions**:
1. **Can I do the NEBOSH IG2 at my own workplace?** → Answer: Yes. NEBOSH permits students to use their own employer's workplace for the IG2 practical assessment, provided the site has sufficient hazard diversity to complete a meaningful risk assessment. Students must conduct the assessment themselves — they cannot use a pre-existing company risk assessment.
2. **How many hazards do I need for the NEBOSH IG2 risk assessment?** → Answer: NEBOSH does not specify a minimum number in published guidance, but examiner reports and marking guidance consistently indicate that 8–10 fully assessed hazard entries (with all fields completed) are required for distinction-level submissions. Fewer than 5 complete entries typically results in a refer.
3. **How long does the NEBOSH IG2 take to complete?** → Answer: NEBOSH requires the IG2 to be completed within a defined period after course completion — typically within 10 years for most IGC learners. The physical site visit and observation typically takes 2–4 hours; completing the risk assessment document and management report to distinction standard takes most students 8–16 hours total.
4. **Does the NEBOSH IG2 need to be typed or handwritten?** → Answer: NEBOSH accepts both typed and handwritten submissions, but typed submissions are strongly recommended for readability. NEBOSH provides a standard IG2 template document that must be used — students cannot create their own format. The completed document is submitted electronically via the NEBOSH portal.
5. **Can I use a risk assessment template from my workplace for the NEBOSH IG2?** → Answer: No. NEBOSH provides its own IG2 assessment template and requires students to use it. However, students may adapt their workplace's hazard categories and control measures as examples within the NEBOSH template — as long as the content represents the student's own analysis of the site, not a copied company document.

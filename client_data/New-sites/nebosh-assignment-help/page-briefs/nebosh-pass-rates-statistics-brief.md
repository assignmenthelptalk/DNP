## EAV Depth Map

**Main Attributes**: overall pass rate by qualification, Distinction rate, Referral rate, most common failure causes, examiner report insights, comparison across qualification types

**Derived Attributes**:
- **Overall Pass Rate (IGC/NGC OBE)**: NEBOSH does not always publish unit-level pass rates in granular form, but NEBOSH annual reviews and examiner reports indicate overall pass rates for certificate OBE units typically range from 60–75% depending on the sitting. This means approximately 25–40% of candidates receive a Referral on their first attempt.
- **Distinction Rate**: The proportion of candidates achieving Distinction (80%+) is considerably smaller than the pass population — approximately 10–20% of all sitting candidates achieve Distinction, varying by unit and window. NEBOSH examiner reports confirm that many candidates achieve the 45–64% Pass band but do not reach Merit (Credit) or Distinction thresholds.
- **Credit Rate**: The Credit band (65–79%) represents candidates who demonstrate good H&S understanding and reasonable scenario application but fall short of Distinction-level specificity and evaluative depth.
- **Referral Rate**: Approximately 25–40% of first-time OBE sitters receive a Referral. The most commonly cited failure causes in NEBOSH examiner reports: (1) generic answers not referencing the scenario provided; (2) misapplication of command words — writing an outline when the task says describe, or identifying when the task says explain; (3) insufficient detail for multi-mark tasks; (4) answers addressing only part of a multi-part task question.
- **Diploma Pass Rates**: Lower than certificate-level pass rates — extended academic assignment format with higher cognitive demands results in a higher proportion of referrals and resubmissions. NEBOSH Diploma candidates typically have more experience with H&S than certificate candidates but encounter the academic writing standard as a new challenge.
- **NEBOSH Annual Review Data**: NEBOSH publishes an annual review document that includes candidate numbers and some performance data. Examiner reports for specific OBE sittings are published by NEBOSH and contain the most detailed commentary on candidate performance — these are the most authoritative source of information about what differentiates passing and failing responses.
- **Post-2020 OBE Format Shift**: Pass rates and Distinction rates should be interpreted in the context of the 2020 shift to OBE format — the first cohort of OBE sitters had less preparation resource available than subsequent cohorts; pass rate patterns under the OBE format have evolved as candidates and ALPs adapted to the new format.

#### H2: What NEBOSH Grading Bands Mean in Practice
> **EAV to cover**: Distinction / Credit / Pass / Refer thresholds — what candidate performance looks like at each band
> **Word count**: 280–330 words
> **Specific content**:
> - Refer (below 45%): Pass criteria not met — answers significantly incomplete, substantially generic without scenario reference, or multiple tasks addressed at inadequate depth; qualification unit not achieved; one resit permitted

#### H2: Why Candidates Fail NEBOSH: Most Common Referral Causes From Examiner Reports
> **EAV to cover**: The top 3–4 failure causes identified in NEBOSH examiner reports, with specificity
> **Word count**: 340–400 words
> **Specific content**:
> - Failure cause #1 (most common): generic answers not connected to the scenario — NEBOSH examiner reports consistently identify this as the primary reason for Referrals and low Pass grades; candidates write accurate H&S theory but do not reference the specific scenario, workplace, hazards, or people described in the document; an examiner cannot award scenario-application marks for an answer that would be equally valid for any workplace
> - Failure cause #2: command word misapplication — writing a brief identification response when the task says "describe" (which requires full detail); writing a list when "explain" requires causation; writing an analytical discussion when "suggest" requires a practical proposal; command words define the required response depth and candidates who ignore them cannot achieve full marks even with accurate content
> - Failure cause #3: insufficient depth on multi-mark tasks — tasks worth 6, 8, or 10 marks require correspondingly detailed responses; candidates who provide 2–3 points for an 8-mark task achieve partial credit at best; examiner reports note that candidates systematically under-answer multi-mark tasks
> - Failure cause #4 (less frequent): addressing only part of a multi-part question — tasks that ask for two or three distinct elements (e.g., "identify the hazard AND the potential harm") require explicit answers to each element; addressing one element only produces partial credit
> - Examiner reports are published by NEBOSH after each OBE sitting and are available on the NEBOSH website — reading the most recent two or three reports for the specific unit being studied is one of the highest-value preparation activities available

### FAQ SECTION

**Q: What is the pass rate for the NEBOSH IGC?**
Based on NEBOSH annual review data and examiner reports, pass rates for the NEBOSH IGC Open Book Exam (IG1) typically range from 60–75% depending on the specific sitting. This means approximately 25–40% of candidates receive a Referral on their first attempt. NEBOSH publishes examiner reports after each sitting that provide the most detailed commentary on candidate performance — these are available on the NEBOSH website.

**Q: How many people get Distinction in NEBOSH?**
Approximately 10–20% of candidates achieve Distinction (80%+ on the OBE) in a typical window. Distinction is a minority outcome but is consistently achievable by candidates who focus preparation on scenario-specific answering and command word compliance — examiner reports note that Distinction is not restricted to candidates with the greatest H&S experience.

**Q: What is the most common reason NEBOSH candidates fail?**
According to NEBOSH examiner reports, the most common reason for Referral is writing generic health and safety answers that do not reference the specific workplace scenario provided. Candidates demonstrate accurate H&S knowledge but apply it in general terms rather than in the context of the scenario — examiner marks for scenario application cannot be awarded for generic responses.

**Q: Is NEBOSH hard to pass?**
NEBOSH certificate qualifications have a pass rate of approximately 60–75%, meaning a significant minority of first-time candidates receive a Referral. The qualification is not unreachable — the majority of candidates who prepare specifically for the OBE format (scenario application, command word compliance, multi-mark task depth) pass on their first attempt. The difficulty is less in the H&S content and more in the specific OBE technique required.

## EAV Depth Map
**Primary Entity**: CACHE Level 2 qualification assignment
**Main Attributes**: qualification type, assessment format, subject routes, academic level, grading criteria, progression pathway
**Derived Attributes**:
- **Qualification type**: CACHE Level 2 Certificate in Early Years Practice; CACHE Level 2 Certificate in Health and Social Care; some providers offer CACHE Level 2 Award (shorter qualification). All awarded by NCFE CACHE. Level 2 on the Regulated Qualifications Framework (RQF).
- **Assessment format**: Portfolio of evidence — no formal examinations. Evidence types at Level 2: short written assignments, guided observation records (simpler than Level 3), professional discussion records with assessor, witness testimonies from workplace supervisors, product evidence (e.g., completed care plans, activity records)
- **Academic demand at Level 2**: Descriptive rather than analytical. Pass requires demonstrating understanding of core concepts. Candidates describe what they do and what they know — evaluation and critical analysis are not required at Level 2 but are introduced at Level 3. Harvard referencing is not typically required at Level 2 — references and sources may be encouraged but not rigorously assessed.
- **Early Years route units**: Child Development, Play and Learning, Safeguarding Children, Communication, Health and Safety, Supporting Children's Learning, Professional Practice in Early Years
- **Health and Social Care route units**: Communication in H&SC, Equality and Diversity, Safeguarding in H&SC, Personal Development, Duty of Care, Promote Person-Centred Values, Supporting Mental Health
- **Grading at Level 2**: Pass/Merit/Distinction. Pass = all learning outcomes demonstrated. Merit = with some analysis and specific examples. Distinction = with evaluation and clear links between theory and practice. At Level 2, Pass is the most common target — Merit and Distinction require additional depth but are achievable.
- **Progression to Level 3**: CACHE Level 2 is the standard entry route to CACHE Level 3 Diploma. Level 3 introduces: Harvard referencing, reflective models (Gibbs, Kolb, Schon), analytical writing, child development theory depth (Piaget, Vygotsky, Bowlby, Bruner), and longer more complex assignments.
- **Apprenticeship route**: Many Level 2 learners are on Early Years Practitioner apprenticeships (Level 2 standard). The CACHE Level 2 Certificate may be embedded within the apprenticeship framework.

**Values per Attribute**:
- RQF level: Level 2 (equivalent to GCSE grades 4–9 / C and above)
- Credit value: typically 36–65 credits depending on qualification size (Certificate vs Award)
- Harvard referencing required: No (not at Level 2 — encouraged but not assessed rigorously)
- Grading: Pass / Merit / Distinction (criterion-referenced)
- Assessment: portfolio of evidence, no exams
- Typical assignment length: 300–600 words per unit task (shorter than Level 3)

**Attributes on THIS page**: Qualification type and RQF level, assessment format and evidence types, Level 2 Early Years route units, Level 2 H&SC route units, grading at Level 2, progression to Level 3, apprenticeship context
**Attribute depth warning**: Do not import Level 3 academic expectations (reflective models, Harvard referencing requirements, Piaget/Vygotsky depth) into Level 2 descriptions — this will mislead the target audience of entry-level learners. Level 2 is descriptive, not analytical.

#### H2: How CACHE Level 2 Assignments Are Assessed
> **EAV to cover**: Portfolio of evidence, evidence types, assessment criteria, assessor role, no exams
> **Word count**: 280–330 words
> **Specific content**:
> - Assessment is 100% portfolio-based — candidates build a portfolio demonstrating competence and knowledge across each unit
> - Evidence types used at Level 2: short written assignments (300–600 words per task), professional discussion records (recorded Q&A with assessor), witness testimonies (from workplace supervisor or mentor), product evidence (completed documentation — activity plans, observation records, care records), observation of practice (assessor directly observes candidate in placement/workplace)
> - Each unit has Learning Outcomes (LOs) — candidates must demonstrate all LOs to achieve a Pass
> - Merit and Distinction require evidence of deeper understanding: specific examples from placement, some evaluation of practice, making connections between what they do and why
> - Harvard referencing not formally required at Level 2 — but candidates are encouraged to cite sources used
> - Assessors are occupationally competent professionals who verify evidence meets the unit standards

#### H2: Pass, Merit, and Distinction at CACHE Level 2
> **EAV to cover**: What each grade requires at Level 2 in practice, how Level 2 grading differs from Level 3
> **Word count**: 250–300 words
> **Specific content**:
> - CACHE Level 2 uses the same P/M/D grading framework as Level 3 but the depth expectations are significantly lower
> - **Pass**: All learning outcomes demonstrated with accurate, relevant information. Descriptions are clear and show understanding. Examples from placement can be vague or hypothetical at Pass.
> - **Merit**: LOs met with some analysis — candidate begins to explain *why*, not just *what*. Examples from placement are specific and relevant. Some connection made between theory and practice.
> - **Distinction**: LOs met with evaluation — candidate considers different viewpoints, evaluates the effectiveness of their practice, and connects what they do to underlying principles (child development, person-centred values). Not expected to use academic referencing in the same way as Level 3.
> - Common Level 2 mistake: submitting generic descriptions without any placement-specific detail — assessors at Level 2 still expect the evidence to be personalised to the candidate's setting

### FAQ SECTION

**Q: Does CACHE Level 2 require Harvard referencing?**
Harvard referencing is not a formal requirement at Level 2 — most providers encourage candidates to cite sources they have used but do not assess referencing technique as rigorously as at Level 3. Candidates should use any sources they reference but do not need to learn full Harvard format until progressing to Level 3.

**Q: How long are CACHE Level 2 assignments?**
Written tasks at CACHE Level 2 are typically 300–600 words per question or learning outcome — significantly shorter than Level 3 assignments. The focus is on demonstrating clear understanding and relevant examples from placement, not on academic depth or critical analysis.

**Q: Can I do CACHE Level 2 without working in a nursery or care setting?**
Most CACHE Level 2 programmes require a placement element — either a nursery, early years setting, or care provider — because portfolio evidence must include observations and practice-based evidence. Some providers arrange placement for candidates; others require candidates to source their own. Contact your training provider to confirm placement requirements before enrolling.

**Q: How does CACHE Level 2 lead to Level 3?**
CACHE Level 2 is the standard entry route to CACHE Level 3 Diploma — the most widely held childcare qualification in England. After completing Level 2, candidates apply to a Level 3 programme at their college or training provider. Level 3 introduces academic writing, reflective models, child development theory, and Harvard referencing — the skills required for Early Years Educator (EYE) status.

#### H2: CACHE Assessment Formats: Portfolio Evidence, Observation Records, and Reflective Accounts
> **EAV to cover**: all portfolio component types, assessment format specifics, what each format requires, common errors per format
> **Word count**: 320–380 words
> **Specific content**:
> - Written assignments: analytical response to an assignment brief — must address all assessment criteria; structured with introduction, main analysis sections, conclusion, reference list (Harvard at Level 3+)
> - Observation records: formal written records of observing a child's activity — must include date, setting, age of child (years and months), observation type, objective description, analysis linking to EYFS areas of learning and child development theory; observation types: narrative (continuous written description), time sampling (snapshot at set intervals), event sampling (recording specific behaviour), sociogram (social interactions mapping), child tracker (movement through space), anecdotal (brief descriptive record of significant event)
> - Reflective accounts: practitioner reflects on their own practice using a recognised model — Gibbs' Reflective Cycle (1988): Description, Feelings, Evaluation, Analysis, Conclusion, Action Plan; Kolb's Experiential Learning Cycle (1984): Concrete Experience, Reflective Observation, Abstract Conceptualisation, Active Experimentation; Schon's Reflection-in-Action (1983): real-time reflection during practice
> - Professional discussion records: tutor/assessor-led discussion with written record — candidate demonstrates knowledge verbally and answers questions mapped to assessment criteria
> - Witness testimonies: supervisor or workplace assessor confirms observed practice — must be signed and dated and linked to specific units and assessment criteria
> - Common errors: writing observation records as opinion rather than objective fact; writing reflective accounts without linking to theory; submitting witness testimonies without unit mapping

### FAQ SECTION

**Q: What is CACHE and who awards CACHE qualifications?**
CACHE stands for Council for Awards in Care, Health and Education. CACHE is now a specialist brand of NCFE (Northern Council for Further Education), which is the Ofqual-regulated awarding body. NCFE CACHE qualifications are nationally recognised in England across childcare, early years, and health and social care sectors.

**Q: Do CACHE qualifications have exams?**
CACHE qualifications are assessed entirely through a portfolio of evidence — no formal examinations are required. The portfolio includes written assignments, observation records, reflective accounts, professional discussion records, and witness testimonies from workplace supervisors.

**Q: What CACHE qualification do I need for Early Years Educator status?**
Early Years Educator (EYE) status requires completion of a Level 3 CACHE qualification in Early Years Education and Care (or an approved equivalent). EYE status is mandatory for practitioners to be counted in staff:child ratios in Early Years settings in England under statutory DfE guidance.

**Q: What is the difference between CACHE Level 2 and Level 3?**
Level 2 is an entry-level qualification assessing descriptive knowledge and basic reflections — suitable for nursery support workers and care assistants beginning their career. Level 3 is a full Diploma requiring analytical writing, Harvard referencing, child development theory application, EYFS framework knowledge, and a complete portfolio of evidence. Level 3 is required for Early Years Educator status.

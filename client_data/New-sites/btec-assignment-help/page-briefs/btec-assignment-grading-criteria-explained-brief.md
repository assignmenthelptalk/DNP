## EAV Depth Map
**Primary Entity**: BTEC grading criteria

**Main Attributes**: criterion code structure, grade band hierarchy, command verb taxonomy, criterion-referenced vs mark-based mechanics, qualification-level grade profiles

**Derived Attributes**:
- **Criterion Code Structure**: letter (P/M/D) + sequential number within band; P1/P2/P3 = Pass criteria in order; M1/M2 = Merit criteria; D1/D2 = Distinction criteria; number of criteria per band varies by unit (minimum 1 Distinction criterion; typically 3–5 Pass, 2–4 Merit, 1–3 Distinction)
- **Grade Band Hierarchy**: cumulative rule — Merit requires ALL Pass criteria met; Distinction requires ALL Pass AND ALL Merit criteria met; missing one P criterion = Referral regardless of D criterion quality
- **Command Verb Taxonomy by Band**: Pass = describe, identify, outline, state, list, explain, apply, demonstrate, produce (Bloom's Level 1–2: recall and comprehension); Merit = analyse, compare, discuss, justify, assess, examine, investigate, contrast (Bloom's Level 3–4: application and analysis); Distinction = evaluate, critically evaluate, synthesise, appraise, recommend, justify (Bloom's Level 5–6: synthesis and evaluation)
- **Criterion-Referenced Mechanics**: binary per criterion — met or not met; no partial marks; no compensatory marking; no holistic aggregate; each criterion independently assessed
- **Qualification-Level Grade Profiles**: unit grades aggregate to qualification grade; D* only at National Level 3 (not HNC/HND); requires achieving D across all contributing units to the required profile; UCAS tariff: D*=56pts, D=56pts, M=48pts, P=32pts per unit; D*D*D* Extended Diploma = 168 UCAS points (equivalent to 3 A*)
- **Referral Definition**: BTEC term for failing to meet all Pass criteria; not a partial pass; one resubmission permitted on internal units; external units: resit only

**Values per Attribute**:
- Pass verb characteristic: answers "what" — description, identification, application without analytical reasoning
- Merit verb characteristic: answers "how" and "why" — causal reasoning, comparison, structured argument
- Distinction verb characteristic: answers "to what extent" and "with what justification" — evaluative judgement, synthesis, reasoned conclusion
- Linked criteria: some units have P1/M1/D1 all addressing the same learning outcome at increasing depth — one extended piece of evidence can satisfy all three
- Independent criteria: P1 and D2 may address different learning outcomes entirely — separate evidence required

### FAQ SECTION

**Q: Can Distinction-quality writing compensate for a missed Pass criterion in BTEC?**
No. Criterion-referenced grading means each criterion is independently assessed. One unmet Pass criterion = Referral regardless of the quality of the rest of the submission.

**Q: What is the difference between Pass and Merit verbs in BTEC assignments?**
Pass verbs (describe, identify, explain) require accurate knowledge at Bloom's Level 1–2 — the "what." Merit verbs (analyse, compare, discuss) require structured analytical reasoning at Bloom's Level 3–4 — the "how" and "why."

**Q: How many criteria does a typical BTEC unit have?**
Most units have 6–12 criteria total: typically 3–5 Pass, 2–4 Merit, 1–3 Distinction. Count the criteria in your specific brief — there is no standard number across all units.

**Q: What does Distinction* mean in BTEC National grading?**
D* is awarded at National Level 3 qualification level (not on individual units, and not at HNC/HND) when the combination of unit grades across the qualification meets the D* profile threshold in the Pearson specification.

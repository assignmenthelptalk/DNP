#### H2: Harvard Referencing at HNC: Requirements, Format, and Common Errors
> **EAV to cover**: Harvard referencing format (in-text and reference list), source types required (academic vs non-academic), common errors that cause referrals, why it is introduced at HNC and not National
> **Word count**: 290–340 words
> **Specific content**:
> - Harvard referencing is mandatory at HNC for all written assignments — it is the standard referencing system used in UK higher education and is required by Pearson at Level 4
> - In-text citation format: (Author Surname, Year) for paraphrase; (Author Surname, Year: page number) for direct quotes — both must match an entry in the reference list
> - Reference list format varies by source type: books = Author Surname, Initial. (Year) *Title*. Edition. Place: Publisher; journal articles = Author Surname, Initial. (Year) 'Article title', *Journal Title*, Volume(Issue), pp. Page range
> - Academic sources required: textbooks, peer-reviewed journal articles, institutional reports, government publications; websites may supplement but should not dominate; Wikipedia is not an academic source and will cause a referral if used as a primary reference
> - Common referencing errors that cause referrals: in-text citation with no corresponding reference list entry (or vice versa); incorrect author format (first name instead of surname initial); no page number on direct quotes; using "anon" for institutional sources that have named authors; inconsistent formatting across entries
> - At Merit and above, tutors expect not only correct formatting but appropriate source selection — using only textbook definitions when journal articles are available signals limited academic engagement

### FAQ SECTION

**Q: Is Harvard referencing required for all BTEC HNC assignments?**
Harvard referencing is mandatory for all written assignments at HNC (Level 4). Tutors expect in-text citations and a complete reference list in every submitted piece. Assignments submitted without Harvard referencing, or with significant referencing errors, risk a Referral even if the substantive content meets the criteria. Academic sources — textbooks, peer-reviewed journals, and institutional reports — are required at Merit and Distinction level.

**Q: What is the difference between Merit and Distinction at HNC Level 4?**
Merit at HNC requires structured analysis: applying theories and models to a scenario, using academic sources correctly, and constructing a logical argument. Distinction requires critical evaluation: acknowledging the limitations of theories and approaches, synthesising multiple sources to build an original argument, and making evidence-based professional judgements. Merit demonstrates understanding and application; Distinction demonstrates critical thinking and academic judgement.

**Q: Can I get HNC assignment help if I have been referred on a unit?**
A Referral at HNC means one or more Pass criteria were not met. You have one resubmission opportunity on internally assessed units. Our service identifies the specific criteria that were not met, explains the evidence required to meet them, and provides a structured response guide for the resubmission — targeting only the unmet criteria, as your amended Assignment Brief specifies.

**Q: I passed BTEC National with Merit/Distinction. Why am I struggling at HNC?**
The Level 3 to Level 4 transition requires a significant change in writing style. At National, Merit and Distinction require analysis and evaluation at a Level 3 academic register. At HNC, even Pass criteria expect academic sourcing and structured analysis at Level 4 standard. The introduction of Harvard referencing as a new requirement — combined with longer assignments and higher register expectations — creates a common transition gap that many strong National students encounter.

## New H2 Section Specification

### H2: BTEC Engineering Distinction: What Technical Evaluation Looks Like Across Disciplines
> **EAV to cover**: Discipline-specific Distinction evidence — what evaluation means in Mechanical, Electrical/Electronic, Civil, and Manufacturing Engineering; specific frameworks and standards by discipline
> **Word count**: 300–360 words
> **Specific content**:
> - **Mechanical Engineering**: Distinction requires tolerance analysis — not just calculating a component dimension but evaluating whether the tolerance stack-up under worst-case conditions still falls within the functional specification; material selection evaluation using comparative data (density, yield strength, cost, machinability) rather than nominating a material and describing its properties; failure mode analysis identifying where the design is most vulnerable under realistic operating conditions
> - **Electrical and Electronic Engineering**: Distinction requires evaluating the difference between theoretical predictions and simulation or experimental results — identifying why a real circuit's frequency response or transient behaviour differs from the ideal, and quantifying the effect of component tolerances and parasitic elements on circuit performance. Referencing IEC standards (IEC 61000 for EMC compliance) demonstrates the standards awareness Distinction requires at HNC level
> - **Civil Engineering**: Distinction requires evaluating structural analysis results against code requirements — not merely checking whether a beam section passes the Eurocode bending moment calculation, but critically assessing the assumptions in the analysis (simply supported vs continuous, live load combinations, partial safety factors) and evaluating the design's performance under alternative loading scenarios. CDM Regulations 2015 compliance evaluation in design projects demonstrates legislative awareness
> - **Manufacturing Engineering**: Distinction requires process capability analysis — calculating Cp and Cpk values from process data, evaluating whether the process is capable of meeting the specification limits, and recommending process improvements with engineering justification (reduced variability through SPC, tool change frequency optimisation, material batch control). ISO 9001:2015 quality management system requirements provide the framework for manufacturing process evaluation at HNC and HND level

### FAQ SECTION

**Q: What is the difference between HNC and HND Health and Social Care assignment standards?**
HNC Health and Social Care Level 4 requires application of sociological theory and reflective writing to care practice contexts, with analysis and justification at Merit level. HND Health and Social Care Level 5 requires degree-equivalent critical evaluation and synthesis — synthesising multiple sociological frameworks into a coherent critical analysis of health inequality, critically appraising research evidence using CASP tools, evaluating leadership effectiveness in complex NHS contexts, and producing professional-quality public health strategy recommendations. The Level 5 standard also introduces systematic review methodology, evidence hierarchy evaluation, and leadership framework analysis that are not assessed at Level 4.

**Q: Which health promotion frameworks are covered in HND Promoting Public Health?**
HND Promoting Public Health typically covers: the Ottawa Charter (1986) — five action areas for health promotion; Beattie's model (1991) — four quadrants of health promotion approach; Marmot Review (2010) — six domains of social determinants of health; and Tannahill's model of health promotion. Distinction criteria require critical evaluation of the evidence base for each approach — not just describing the frameworks. Students must assess which health promotion approach is most effective for a specific public health priority, drawing on NICE guidelines and epidemiological evidence to support their evaluation.

**Q: What is CASP and why is it used in HND Health and Social Care research assignments?**
CASP (Critical Appraisal Skills Programme) provides standardised checklists for evaluating the quality of different types of health and social care research — separate checklists exist for randomised controlled trials (RCTs), systematic reviews, cohort studies, case-control studies, and qualitative research. CASP is used in HND HSC research assignments to apply the evidence hierarchy systematically — assessing whether the research was well-conducted (internal validity) and whether findings apply to the population of interest (external validity). At Distinction level, students must use CASP to critically evaluate specific studies cited in their literature review, not just describe the research approach.

**Q: Which legislation do HND Health and Social Care assignments at Level 5 require?**
HND Health and Social Care Level 5 assignments draw on a broader legislative framework than HNC Level 4. Core legislation includes: Care Act 2014 (adult social care duties and wellbeing principle), Mental Health Act 1983 (amended 2007 — detention, treatment, and community orders), Mental Capacity Act 2005 (capacity assessment, best interests, Deprivation of Liberty Safeguards), Health and Social Care Act 2012 (NHS structure, CCGs, Public Health England), Equality Act 2010 (protected characteristics, reasonable adjustments), and Human Rights Act 1998 (Articles 2, 3, 5, 8 relevant to care). Distinction criteria require critical evaluation of how specific legislation affects care management decisions and leadership practice — not just identification of the legislation.

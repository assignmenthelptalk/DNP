### H1: Real Estate Website Design in Kenya: Property Listings That Attract Buyers and Tenants

**Opening paragraph (50–80 words):**
A Kenyan property buyer's journey begins online — on Google, then on a real estate website. Whether they are searching for a 3-bedroom apartment in Kilimani or a commercial space in Westlands, the website that presents the most complete, most trustworthy property information wins the inquiry. A real estate website in Kenya is not a brochure — it is a searchable, filterable property database that earns WhatsApp messages and phone calls every day.

---

### H2: What Kenyan Property Buyers and Tenants Expect From a Real Estate Website

**Contextual directive:** Establish the Kenyan property buyer/tenant behaviour and what drives website-to-inquiry conversion in Kenya's property market.

**EAV depth required:**
- Kenyan property search behaviour: buyers start on Google ("3 bedroom apartment Kilimani for sale"), then visit agency websites, then contact via WhatsApp
- Key expectations:
  - Searchable listings: filter by location (Nairobi estate), price range (KES), property type (apartment, maisonette, bungalow, commercial), bedrooms
  - Detailed property information: accurate price (KES), square footage, bedroom count, amenities, nearby landmarks
  - Photos: minimum 5 exterior/interior photos per listing — Kenyan buyers expect multiple photos before any inquiry
  - Location on Google Maps: every listing must have a Google Maps pin — buyers verify the neighbourhood before inquiring
  - Agent contact: WhatsApp number on every listing — Kenyan property inquiries overwhelmingly start via WhatsApp
  - EARB registration: Estate Agents Registration Board (EARB) number displayed — required by law and expected by Kenyan property buyers for serious transactions

**Word count target:** 280–340 words

### H2: Property Listing System: Search, Filter, and Map for Kenyan Real Estate Websites

**Contextual directive:** Cover the technical architecture of a property listing system for Kenyan real estate websites.

**EAV depth required:**
- Listing database requirements:
  - Property types: residential (apartment, maisonette, bungalow, townhouse, villa), commercial (office, retail, warehouse, land), off-plan
  - Search/filter fields: location (Nairobi estate — Kilimani, Westlands, Lavington, Karen, Runda, Muthaiga, Kileleshwa, Lang'ata, Syokimau, Ruaka, Thindigua, Thika Road, Kiambu Road, South C), price range (KES), bedrooms (1, 2, 3, 4, 4+), property type, tenure (sale/rent)
  - Each listing page: price, bedrooms, bathrooms, size (sqm), floor (for apartments), furnishing status, parking, amenities, management fee (if applicable), available date, agent contact
- Google Maps integration: map view of all listings — Kenyan buyers use this to understand neighbourhood positioning
- Off-plan project pages: dedicated landing pages for new developments — floor plans, payment plans, developer info, construction progress gallery
- Rental vs sale distinction: clear visual differentiation — separate sections or filter — to avoid Kenyan buyer confusion

**Word count target:** 300–360 words

### H2: Agent Profile Pages With EARB Registration: Trust Signals for Kenyan Clients

**Contextual directive:** Cover agent credibility display — EARB registration is legally required and a critical trust signal for Kenyan property transactions.

**EAV depth required:**
- EARB (Estate Agents Registration Board) Kenya: all real estate agents practicing in Kenya must be registered with EARB — operating without registration is illegal under the Estate Agents Act
- EARB display: registration number on every agent profile, website footer, and About page
- Agent profile elements:
  - Professional photo
  - Full name and EARB registration number
  - Specialization areas (residential sales, commercial leasing, property management, off-plan sales)
  - Kenya estates specialised in (e.g., "Nairobi South: Karen, Lang'ata, Ngong Road corridor")
  - Years of experience in Kenya property market
  - Languages spoken (English, Swahili — and relevant local languages for upcountry markets)
  - WhatsApp number and direct phone — essential for Kenyan property inquiry
- Team page vs individual profiles: larger agencies may display a team page; individual agent profiles perform better for lead generation in Kenya's relationship-driven property market

**Word count target:** 280–340 words

### H2: Virtual Tours and Property Photography Integration for Kenyan Real Estate Sites

**Contextual directive:** Cover visual content requirements — the quality of property photography directly affects listing inquiry rates in Kenya's competitive real estate market.

**EAV depth required:**
- Kenyan buyer expectations:
  - Minimum 8–12 photos per listing (exterior, living areas, kitchen, master bedroom, bathrooms, outdoor/parking)
  - Photo quality: professional photography dramatically outperforms phone photos — in Kenya's premium residential market (Karen, Runda, Muthaiga), professional photography is now expected
  - Virtual tour options: Matterport 3D tours (premium), 360° photos (affordable alternative), YouTube video walkthroughs
  - Photo delivery: WebP format, max 80KB each — fast loading on Kenyan mobile networks
- Off-plan project galleries: construction progress photos (monthly updates) — high engagement from Kenyan off-plan buyers
- Floor plan display: PDF downloadable floor plans for apartments and houses — Kenyan buyers use these for furniture planning and to share with family
- Drone photography: aerial views showing neighbourhood context — growing adoption in Nairobi premium market

**Word count target:** 260–320 words

### H2: Mortgage Calculator and KRA Stamp Duty Tool: Conversion Features for Kenyan Buyers

**Contextual directive:** Cover financial tools that increase engagement and conversion on Kenyan real estate websites.

**EAV depth required:**
- Mortgage calculator for Kenya:
  - Inputs: property price (KES), down payment percentage (typical Kenya banks: 10–30%), loan term (5–25 years), interest rate (current Kenyan bank rates — typically 13–18% p.a. in 2024–2025)
  - Outputs: monthly repayment (KES), total interest paid, total cost
  - Kenya-specific: mortgage from commercial banks (KCB, Equity, NCBA, Co-op Bank, HF Group), housing fund (KMRC-backed affordable housing)
- KRA Stamp Duty calculator:
  - Formula: 4% of property value (urban property Kenya), 2% (rural property Kenya)
  - Essential for Kenyan property buyers — stamp duty is often overlooked until close of transaction
  - Displaying this tool saves buyers a call to their lawyer and keeps them on your website
- Legal fees estimator: based on Advocates Remuneration Order — estimated conveyancing fees for the property price range
- Value proposition: these financial tools keep Kenyan property buyers on the website longer, increase trust, and reduce inquiry friction

**Word count target:** 260–320 words

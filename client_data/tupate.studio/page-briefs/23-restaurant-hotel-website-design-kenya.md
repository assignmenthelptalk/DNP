### H1: Restaurant and Hotel Website Design in Kenya: Convert Searches Into Bookings and Reservations

**Opening paragraph (50–80 words):**
Every time a Kenyan diner searches "best restaurant Westlands" or an international tourist searches "lodge near Maasai Mara" and they click your website — that click is a revenue opportunity. A restaurant or hotel website in Kenya converts that opportunity when it shows compelling food photos, makes the menu immediately accessible, and lets the visitor book or reserve without calling first. A website that doesn't do this sends the visitor to TripAdvisor or Booking.com instead.

---

### H2: What Kenyan Diners and Guests Look for on a Restaurant or Hotel Website

**Contextual directive:** Establish Kenyan hospitality consumer behaviour — how diners and guests use websites in their booking decision.

**EAV depth required:**
- Restaurant website decision factors for Kenya:
  - Menu visibility: the menu must be viewable without downloading a PDF — digital menus with food photos, pricing in KES, and dietary labels (vegetarian, halal, vegan — important in Nairobi's diverse market)
  - Opening hours: accurate, including weekend hours — Nairobi restaurants see high Friday/Saturday evening traffic
  - Table availability: WhatsApp reservation number prominently displayed — most Kenyan restaurant reservations are made via WhatsApp, not online forms
  - Location with map: exact Google Maps embed with directions — particularly critical for restaurants in Nairobi's complex road system
  - Food photos: high-quality food photography is the #1 conversion driver for Kenyan restaurant websites
- Hotel/lodge website decision factors:
  - Room types and rates: KES pricing displayed — Kenyan domestic travellers search by price range
  - Availability and booking: direct booking engine or clear WhatsApp booking path
  - Facilities: photos of rooms, pool, conference facilities, restaurant
  - Location: distance from Nairobi (for upcountry), nearest gate (for safari lodges), GPS coordinates
  - Testimonials: TripAdvisor reviews linked or embedded — Kenyan and international travellers both rely on reviews

**Word count target:** 300–360 words

---

### H2: Digital Menu Design for Kenyan Restaurants: Photos, Pricing, and Dietary Labels

**Contextual directive:** Cover digital menu design as a primary conversion element for Kenyan restaurant websites.

**EAV depth required:**
- Digital menu vs PDF menu: PDF menus are difficult to view on mobile phones (80%+ of Kenyan diners browse on phone), cannot be indexed by Google, and cannot include food photos — HTML menus solve all three problems
- Menu structure:
  - Starters, mains, desserts, drinks — clear category navigation
  - Each dish: name, description (ingredients, preparation style), price in KES, dietary tags (Vegetarian V, Vegan VE, Halal H, Gluten-Free GF), food photo
- Nairobi dietary considerations:
  - Halal certification: critical for restaurants serving Nairobi's Muslim community — clearly stated, halal certification logo displayed
  - Vegetarian options: growing demand in Nairobi — visible vegetarian menu section
  - International cuisine labeling: Kenyan diners at upmarket restaurants expect description of unfamiliar dishes
- Pricing in KES: all prices in Kenyan Shillings — USD pricing signals tourist pricing and alienates Kenyan domestic diners
- Menu update process: menu managed through admin panel — staff update prices without developer involvement (essential for restaurants with seasonal/changing menus)
- Special menus: Sunday buffet, lunch specials, seasonal promotions — separate highlighted section

**Word count target:** 300–360 words

### H2: Direct Hotel Booking vs OTA Dependency: Why Kenyan Hotels Need Their Own Booking Engine

**Contextual directive:** Cover the direct booking engine case — the highest-value conversion element for Kenyan hotels.

**EAV depth required:**
- OTA commission problem: Booking.com, Expedia, and Airbnb charge 15–25% commission on every booking from Kenyan hotels — a Ksh 10,000 room booking costs Ksh 1,500–2,500 in commission
- Direct booking advantages for Kenyan hotels:
  - Zero commission on direct bookings
  - Guest data ownership (for repeat marketing)
  - Rate parity control (no OTA undercutting)
  - Upsell opportunities at booking (room upgrade, airport transfer, safari package)
- Direct booking engine options for Kenya:
  - Cloudbeds (integrates with Kenyan banks, M-Pesa via Pesapal)
  - Little Hotelier (designed for small Kenya properties)
  - Custom-built booking system by Tupate Studio (for unique requirements)
  - Simple WhatsApp-based booking with M-Pesa payment (for small lodges/B&Bs)
- Best available rate guarantee: display "Book Direct — Best Rate Guaranteed" — encourages Kenyan and international guests to bypass OTAs
- M-Pesa payment for bookings: domestic Kenyan guests prefer M-Pesa payment — booking engine must support M-Pesa via Daraja API or Pesapal

**Word count target:** 300–360 words

### H2: Photo Gallery Optimisation for Kenyan Hospitality Websites

**Contextual directive:** Cover photography as the primary visual conversion driver for hospitality websites.

**EAV depth required:**
- Why photography is critical for Kenyan hospitality: travellers and diners make visual decisions — a restaurant or lodge with outstanding photos receives 3× more booking inquiries than equivalent properties with poor photos
- Photo categories for hotels/lodges:
  - Room types: each room category with multiple angles (sleeping area, bathroom, view)
  - Common areas: lobby, pool, gym, spa, restaurant
  - Food and beverage: restaurant plating, bar setup, breakfast spread
  - Activities: game drives (safari lodges), water sports (coastal properties), conference setup
  - Exterior: building façade, gardens, surrounding landscape
- Photo requirements: professional photography minimum — 24MP resolution, proper lighting, styled interiors
- WebP optimization: hospitality sites are photo-heavy — WebP format with lazy loading essential for Kenya mobile performance (each image max 80KB)
- Upcountry Kenya specifics: safari lodge photos must show the natural environment context (Mara savannah, coastal mangroves, Mt Kenya backdrop) — Kenyan and international travellers choose lodges based on environment as much as facilities

**Word count target:** 260–320 words

---

### H2: WhatsApp Booking Integration: How Kenyan Guests Actually Make Reservations

**Contextual directive:** Cover WhatsApp as the primary booking channel for Kenyan hospitality businesses — a cultural reality that most website guides ignore.

**EAV depth required:**
- WhatsApp dominance in Kenyan hospitality: the majority of Kenyan restaurant table reservations and lodge bookings are initiated via WhatsApp — not through online forms or phone calls
- WhatsApp button implementation: each page has a click-to-WhatsApp button (wa.me link) with pre-filled message: "I'd like to make a reservation for [date/time/party size]"
- WhatsApp Business features: automated greeting message, away message (outside business hours), quick replies for common questions (menu, pricing, availability)
- WhatsApp Business API: for high-volume hospitality businesses — enables automated booking confirmation, appointment reminders, and post-stay review requests
- Balancing WhatsApp vs online booking: display both options prominently — some international guests prefer online booking confirmation documents; domestic Kenyan guests prefer WhatsApp flexibility
- TripAdvisor and Google Reviews integration: link from website to Google Reviews and TripAdvisor profile — Kenyan and international guests check these before booking

**Word count target:** 260–320 words

### H2: Upcountry Kenya and Safari Lodge Website Design: International and Domestic Traveller Needs

**Contextual directive:** Cover the unique requirements of upcountry hospitality websites — a significant market for Tupate Studio given the business plan's upcountry focus.

**EAV depth required:**
- International vs domestic traveller design considerations:
  - International travellers (US, UK, Europe): USD pricing option, English description of Kenyan wildlife/culture context, high-resolution photos, conservation messaging, departure city flight information (Nairobi → Mara via chartered flights)
  - Domestic Kenyan travellers: KES pricing, driving directions from Nairobi (hours, road quality), WhatsApp booking, weekend package pricing
- Location information for upcountry properties:
  - GPS coordinates (for Google Maps navigation in areas without precise address)
  - Distance from Nairobi and nearest town
  - Road type (tarmac/murram — critical for guest vehicle planning)
  - Nearest airstrip (for fly-in guests)
- Safari lodge content specific needs:
  - Game sighting reports (wildlife blog updated regularly — drives organic traffic and repeat visitor engagement)
  - Safari packages with clear pricing (full board, half board, game drive inclusions)
  - Conservation and community story pages — increasingly expected by international eco-tourists
- Booking seasons calendar: peak season (Jul–Oct for Mara migration), green season discounts — helps guests plan and sets revenue expectations

**Word count target:** 280–340 words

### H1: Landing Page Design in Kenya: Convert Visitors Into Leads and Customers

**Opening paragraph (50–80 words):**
Define a landing page from the Kenyan business perspective: a landing page is a single-purpose web page where a visitor from a Google Ad, social media post, or WhatsApp link "lands" — and either converts (calls, WhatsApps, buys) or leaves. Unlike a full website with navigation, multiple pages, and multiple purposes, a landing page has one goal and one conversion action. For Kenyan businesses running Google Ads, the landing page determines whether ad spend becomes revenue.

---

### H2: What Makes a Landing Page Different From a Regular Website Page

**Contextual directive:** Establish the structural and strategic difference between a landing page and a standard website page. This section serves the "what is a landing page" informational query.

**EAV depth required:**
- Entity: Landing Page (Kenya)
- Structural differences:
  - No navigation menu: removes exit paths — Kenyan visitors stay focused on the conversion action
  - Single CTA: one call to action repeated 2–3 times on the page (vs website pages that may have 5–10 different links)
  - Message match: headline exactly matches the Google Ad or social post that sent the visitor — "We offer website design" on the ad, "Professional Website Design for Kenyan Businesses" on the landing page
  - No footer links: eliminates distractions — Kenyan user stays on conversion path
  - No blog, no about page link, no social media icons in the body
- Purpose types for Kenyan businesses:
  - Lead generation: collect WhatsApp number or email for follow-up
  - Sales: direct purchase via M-Pesa (product or service)
  - Appointment booking: click to book → booking form
  - Event registration: webinar, training, open day
  - Free resource download: in exchange for contact details

**Word count target:** 280–340 words

**EAV depth required:**

### H2: Trust Signals That Convert Kenyan Website Visitors

**Contextual directive:** Cover the specific trust signals that Kenyan online visitors need before taking conversion actions — these are culturally specific and critical.

**EAV depth required:**
- Physical presence signals: Nairobi office address displayed prominently (even if remote-first) — Kenyan online buyers distrust businesses with no physical address
- Phone number: visible in header and CTA — clickable tel: link for mobile visitors — Kenyan buyers often call before WhatsApp-ing a new business
- WhatsApp number: WhatsApp button with the number displayed — Kenyans prefer WhatsApp over forms for first contact
- M-Pesa payment badge: if selling directly on the landing page — "We accept M-Pesa" with paybill number displayed prominently
- KRA PIN / Business Registration: for B2B landing pages, displaying KRA PIN number signals legitimacy to Kenyan business buyers
- Social proof freshness: testimonials with dates — Kenyans are skeptical of undated reviews
- SSL padlock: HTTPS is visible in browser bar — Chrome shows "Not Secure" for HTTP pages, destroys conversions
- Google reviews screenshot: screenshots of 4–5 star Google reviews (with reviewer names visible) convert better than text testimonials on Kenyan landing pages

**Word count target:** 280–340 words

### H2: Mobile Landing Page Design for Kenya's Phone-First Market

**Contextual directive:** Address mobile-specific landing page design for Kenya — where 80%+ of Google Ads clicks from Kenyan traffic land on mobile screens.

**EAV depth required:**
- Mobile landing page requirements for Kenya:
  - Above-fold visibility on 5–6 inch Android screen (Tecno Spark, Samsung A14): headline, sub-headline, and CTA button must all be visible without scrolling
  - Click-to-call button: one-tap calling — critical for Kenyan mobile visitors who prefer voice over forms
  - WhatsApp button: opens WhatsApp chat directly — highest conversion action for Kenyan B2B landing pages
  - Form field minimization: maximum 3 fields on mobile forms (Name, Phone/WhatsApp, Service Needed) — more fields = abandoned form
  - Tap target sizing: minimum 44×44px buttons — Kenyan budget Android users have less precise touch control on smaller screens
  - Page weight: maximum 500KB total for mobile — keeps load time under 2.5s on Safaricom 4G
  - No pop-ups on mobile: Google penalizes interstitials on mobile that cover content — also infuriates Kenyan mobile users

**Word count target:** 280–340 words

### H2: Landing Pages for Google Ads: What Google Rewards in Quality Score

**Contextual directive:** Connect landing page design to Google Ads Quality Score — the metric that directly affects how much a Kenyan business pays per click and whether its ads show at all.

**EAV depth required:**
- Quality Score definition: Google's 1–10 rating of ad relevance × expected CTR × landing page experience
- Landing page experience components (Google's scoring):
  - Message match: landing page content must closely match the ad's keywords and copy — "Kenyan dental services" ad → landing page must prominently feature dental services in Kenya
  - Page load speed: slow landing pages receive low Quality Scores — Google explicitly penalizes slow pages in ad auctions
  - Mobile-friendliness: tested by Google's mobile-first crawl
  - Content relevance: the landing page must contain the keywords from the ad campaign
  - Transparency: clear information about the business, privacy policy link (required by Kenya Data Protection Act)
- Quality Score impact: a Quality Score of 8/10 vs 4/10 for the same keyword can reduce Cost Per Click by 30–50% — significant for Kenyan ad budgets priced in KES
- Dedicated landing pages vs homepage: sending Google Ads traffic to a homepage typically scores 3–5; dedicated landing pages typically score 6–9

**Word count target:** 280–340 words

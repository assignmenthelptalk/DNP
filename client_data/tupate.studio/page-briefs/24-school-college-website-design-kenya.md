### H1: School and College Website Design in Kenya: Attract Enrolments and Build Community Trust

**Opening paragraph (50–80 words):**
A Kenyan parent searching for a school for their child visits 4–6 school websites before contacting any school directly. What they're looking for: fee structure, curriculum clarity, facilities photos, academic results, and safety. The school website that communicates all of this clearly, in a way that matches their child's needs, earns the call. The school website that buries fees, shows no photos, and doesn't mention KCSE results loses that enrolment to a competitor that does.

---

### H2: What Kenyan Parents and Students Look for on a School or College Website

**Contextual directive:** Define the parent/student decision-making process in Kenya — what information they need from a school website before making first contact.

**EAV depth required:**
- Entity: Kenyan Parent / Student Decision-Maker
- Primary information sought:
  - Fee structure: Ksh per term (tuition, boarding, activity fees, exam fees, uniform costs) — transparency is the #1 conversion driver for Kenyan school websites. Parents comparison-shop schools by fees before visiting
  - Curriculum: CBC (Competency-Based Curriculum for primary/junior secondary), 8-4-4 (legacy for existing secondary students), IGCSE, A-level — clearly stated which system the school follows
  - Academic results: KCSE mean grade (for secondary schools), KCPE results (for primary schools), tertiary placement rates (for colleges)
  - Location: Google Maps embed — Kenyan parents assess commute feasibility
  - Boarding vs day school options: clearly stated with separate pricing
  - Facilities: photos of classrooms, science labs, sports fields, boarding facilities, computer labs
  - Extracurricular activities: sports teams, music, drama, debate — important for Kenyan parents' holistic education decision

**Word count target:** 300–360 words

---

### H2: Admissions Pages for Kenyan Schools: Entry Requirements, Applications, and Deadlines

**Contextual directive:** Cover the admissions section — the conversion mechanism for Kenyan school websites.

**EAV depth required:**
- Admissions section structure:
  - Entry requirements: KCPE minimum grade for secondary school entry, KCSE minimum for Form 6 or college entry — state specific cut-offs (e.g., "Minimum KCPE score of 350/500 for Form 1 entry")
  - Application form: download PDF application form (most Kenyan schools still use physical applications in 2025) OR online application form — provide both options
  - Required documents for admission: birth certificate, KCPE/KCSE certificate, passport photos, transfer letter (for transfers), medical certificate
  - Application deadlines: term dates in Kenya — three terms (Term 1: Jan–Apr, Term 2: May–Aug, Term 3: Sept–Nov) — application deadlines per intake
  - Interview process: if applicable — state whether interviews are held and what they assess
  - Waiting list policy: for oversubscribed Kenyan schools — transparent policy builds parent trust
- Online application workflow:
  - Form fields: student name, DOB, previous school, KCPE/KCSE result, parent contact, preferred entry date
  - Submission: email confirmation to parent, WhatsApp notification to admissions team
  - Follow-up: admissions team contacts parent via WhatsApp within 24 hours

**Word count target:** 300–360 words

---

### H2: Fee Structure Transparency: Why Kenyan Parents Need Clear Pricing Before Enquiring

**Contextual directive:** Cover fee transparency as the most powerful conversion element for Kenyan school websites — and the most commonly withheld.

**EAV depth required:**
- Why Kenyan schools hide fees (and why they shouldn't): fear of price comparison, fear of deterring price-sensitive parents
- Why fee transparency converts more Kenyan enrolments: parents who see fees and proceed to contact are pre-qualified leads who have already accepted the price — they are ready to enroll; parents who don't see fees and contact are half as likely to proceed
- Fee page structure for Kenyan school websites:
  - Day school vs boarding fee distinction
  - Term-by-term breakdown (Ksh per term)
  - What's included: tuition, learning materials, meals (boarding), uniform, activities
  - What's not included: transport (if bus service is separate), extra-curricular fees, exam fees
  - Payment options: MPESA Paybill number with instructions, bank account details, installment payment option (if available)
  - Fee review schedule: "Fees reviewed annually at the beginning of each academic year"
- Bursary/scholarship information: if the school offers financial assistance — stated on the fees page
- Comparison-shopping reality: Kenyan parents compare 4–6 schools by fee structure — a website with hidden fees is not shortlisted for that comparison

**Word count target:** 300–360 words

---

### H2: Academic Programme Pages: CBC, KCSE, and Professional Certification for Kenyan Institutions

**Contextual directive:** Cover curriculum and programme pages — critical for parents and students evaluating institutional fit.

**EAV depth required:**
- Primary schools (CBC focus):
  - Competency-Based Curriculum overview (Grade 1–9 under Kenya's new system as of 2019–2024 rollout)
  - Junior Secondary (Grade 7–9) — schools that have upgraded to include JSS should state this prominently
  - Learning areas covered: Languages, Mathematics, Science, Social Studies, Creative Arts, Hygiene & Nutrition, Religious Education
  - Transition to Senior Secondary: CBC Grade 10–12 pathway clarity for parents
- Secondary schools:
  - KCSE preparation: mean grade history for the school (last 5 years) — displays academic performance trend
  - Subject options: Sciences, Arts, Commercial subjects — what elective pathways the school offers
  - University placement: percentage of leavers admitted to Kenyan public universities (KUCCPS), private universities, international universities
- Professional training institutes:
  - Courses accredited by: TVETA (Technical and Vocational Education and Training Authority), KNEC, relevant professional bodies (ICPAK, LSK, NCA Kenya)
  - Course duration, start dates, examination bodies
  - Job placement rates after completion

**Word count target:** 280–340 words

### H2: Photo Gallery and Facilities Showcase for Kenyan School Websites

**Contextual directive:** Cover visual content requirements for school websites — the second-most impactful conversion element after fee transparency.

**EAV depth required:**
- Kenyan parent visual decision factors: parents visit school websites to see classrooms, boarding facilities, labs, and sports facilities before deciding whether to visit physically
- Photo categories essential for Kenyan school websites:
  - Classrooms: well-lit, furnished, showing teaching materials and student work displays
  - Science labs: equipment quality signal — particularly important for secondary schools competing on academic performance
  - Computer labs: number of computers, internet connectivity displayed (important for Kenyan parents prioritizing digital literacy)
  - Sports facilities: football pitch, athletics track, basketball court, swimming pool (if applicable)
  - Boarding facilities: dormitories, dining hall, bathrooms — boarding school parents specifically assess safety and hygiene through photos
  - Graduation/events: students in uniform, prize-giving ceremony, sports day — community and achievement signal
- Video content: 2–3 minute virtual school tour video on YouTube embedded on website — 3–5× more engagement than static photos for Kenyan school websites
- Before-photo timing: fresh photos annually — outdated school photos (showing old classrooms or outdated uniforms) undermine trust

**Word count target:** 260–320 words

### H2: Local SEO for Schools: Ranking When Parents Search for Schools in Your Area

**Contextual directive:** Cover local SEO for Kenyan schools — the highest-traffic organic opportunity for most school websites.

**EAV depth required:**
- Kenyan school search patterns:
  - "primary school [Nairobi area]" — "primary school Runda," "primary school Kiambu"
  - "boarding school Kenya" — high volume, national competition
  - "IGCSE school Nairobi"
  - "CBC school [county name]"
  - "affordable secondary school [town name] Kenya"
- Google Business Profile for schools: category "School" or "College" — appears in Google Maps when parents search by area
- School location page targeting: multiple campuses need individual location pages
- Neighbourhood relevance: school website must mention specific Nairobi estates or county towns it serves (e.g., "serving families in Runda, Ridgeways, and Gigiri")
- KCSE/KCPE results page: annually updated academic results page — generates fresh content and seasonal organic traffic during results season (November KCSE, March KCPE)

**Word count target:** 250–300 words

### SUPP H2: School Website FAQs for Kenyan Institutions

**FAQ entries:**
1. Q: How much does a school website cost in Kenya? A: A standard Kenyan school website at Tupate Studio — admissions pages, fee structure, curriculum pages, photo gallery, Google Maps, and contact — costs Ksh 25,000. School websites with online application forms and M-Pesa payment integration start from Ksh 35,000. Parent portals and school management system integration are quoted separately.
2. Q: Should a Kenyan school website show exam results? A: Yes — displaying historical KCSE/KCPE results is a significant competitive advantage for well-performing Kenyan schools. Parents actively compare academic performance. Results should be presented professionally (mean grade trend, top performer count) — not as individual student scores which would violate privacy.
3. Q: How often should a school website be updated? A: Minimum: term dates, fee structure (annually), and news/events (termly). Schools that update their website regularly with sports results, academic achievements, and event photos retain higher Google Maps rankings and build stronger parent community engagement.
4. Q: Can parents register their children through the school website? A: Yes — Tupate Studio builds online application forms for Kenyan school websites. Parents submit their application, supporting documents, and a registration deposit via M-Pesa — streamlining the admissions process.

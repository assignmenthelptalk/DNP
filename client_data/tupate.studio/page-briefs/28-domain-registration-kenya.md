### H2: .co.ke vs .com: Which Domain Extension for a Kenyan Business?

**EAV depth required:**
- .co.ke attributes:
  - Kenya ccTLD (Country Code Top-Level Domain) — managed by KENIC (Kenya Network Information Centre)
  - Local SEO advantage: Google gives a mild ranking preference to ccTLDs for local searches — .co.ke can perform marginally better in Google.co.ke results for Kenya-only businesses
  - Trust signal for Kenyan audience: Kenyan customers recognize .co.ke as a Kenyan business identifier — builds local trust
  - Cost: Ksh 1,200–2,500/year through KENIC-accredited registrars
  - Example: tuskers.co.ke, kenyaairways.co.ke, equity.co.ke
- .com attributes:
  - Global TLD — no geographic restriction
  - Widely recognized internationally and by Kenyan audiences alike
  - Better for: businesses with international clients, NGOs, businesses planning regional expansion
  - Cost: USD 10–15/year (Ksh 1,300–2,000 at current rates)
  - Example: safaricom.com, kenyaairways.com (many large Kenyan brands hold both)
- Recommendation: for a Kenyan B2B or B2C business serving primarily the Kenyan market — register .co.ke as primary. Register .com as secondary to prevent brand squatting. If budget allows only one: .co.ke for local businesses, .com for businesses with international ambitions.

### H2: Domain Privacy and WHOIS Protection for Kenyan Businesses

**EAV depth required:**
- WHOIS: public database that shows who registered a domain — by default includes registrant name, address, phone, email
- Privacy risk: without WHOIS protection, any person can look up the business owner's personal details for a .co.ke or .com domain
- Domain privacy/WHOIS protection: replaces your personal details with the registrar's proxy information in the public WHOIS database
- Availability: not all Kenyan registrars offer WHOIS protection for .co.ke domains — .com domains can always use WHOIS privacy through registrar proxy services
- Recommendation: enable WHOIS privacy if available — prevents spam, cold calls, and reduces risk of domain hijacking attempts

**Word count target:** 200–250 words

### H2: DNS Management and Connecting Your Domain to Your Website

**EAV depth required:**
- DNS (Domain Name System): the system that translates your domain name (e.g., businessname.co.ke) into a server IP address
- DNS records relevant to Kenyan business websites:
  - A record: points domain to web hosting server IP address
  - CNAME: points subdomain (www) to root domain
  - MX record: routes email to your email provider (Google Workspace, Microsoft 365, Zoho Mail Kenya)
  - TXT record: used for email authentication (SPF, DKIM, DMARC) and domain verification
- DNS propagation: after changing DNS settings, changes take 24–48 hours to propagate globally — during this time the website may not be accessible from all locations
- Nameservers: if using Cloudflare for CDN/performance, change nameservers from registrar to Cloudflare — Cloudflare then manages all DNS records with performance and security benefits
- Tupate Studio note: we handle DNS configuration as part of all website projects — clients need only purchase the domain and provide us registrar access

### SUPP H2: Domain Registration FAQs for Kenyan Businesses

**FAQ entries:**
1. Q: How much does a .co.ke domain cost in Kenya? A: A .co.ke domain costs Ksh 1,200–2,500/year through KENIC-accredited registrars. Truehost Kenya offers .co.ke at Ksh 1,500/year. Renewal prices are typically the same as the first-year price — no introductory discounts on .co.ke.
2. Q: Can Tupate Studio register the domain for me? A: Yes — we can assist with domain registration and handle all DNS configuration as part of your website project. We register the domain in your name (you own it) — Tupate Studio is never the domain owner.
3. Q: What happens if I don't renew my domain? A: Your domain expires and becomes available for anyone to register. Within 30–60 days after expiry, the domain enters a redemption period (recoverable at a premium fee). After 60–90 days, it is deleted and available publicly. Always auto-renew your domain to prevent accidental loss.
4. Q: Should I register multiple domain extensions (e.g., both .co.ke and .com)? A: Yes — register both if budget allows. This prevents competitors or squatters from registering the .com version of your .co.ke domain (or vice versa) and directing traffic away from your business.

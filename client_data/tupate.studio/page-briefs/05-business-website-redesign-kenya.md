### H2: Signs Your Kenyan Business Website Needs a Redesign

**Contextual directive:** Problem-awareness section. Help business owners self-diagnose. Each sign is a specific, recognizable symptom — not abstract advice.

**EAV depth required:**
- Entity: Outdated Business Website (Kenya)
- Redesign trigger attributes with specific values:

1. **Mobile failure:** site is not responsive — zoom required on a phone. In Kenya where 80% of users are on mobile, this is catastrophic. Google Mobile-First Indexing has been penalizing non-mobile sites since 2019.
2. **Speed failure:** loads in 6+ seconds on 4G. Each second over 3 seconds loses 7–10% of visitors on Kenyan mobile networks.
3. **Design age > 3 years:** flat design, outdated typography, no white space. Visitors leave within 3 seconds — credibility is judged in 50 milliseconds.
4. **No HTTPS:** Google Chrome shows "Not Secure" warning. Kenyan users, increasingly aware of online fraud, abandon HTTP sites.
5. **Not ranking on Google:** organic traffic has been declining for 12+ months (visible in Google Search Console if set up).
6. **High bounce rate:** Google Analytics shows 70%+ bounce rate — visitors landing and leaving immediately, indicating disconnect between expectation and reality.
7. **No clear CTA:** visitors cannot find phone number, WhatsApp button, or "Contact Us" without effort — this is a design failure, not a marketing failure.
8. **Competitor websites are better:** a Kenyan business owner visits a competitor's site and it looks more professional. Credibility gap = lost clients.
9. **WordPress plugin chaos:** site breaks every 2–3 months because of plugin conflicts, update failures, or hacking — Kenyan WordPress sites are targeted routinely.

### H2: How Website Redesign Affects Your SEO Rankings in Kenya

**Contextual directive:** Address the #1 fear: "if I redesign my website, will I lose my Google rankings?" This section is critical for converting prospects who have hesitated to redesign because of SEO risk.

**EAV depth required:**
- The risk is real: poorly executed redesigns DO cause ranking drops. Common mistakes:
  1. Changing URL structure without 301 redirects — every old URL becomes a 404 error, destroying ranking history
  2. Changing page titles and H1s without query research — losing the keyword relevance built over time
  3. Removing content — eliminating pages that had ranking value
  4. Changing domain/subdomain — resets domain authority entirely
  5. Removing internal links — breaks PageRank flow through the site
- The SEO-safe redesign process: audit existing rankings first, map all current URLs, implement 301 redirects for every changed URL, preserve or improve all title tags, carry over and improve meta descriptions, maintain or improve content depth, rebuild internal link structure, re-submit XML sitemap after launch
- Google's position: redesigns that improve Core Web Vitals and mobile experience while preserving content generally see rankings recover and improve within 2–4 months
- Tupate Studio's approach: we audit existing rankings BEFORE redesigning, create a redirect map, and monitor Google Search Console weekly for 90 days post-launch to catch and fix any ranking drops immediately

**Word count target:** 350–420 words

### H2: Website Audit Before Redesign: What We Check for Kenyan Sites

**Contextual directive:** Demonstrate methodology. This builds trust by showing Tupate Studio's systematic approach before a single line of code is written.

**EAV depth required:**
- Pre-redesign audit checklist:
  1. Google Search Console: current ranking positions, indexed pages, crawl errors, Core Web Vitals report
  2. Google Analytics: top traffic pages, bounce rate by page, mobile vs desktop ratio, conversion events
  3. Current URL structure: document all URLs, identify which have ranking value
  4. Backlink audit (Ahrefs/SEMrush): which pages have inbound links — these must be preserved exactly
  5. Page speed test: Google PageSpeed Insights, GTmetrix — current scores and specific failing elements
  6. Mobile usability: Google Search Console mobile usability report
  7. Content audit: identify pages with strong content vs thin content
  8. Competitor benchmark: 3 top-ranking Kenya competitors — what design standards are they meeting?
- Output: a Redesign Brief document that guides the rebuild and ensures no ranking equity is abandoned
- Time investment: audit takes 3–5 business days for a standard Kenyan business website

### H2: Mobile-First Redesign for Kenya's Mobile Internet Market

**Contextual directive:** Connect redesign to mobile-first imperative. A redesign that doesn't prioritize mobile is a failed redesign in Kenya's market.

**EAV depth required:**
- Responsive vs mobile-specific: responsive design (one codebase that adapts to all screens) is the modern standard — mobile-specific subdomains (m.website.co.ke) are outdated
- Design for lowest common device: Tecno Spark, Infinix Hot — budget Android devices with 5-inch screens running Chrome on Android 10–12
- Touch targets: 44×44px minimum for any clickable element
- Typography: minimum 16px body text on mobile — Kenyan mobile users on 5-inch screens find 12px text unusable
- No hover menus: hover states don't exist on touchscreens — all navigation must work by tap
- Performance: target LCP under 2.5 seconds on Safaricom 4G (measured from Nairobi server)
- Google Mobile-First Indexing: if the redesigned site's mobile version has less content than the desktop version, it will rank lower than the old site

**Word count target:** 260–320 words

### H2: Modern Website Design Standards for Kenyan Businesses in 2025

**Contextual directive:** What "modern" means in Kenya's 2025 web design context. Grounds the aesthetic conversation in functional standards.

**EAV depth required:**
- 2025 Kenya design standards:
  - Clean typography: Inter, Plus Jakarta Sans, or similar — loaded as system fonts or WOFF2 self-hosted (not Google Fonts blocking render)
  - Color: high contrast ratios (WCAG 2.1 AA minimum) — primary color + white + dark gray system
  - Hero section: full viewport, single CTA, value proposition in under 10 words, mobile hero loads under 1.5 seconds
  - Section structure: problem → solution → evidence → CTA — repeated across scroll
  - Social proof placement: testimonials above footer on every service page
  - WhatsApp float button: bottom-right sticky button — present on every page of Kenyan business websites
  - Dark mode: 2025 standard for professional sites; optional but expected by tech-savvy Kenyan audience
  - Microanimations: subtle scroll-triggered animations — signals code quality and modernity; must not impact Core Web Vitals

**Word count target:** 240–290 words

#### CII Diploma in Insurance — Core Units

| Unit | Full Name | Credits | Level | Priority |

**M05 EAV Depth Note — Critical:** M05 is known as the hardest CII unit. Case law is not optional — it IS the depth:
- Utmost good faith: *Consumer Insurance Act 2012* (inadvertent vs deliberate/reckless), *Insurance Act 2015* (business — fair presentation duty)
- Insurable interest: *Macaura v Northern Assurance* (shares ≠ insurable interest), *Life Assurance Act 1774*
- Subrogation: *Castellain v Preston 1883* (timing — after indemnity paid)
- Contribution: *North British & Mercantile Insurance v London* (rateable proportion)
- Proximate cause: *Leyland Shipping v Norwich Union*, *Wayne Tank v Employers Liability*
- Warranties post-Insurance Act 2015: s.10 — breach suspends cover, doesn't terminate

**Pages that mention these concepts have NOT covered M05. Pages that define the case law holding, connect it to the relevant principle, and show its application in exam scenarios — that is coverage.**

### TIER 1 — Foundational Insurance Concepts (Publish Weeks 19–22)

#### 1. The Six Principles of Insurance Law
- **EAV depth required:** Each principle must be defined with its PRIMARY CASE LAW:
  - Utmost good faith → *Consumer Insurance Act 2012*, *Insurance Act 2015* (the change), *Société Anonyme d'Intermediaires Luxembourgeois v Farex*
  - Insurable interest → *Macaura v Northern Assurance*, *Life Assurance Act 1774*, *Marine Insurance Act 1906 s.5*
  - Subrogation → *Castellain v Preston 1883* (must receive indemnity first), waiver of subrogation
  - Contribution → rateable proportion formula
  - Proximate cause → *Leyland Shipping v Norwich Union*, *Wayne Tank*, concurrent cause rule
- **Bridge to core:** IF1, IF3, M05

#### 2. Lloyd's of London — Market Structure, Syndicates and Underwriting Process
- **EAV depth required:** Names vs corporate members, syndicates, managing agents, Lloyd's governance (Council, Franchise Board), slip process, lead/follow underwriting, Lloyd's Chain of Security (three links — premium trust funds, member funds, central assets), Lloyd's financial ratings (S&P A+), PPL electronic placement platform, Blueprint Two reform
- **Bridge to core:** LM3, M80 (M81 broking context), 930, 960, 991

#### 3. Reinsurance Fundamentals — Treaty, Facultative and the Global Market
- **EAV depth required:** Proportional (quota share vs surplus — cession %, pro-rata premium), non-proportional (risk XL vs cat XL vs aggregate — per risk retention, per occurrence limit, annual aggregate deductible), facultative (per risk, prior agreement), burning cost pricing, rate on line, retrocession, global centres (London, Munich Re, Swiss Re, Bermuda), credit risk in reinsurance
- **Bridge to core:** M97, 930, 960, 993

#### 4. UK Insurance Regulation — FCA, PRA, Solvency II and IDD
- **EAV depth required:** FCA/ICOBS, PRA/Solvency II (SCR, MCR, ORSA, technical provisions), IDD (25-hour CPD for distributors, demands and needs test, product oversight and governance), Consumer Duty for insurers (four outcomes — products and services, price and value, consumer understanding, consumer support), Solvency UK post-Brexit divergence
- **Bridge to core:** IF1, M92, 990, 992

#### 5. Enterprise Risk Management in Insurance — ISO 31000, ERM and ORSA
- **EAV depth required:** ISO 31000 process, TARA framework, probability/impact matrix vs Monte Carlo vs VaR, Solvency II risk categories (underwriting/market/credit/operational/liquidity), ORSA (Board approval, PRA submission), risk appetite vs tolerance vs capacity, KRIs and risk registers
- **Bridge to core:** IF11, M67, 992, 993

#### H2: Frequently Asked Questions About CII Advanced Diploma in Insurance Assignment Help
> Each question must NOT repeat Main Content — must extend to new representative query variants.

1. **Do I need to re-sit M05, M92, and 530 if I already completed them for the CII Diploma?**
   → Answer direction: No — M05 Insurance Law and Practice (25 credits), M92 Insurance Business and Finance (25 credits), and 530 Economics and Business (30 credits) completed as part of the CII Diploma in Insurance are recognised as credit toward the Advanced Diploma requirements. Students who already hold these units at Diploma level progress directly to Advanced Diploma core units 820, 930, and 960. Students enrolling directly for the Advanced Diploma without prior Diploma completion must complete M05, M92, and 530 as part of their Advanced Diploma programme.

2. **What is the difference between a Level 6 optional unit and a Level 7 unit in the Advanced Diploma?**
   → Answer direction: Level 6 optional units carry 30 credits and are assessed through written examinations under timed conditions — the same format as the core units 820, 930, and 960. Level 7 optional units (991 Strategic Risk Management, 993 Advanced Reinsurance, 994 Advanced Financial Planning in Insurance) carry 50 credits each and are assessed through research-based written submissions rather than timed examinations. Level 7 units require independent research, referenced strategic analysis, and represent the highest qualification level within the CII framework.

3. **Can the service help with 991 Strategic Risk Management research submissions?**
   → Answer direction: Yes — the service provides support for 991 Strategic Risk Management research submissions, which require engagement with enterprise risk management frameworks (ISO 31000, COSO), ORSA requirements under Solvency II, risk governance at board level, and regulatory risk reporting. Research-based submissions at Level 7 are distinct from timed written exams and require a different preparation approach — the service can assist with structure, strategic framing, and analytical depth appropriate to Level 7 standard.

4. **Is the 820 Advanced Claims Management unit significantly harder than Diploma claims units?**
   → Answer direction: Yes — the distinction is not merely one of difficulty but of analytical type. Diploma claims content (M85 or M86) requires understanding of claims processes, coverage decisions, and settlement principles. The 820 Advanced Claims Management unit requires strategic analysis: why a claims reserving strategy affects the insurer's Solvency II capital position, how a systemic claims fraud pattern affects portfolio performance, and what board-level response is appropriate to a deteriorating claims combined ratio. Operational claims experience is necessary context for 820, but is not sufficient to pass it without strategic framing.

5. **How long does it take to complete the CII Advanced Diploma in Insurance from Diploma level?**
   → Answer direction: The time required depends on the number of optional units selected, how many core units are carried from Diploma level, individual study pace, and exam sitting availability. Students who completed M05, M92, and 530 at Diploma level need to pass three core units (820, 930, 960) plus sufficient optional units to meet the Advanced Diploma credit threshold. The CII's qualification framework and unit enrolment windows affect how quickly students can sit each unit. The service can support students across any number of units simultaneously or sequentially — contact the service with your specific qualification plan for an assessment of how support can be structured.

#### H2: Frequently Asked Questions About CII Diploma in Insurance Assignment Help
> Each question must NOT repeat Main Content — must extend to new representative query variants.

1. **How many optional units do I need to pass for the CII Diploma in Insurance?**
   → Answer direction: Students must complete all three core units (M05, M92, 530 = 80 credits combined) plus a sufficient number of optional units to reach the CII Diploma credit threshold. The exact number of optional units depends on the credit value of units selected (most are 20 credits; some specialist units are 25 credits). Contact CII directly or check your enrolment documentation for your specific credit target.

2. **Can I pass M05 Insurance Law without knowing detailed case law?**
   → Answer direction: The M05 written examination requires students to apply legal principles to case study scenarios. While a comprehensive knowledge of named individual cases is not mandatory for every question, understanding the legal principles they establish (utmost good faith, insurable interest, subrogation) and the key statutes (Marine Insurance Act 1906, Insurance Act 2015) is essential. Tutors experienced in M05 can guide students on which legal knowledge is most likely to be examined and how to structure written answers effectively.

3. **What is the difference between M05 and IF1 for insurance law content?**
   → Answer direction: IF1 (Insurance, Legal and Regulatory) covers foundational insurance law principles — contract formation, principles of insurance, regulatory framework — at RQF Level 3 using MCQ format. M05 (Insurance Law and Practice) covers the same principles at significantly greater depth (RQF Level 4), requires knowledge of named statutes and legal cases, and is assessed through a written examination requiring structured legal argument. M05 is not a repetition of IF1 — it is a substantial escalation in legal depth and assessment format.

4. **Is the CII Diploma in Insurance available to students who have not completed the Certificate?**
   → Answer direction: Yes — the CII Diploma in Insurance is not strictly contingent on holding the Certificate in Insurance. Students can enrol directly for Diploma units if they meet CII's enrolment requirements. However, Certificate-level knowledge (particularly IF1 for insurance law principles underpinning M05) is strongly recommended as preparation. Many students who struggle with M05 have not had foundational insurance law grounding from Certificate level.

5. **Does the service help with CII Diploma optional units in specialist insurance classes like professional indemnity or construction?**
   → Answer direction: Yes — the service covers specialist optional units including M93 (Medical Malpractice), M94 (Professional Indemnity), M95 (Construction Insurance), and M96 (Engineering Insurance). These specialist class units require sector-specific knowledge that goes beyond standard insurance product coverage. The service matches students to tutors with relevant specialist experience for these units.

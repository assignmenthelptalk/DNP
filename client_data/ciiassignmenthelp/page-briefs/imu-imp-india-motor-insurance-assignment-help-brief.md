#### H2: IRDAI and the Indian Motor Insurance Regulatory Framework
> **EAV to cover**: IRDAI establishment, powers, premium regulation, product approval
> **Word count**: 250–300 words

> **Specific content**:
> - **IRDAI (Insurance Regulatory and Development Authority of India)**: Established under the Insurance Regulatory and Development Authority Act 1999. Replaced the Controller of Insurance under the colonial-era Insurance Act 1938. IRDAI regulates all insurance business in India — general insurance, life insurance, and health insurance.
> - **IRDAI powers in motor insurance**: Sets mandatory terms for compulsory third party (TP) cover, approves motor product wordings, sets solvency margin requirements for motor insurers, and issues circulars mandating changes to motor insurance terms (e.g., long-term policies, CPA cover amount).
> - **Premium regulation**: Until 2007, both TP and OD rates were tariff-controlled (set by the Tariff Advisory Committee). In 2007, OD rates were detariffed — insurers can set their own OD premium rates based on underwriting judgement. TP rates remain regulated by IRDAI — insurers cannot compete on TP premium; rates are set annually by IRDAI.
> - **Product approval**: All motor insurance products (wordings, add-ons) must be filed with IRDAI before sale (Use & File or File & Use procedure). IRDAI reviews for consumer protection compliance.

#### H2: Motor Vehicles Act 1988 (India) — Compulsory Third Party Cover
> **EAV to cover**: Section 146 compulsory obligation, TP cover scope, Solatium Fund
> **Word count**: 250–300 words

> **Specific content**:
> - **Motor Vehicles Act 1988 s.146**: No person shall use a motor vehicle in a public place without a policy of insurance covering at minimum third party liability for: death or bodily injury to any third party, damage to third party property, and death or injury of passengers (if a public service vehicle).
> - **Third Party Insurance scope**: TP cover is unlimited for death or personal injury to third parties (no cap on liability for personal injury claims — Motor Vehicles Act). Property damage to third parties is covered up to ₹7.5 lakh (subject to IRDAI updates).
> - **Victim Compensation (Solatium Fund)**: Similar to the UK MIB, India's Solatium Fund (administered by the General Insurance Corporation) provides compensation to victims of hit-and-run accidents where the at-fault vehicle and driver cannot be traced. Fixed compensation of ₹50,000 for grievous hurt and ₹2 lakh for death (subject to IRDAI revision).
> - **Motor Claims Tribunal (MACT)**: Disputes arising from motor accident claims are heard by Motor Accident Claims Tribunals — not civil courts. The tribunal process and compensation principles (Pranay Sethi formula for multiplier-based compensation) are tested in IMU exam scenarios.

#### H2: Indian Motor Cover Types — TP Only vs Comprehensive
> **EAV to cover**: TP only scope, OD component, IDV, NCB, add-ons
> **Word count**: 300–350 words

> **Specific content**:
> - **Third Party Only cover**: Covers ONLY third party liability obligations — exactly as required by Motor Vehicles Act 1988. Does NOT cover own vehicle damage, fire, theft, or personal injury to the owner-driver. Mandatory minimum for all vehicles. TP premium is IRDAI-regulated.
> - **Comprehensive cover (Package policy)**: TP liability cover PLUS Own Damage (OD) section covering: accidental damage to the insured's own vehicle, fire, theft, natural calamities (flood, cyclone, earthquake), and personal accident cover for owner-driver. OD section premium is set by the insurer (not IRDAI-regulated).
> - **Insured Declared Value (IDV)**: The sum insured for the OD section of a comprehensive policy. Calculated as: manufacturer's listed selling price minus depreciation per IRDAI schedule based on vehicle age (0–6 months = 5% depreciation; 6–12 months = 15%; 1–2 years = 20%; 2–3 years = 30%; 3–4 years = 40%; 4–5 years = 50%). IDV is negotiable within a band above/below the IRDAI schedule figure.
> - **No Claim Bonus (NCB)**: India motor NCB scale: 20% after 1 claim-free year, 25% after 2 years, 35% after 3 years, 45% after 4 years, 50% after 5+ years. NCB applies to the OD premium only (not TP premium). NCB is transferable between insurers on renewal with NCB certificate.
> - **Add-on covers**: Zero depreciation (nil deduction for depreciation on parts at time of claim — most popular add-on), engine protect, consumables cover, roadside assistance, return to invoice (IDV = original invoice value in total loss), passenger cover.
> - **Compulsory PA (CPA) cover**: IRDAI mandates ₹15 lakh CPA cover for the owner-driver (mandatory since 2019). Available as a standalone annual policy or bundled within comprehensive cover.

#### H2: Long-Term Motor Policies — IRDAI 2018 Circular
> **EAV to cover**: Mandatory long-term TP, optional long-term OD, new vehicle applicability
> **Word count**: 150–200 words

> **Specific content**:
> - From 1 September 2018, IRDAI mandated that all new private cars must have a 5-year TP policy bundled at point of sale. New two-wheelers require a 5-year TP policy.
> - **New private cars — options**: (1) 5-year TP + 3-year OD bundle, (2) 5-year TP only (customer arranges annual OD separately). The 5-year TP cannot be cancelled or changed — it is a locked-in statutory cover period.
> - **OD flexibility**: The OD component can be from a different insurer than the TP insurer — and can be renewed annually independently of the 5-year TP.
> - **Rationale**: Reduce uninsured vehicle problem in India by locking in TP cover for the full product warranty period.

### FAQ SECTION

**Q: What is the difference between IMU and IMP?**
IMU focuses on the process of underwriting and handling motor claims in the Indian market. IMP focuses on the motor insurance products available in India — the structure of cover types, IDV, NCB, CPA, and add-ons.

**Q: Are IRDAI regulations tested in CII IMU/IMP exams?**
Yes — IMU and IMP exams test knowledge of the Indian regulatory framework including IRDAI product approval processes, TP premium regulation vs OD detariffication, and IRDAI circulars on long-term policies and CPA cover amounts.

**Q: What is IDV and how is it calculated?**
Insured Declared Value (IDV) is the sum insured for the Own Damage section of an Indian comprehensive motor policy. It is calculated as: manufacturer's listed price minus depreciation per IRDAI schedule based on vehicle age. For a 1–2 year old vehicle, the depreciation is 20%.

**Q: Does India have an equivalent of the UK MIB?**
Yes — India's Solatium Fund compensates victims of hit-and-run accidents where the at-fault vehicle is untraced. Compensation amounts are fixed (currently ₹50,000 for grievous hurt; ₹2 lakh for death) and subject to revision by IRDAI.

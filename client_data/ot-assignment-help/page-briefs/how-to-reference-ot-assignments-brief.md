##### H3: Citing Books and Book Chapters — Including OT Textbooks
> **Attribute + Value**:
> Book format: Author Surname, Initials. (Year) Title of Book. Edition (if not first). Place: Publisher.
> Example: Creek, J. and Lougher, L. (eds.) (2008) Occupational Therapy and Mental Health. 4th edn. Edinburgh: Churchill Livingstone.
> Chapter in edited book format: Author Surname, Initials. (Year) 'Chapter title', in Editor Surname, Initials. (ed.) Book Title. Place: Publisher, pp. pages.
> Example: Taylor, R.R. (2017) 'The model of human occupation', in Taylor, R.R. (ed.) Kielhofner's Model of Human Occupation. 5th edn. Philadelphia: Wolters Kluwer, pp. 3–10.
> **Writer note**: OT textbooks are commonly edited volumes — students must use the chapter-in-edited-book format, not the whole book format, when citing specific chapter authors. Failure to do this correctly is a common error.

##### H3: Referencing RCOT Publications in Harvard Style
> **Attribute + Value**:
> Format: Royal College of Occupational Therapists. (Year). Title of Document. RCOT.
> Example: Royal College of Occupational Therapists. (2021). Royal College of Occupational Therapists Professional Standards for Occupational Therapy Practice, Conduct and Ethics. RCOT.
> In-text first use: (Royal College of Occupational Therapists, 2021)
> In-text subsequent uses: (RCOT, 2021)
> Common error: "RCOT (2021)" without full name at first use is incorrect; citing the RCOT website URL instead of the specific document is incorrect
> **Writer note**: Note that RCOT publishes both free PDF documents and paid publications. Free PDF documents should be cited with the RCOT. publisher designation and include a URL if available. Paid publications follow standard book format with RCOT as publisher. Students must always cite the specific document title, not just "RCOT guidelines."

##### H3: Referencing HCPC Standards and NICE Guidelines
> **Attribute + Value**:
> HCPC format: Health and Care Professions Council. (Year). Title of Document. HCPC. URL if applicable.
> Example: Health and Care Professions Council. (2023). Standards of Proficiency — Occupational Therapists. HCPC. https://www.hcpc-uk.org/standards/standards-of-proficiency/occupational-therapists/
> NICE guideline format: National Institute for Health and Care Excellence. (Year). Title [NICE guideline NGxxx/CGxxx]. NICE. https://www.nice.org.uk/guidance/ngXXX
> Example (NICE): National Institute for Health and Care Excellence. (2018). Dementia: assessment, management and support for people living with dementia and their carers [NICE guideline NG97]. NICE. https://www.nice.org.uk/guidance/ng97
> In-text: (National Institute for Health and Care Excellence, 2018) — first use; (NICE, 2018) — subsequent use
> **Writer note**: NICE guideline numbers are essential — NG = National Guideline; CG = Clinical Guideline; QS = Quality Standard. Students frequently omit the guideline number. The URL is required for online-only documents.

#### H2: Frequently Asked Questions — Referencing in OT Assignments
> **Critical rule**: These questions must NOT repeat Main Content. They expand toward new representative query variants.

**Questions and Answer Directions**:

1. **What referencing style do most UK occupational therapy programmes use?**
   → Answer direction: Most UK OT programmes use Harvard referencing style; some programmes require APA 7th edition for research modules or dissertations; students should check their specific institution's assignment guidelines as there is no single universal standard across all UK OT programmes. 2–3 sentences.

2. **How do I reference a MOHO-based assessment like MOHOST or OPHI-II?**
   → Answer direction: MOHO-based assessments each have their own manual which must be cited as the primary source: MOHOST = Parkinson, Forsyth & Kielhofner (2006); OPHI-II = Kielhofner, Mallinson, Crawford et al. (2004); do not cite the MOHO model as the source for the assessment tool — cite the assessment manual itself. Provide format example. 3–4 sentences.

3. **Can I use a secondary reference in my OT assignment?**
   → Answer direction: Secondary referencing (citing a source you read about in another source) is acceptable only when the original is genuinely inaccessible — not available via university library, interlibrary loan, or open-access repositories. If the original is available (e.g., on PubMed, CINAHL, or Google Scholar), cite it directly. Format: Original Author (Year) cited in Secondary Author (Year). 3–4 sentences.

4. **Do I need to include page numbers in Harvard in-text citations for OT assignments?**
   → Answer direction: Page numbers are required for direct quotes in Harvard (Author, Year: page); for paraphrases, page numbers are recommended but not always required — however, many UK OT programme marking criteria require or recommend page numbers for all in-text citations, so include them wherever you can identify the specific page. 3 sentences.

5. **How do I cite the NICE dementia guidelines or NICE stroke guidelines in an OT assignment?**
   → Answer direction: NICE guidelines follow the format: National Institute for Health and Care Excellence. (Year). Title [NICE guideline NGxxx or CGxxx]. NICE. URL. Example for stroke: National Institute for Health and Care Excellence. (2019). Stroke rehabilitation in adults [NICE guideline NG162]. NICE. https://www.nice.org.uk/guidance/ng162. In-text first use: (National Institute for Health and Care Excellence, 2019); subsequent use: (NICE, 2019). 4 sentences.

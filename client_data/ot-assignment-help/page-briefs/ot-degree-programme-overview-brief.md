## EAV Depth Map
**Primary Entity**: Occupational Therapy Degree Programme

**Main Attributes**:

**UK BSc OT Programme**:
- Duration: 3 years full-time
- Level structure: Year 1 = Level 4; Year 2 = Level 5; Year 3 = Level 6
- RCOT accreditation: required for HCPC registration eligibility
- Minimum practice placement hours: 1,000 hours total (RCOT standard)
- Year 1 Level 4 modules (typical): Foundations of OT, Occupational Science, Anatomy and Physiology for OT, Introduction to Clinical Assessment, Research Skills and Academic Writing
- Year 1 Level 4 assessments: essays 1,500–2,000 words; OSCEs; practical assessments; group presentations; Placement 1 approx 35–40 days
- Year 2 Level 5 modules (typical): OT Models and Theory, Clinical Skills, Mental Health OT, Physical Rehabilitation OT, Paediatric OT, Research Methods
- Year 2 Level 5 assessments: case studies 2,500–3,000 words; literature reviews 2,500 words; research proposals 2,000 words; Placement 2 approx 40 days
- Year 3 Level 6 modules (typical): Specialist OT Practice, Leadership and Service Development, Evidence-Based Practice, Dissertation
- Year 3 Level 6 assessments: dissertation 8,000–12,000 words; practice placement portfolio; advanced case study or clinical reasoning assignment; Placements 3 and 4 approx 75–85 days combined

**UK MSc Pre-Registration OT**:
- Duration: 2 years full-time
- Level: all Level 7 throughout
- Entry requirements: undergraduate degree (any discipline), GPA/grade equivalent, healthcare experience (varies by university)
- Modules: Advanced Clinical Reasoning, Professional Practice, OT Models and Theory, Research Design, Service Improvement, Specialist Elective
- Assessments: critical appraisal essays 3,000–4,000 words; research proposals 3,500 words; dissertation 15,000–20,000 words; advanced case studies; practice placement portfolio
- Practice hours: minimum 1,000 hours (same RCOT requirement as BSc)
- Outcome: HCPC registration eligibility (same as BSc)

**UK OT Degree Apprenticeship**:
- Duration: 4 years (part-time alongside employment)
- Level: Level 6
- Funded via Apprenticeship Levy (employer-funded; no tuition fees for apprentice)
- End-point assessment required for completion
- Growing availability in NHS trusts, social care organisations
- Outcome: BSc OT and HCPC registration eligibility

**US Entry-Level OTD (Occupational Therapy Doctorate)**:
- Duration: 3 years full-time (some accelerated 2.5 years)
- Accreditation: ACOTE (Accreditation Council for Occupational Therapy Education)
- Licensing exam: NBCOT (National Board for Certification in OT) — pass required to practise as OTR/L
- Framework: OTPF-4 (Occupational Therapy Practice Framework) used throughout curriculum
- Fieldwork Level I: observation and brief direct practice during didactic coursework (varies — typically 40–80 hours over Yr 1-2)
- Fieldwork Level II: 24 weeks minimum, split across 2 placements of 12 weeks each (or equivalent); must be completed after didactic coursework
- Doctoral capstone: 14 weeks in a mentored doctoral experience (experiential component unique to OTD vs MSOT)
- Assessment types: case studies, evidence-based practice assignments, SOAP note writing, capstone project, comprehensive examinations
- MSOT (Master of Science in OT): being phased out in favour of OTD as entry-level degree; 2024 deadline for transition set by AOTA

**Australian Master of Occupational Therapy**:
- Duration: 2 years full-time (MSc entry-level)
- Accreditation: Occupational Therapy Australia (OTA) and OTBA (OT Board of Australia)
- Minimum practice hours: 1,000
- Framework: CMOP-E frequently used alongside OTPF and MOHO
- Registration: AHPRA (Australian Health Practitioner Regulation Agency) upon successful completion
- Assessment types: case studies, reflective journals, research projects, practice placement competency

**Canadian Master of Occupational Therapy (MOT)**:
- Duration: 2 years full-time
- Accreditation: CAOT (Canadian Association of OTs) accreditation
- Framework: CMOP-E dominant (developed by CAOT); CPPF (Canadian Practice Process Framework) used in practice process assignments
- COPM as primary assessment tool (developed in Canada)
- Registration: provincial regulatory bodies (e.g., COTO in Ontario) upon completion
- Practice hours: approx 1,000 (varies by province)

**Values per Attribute**:

- HCPC registration → BSc/MSc OT → protected title "Occupational Therapist" — cannot practise without it; annual renewal fee; CPD audit every 2 years

#### H2: FAQ — OT Degree Programme Questions
> **Critical rule**: Questions must NOT repeat Main Content. Expand toward representative query variants.

**Questions and Answer Directions**:
1. How many placements does an OT degree have in the UK? → Answer: UK BSc OT programmes typically include 4 placements across 3 years totalling a minimum of 1,000 hours (RCOT requirement). Placement 1 (Year 1): 35-40 days; Placement 2 (Year 2): approx 40 days; Placements 3 and 4 (Year 3): approx 75-85 days combined. MSc pre-registration programmes compress the same minimum 1,000 hours into 2 years. Specific placement numbers and lengths vary by university — check your programme handbook.
2. Can I do an OT degree while working? → Answer: The OT degree apprenticeship (Level 6, 4 years) is the primary route for working while studying. It is employer-funded via the Apprenticeship Levy, so there are no tuition fees for the apprentice. Traditional BSc and MSc programmes are typically full-time due to placement requirements, though some part-time BSc options exist at specific universities.
3. What is the difference between BSc and MSc OT in terms of assignments? → Answer: BSc Level 6 dissertations are typically 8,000–12,000 words and may be literature-based or small-scale empirical studies. MSc Level 7 dissertations are typically 15,000–20,000 words and are expected to demonstrate greater theoretical depth, critical engagement with methodology, and contribution to OT knowledge. MSc critical appraisal and case study assignments are also marked to a higher analytical standard than Level 6 equivalents.
4. Do US OTD students study the same OT models as UK students? → Answer: US OTD programmes are built around OTPF-4 (AOTA's Occupational Therapy Practice Framework, 4th edition) as the primary framework. UK programmes use MOHO and CMOP-E more prominently alongside RCOT and HCPC professional standards. Kawa Model and OTIPM appear in both. If you are an international student studying a US-accredited programme and need help with OTPF-based assignments, our OTPF assignment help covers the US framework specifically.
5. What does RCOT accreditation mean for my OT degree? → Answer: RCOT (Royal College of Occupational Therapists) accreditation confirms that your programme meets the standards required for graduates to apply for HCPC registration — the legal requirement to practise as an occupational therapist in the UK. Without HCPC registration, you cannot use the protected title "Occupational Therapist." RCOT-accredited programmes must include minimum 1,000 practice placement hours, evidence-based curriculum content, and alignment with HCPC Standards of Proficiency.

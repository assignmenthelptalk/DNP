#### H2: FAQ — RCOT Professional Standards Questions from OT Students
> **Critical rule**: Questions must NOT repeat Main Content. They extend toward representative query variants.

**Questions and Answer Directions**:
1. **What is the difference between RCOT and HCPC for occupational therapists?** → Answer direction: RCOT is the voluntary professional body (professional development, advocacy, ethical guidance, specialist sections); HCPC is the mandatory statutory regulator (registration, fitness to practise, public protection). A UK OT must be HCPC registered to practise; RCOT membership is voluntary but professionally beneficial.
2. **Is RCOT student membership free?** → Answer direction: Yes — student membership is free for students enrolled in HCPC-approved OT programmes in the UK. It provides BJOT access, specialist section resources, student network events, and supports professional identity development during the degree.
3. **How many RCOT Professional Standards are there and when were they last updated?** → Answer direction: 6 standards in the 2021 edition. Briefly name them. Note that the 2021 edition supersedes previous RCOT professional standards documents. Students should always cite the 2021 edition.
4. **Which RCOT ethical principle is most relevant to consent in OT practice?** → Answer direction: Autonomy — the principle that respects service users' rights to make informed decisions about their own care. However, the non-maleficence principle is also relevant when the OT must balance the client's autonomous decision against potential harm. Both principles should be discussed when consent and capacity are the assignment focus.
5. **Can I use RCOT specialist section publications as academic sources in OT assignments?** → Answer direction: Yes — RCOT specialist section publications are grey literature and can be cited as professional guidance documents. They should be referenced fully (author/RCOT, year, title, publisher). They supplement peer-reviewed journal evidence — they do not replace it. Check with your programme whether grey literature counts toward your required number of academic sources.

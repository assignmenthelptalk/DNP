#### H2: FAQ — OT Career Pathways Questions from Students
> **Critical rule**: New representative query variants only.

**Questions and Answer Directions**:
1. **What is the difference between Band 5 and Band 6 in NHS OT?** → Answer direction: Band 5 = newly qualified OT, typically first 1–2 years; supported practice with preceptorship; developing clinical skills. Band 6 = experienced/specialist OT; typically with 2+ years of post-qualification experience; greater clinical autonomy; often developing or consolidating a specialist area. Promotion from Band 5 to Band 6 typically requires demonstrating competency for specialist or advanced practice.
2. **What are the most common OT specialisms in the UK NHS?** → Answer direction: The largest OT employment sectors in the UK NHS are: older adults/community OT, neurological rehabilitation (stroke, TBI, MS), mental health (community and inpatient), paediatrics, and physical rehabilitation (orthopaedics, rheumatology). Hand therapy and vocational rehabilitation are smaller but well-established specialisms. Research/academic OT is a growing route for those with postgraduate qualifications.
3. **What counts as CPD for HCPC re-registration?** → Answer direction: HCPC accepts a wide range of CPD activities, provided they meet the 4 CPD standards (continuous record, wide range of learning, contributes to quality of practice, benefits service users). Activities can include: formal courses and conferences, reflective practice journals, peer supervision, journal reading, service evaluation, research participation, online learning, and peer teaching. There is no prescribed minimum number of hours — but RCOT recommends 450 hours over 2 years.
4. **What is preceptorship for newly qualified OTs?** → Answer direction: Preceptorship is a structured period of supervised support for NQPs in their first role post-qualification. The NHS England preceptorship framework and RCOT both recommend preceptorship for all newly qualified OTs. Typically 6–12 months; includes regular supervision, competency review, and CPD support. Not all trusts offer formal preceptorship — students should ask about NQP support when applying for their first OT post.
5. **How do I join RCOT specialist interest groups?** → Answer direction: RCOT specialist sections (formerly SIGs — specialist interest groups) are accessed through RCOT membership (free for students at accredited UK programmes). As an RCOT member, students can join specialist sections relevant to their practice interests. Specialist sections provide CPD resources, practice guidance, newsletters, events, and a professional network — these are valuable for placement specialisms as well as career development.

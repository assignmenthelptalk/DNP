#### H2: FAQ — HCPC Standards of Proficiency Questions from OT Students
> **Critical rule**: These questions must NOT repeat Main Content. They extend toward new representative query variants for passage-level indexing.

**Questions and Answer Directions**:
1. **How many HCPC Standards of Proficiency are there for occupational therapists?** → Answer direction: 15 standards in the current 2023 revised edition. Briefly note the revision from 14 standards (2013 edition) to 15 in 2023. One sentence summary of the purpose of the SoP.
2. **What is the difference between HCPC fitness to practise and the Standards of Proficiency?** → Answer direction: SoP describe what a competent registrant must know and do; fitness to practise is a process that investigates whether a registrant has fallen below the standards. A student would reference SoP in an assignment to describe professional obligations; fitness to practise is referenced when discussing professional accountability or misconduct scenarios.
3. **Can I practise as an occupational therapist without HCPC registration?** → Answer direction: No. Use of the protected title "occupational therapist" without HCPC registration is a criminal offence in the UK. Students on placement are covered by their university indemnity and their supervisor's registration until they qualify and register.
4. **Which HCPC standard covers consent in occupational therapy?** → Answer direction: Standard 2 (legal and ethical boundaries) covers consent — including the Mental Capacity Act 2005 and the requirement to obtain valid, informed consent before assessment and intervention. Brief note that capacity assessment is an OT skill covered by Standard 2.
5. **Are HCPC standards the same as RCOT standards?** → Answer direction: No — the distinction (regulatory minimum vs professional aspiration) summarised in one to two sentences. HCPC = statutory, minimum, public protection. RCOT = voluntary guidance, professional development, aspirational excellence. Both should be cited in relevant OT assignments but for different purposes.
